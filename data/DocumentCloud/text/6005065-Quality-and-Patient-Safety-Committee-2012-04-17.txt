Registered Nurse, John H. Stroger, Jr. Hospital of Cook County

Report from System Chief Medical Officer
Dr. Terry Mason, System Chief Medical Officer, provided an update on the following subjects.
Celebrating March 30th as National Doctors Day
In recognition of National Doctors Day, Dr. Mason stated that Dr. David Goldberg, of Stroger Hospital, was the
recipient of the Doctor of the Year award for the Cook County Health and Hospitals System. Dr. Mason added
that the System provided breakfast or lunch for the doctors System-wide on March 30th, to celebrate Doctors
Day.

III.

Report from System Chief Medical Officer (continued)
Meeting with Oak Forest Health Center Physicians
Dr. Mason stated that a meeting was held with physicians at Oak Forest Health Center regarding some patient
care issues. Those in attendance included Dr. Emeka Ezike, two doctors who represented some of the union
physicians, and Emilie Junge, who represented the SEIU Doctors Council. He stated that the meeting was
fruitful; they discussed issues around signage and some of the patient care matters. Dr. Ezike will be following
up on some of the issues, and Sylvia Edwards, the Executive Director of Oak Forest Health Center, is following
up on some of the other matters that were brought up.
Update on Get Yourself Tested (GYT) Initiative
Dr. Mason noted that the Committee received information on the GYT initiative at the March meeting; he
provided further information on the subject. He indicated that the total number of individuals tested last year as
part of the initiative was over 7,500; it is expected that this number will be surpassed with the 2012 GYT
initiative.

IV.

Report from System Interim Chief Nursing Officer
Tanda Russell, System Interim Chief Nursing Officer, stated that there were two consecutive nursing
orientations held over the last month; they are looking forward to bringing in more nurses in the next
orientation. She added that there are a total of fourteen nurses starting on April 23rd; these positions are
dedicated to the Medical-Surgical units, with the full intent of re-opening the beds that remain closed. As of
today, she indicated that twelve beds currently remain closed.

V.

Report from System Director of Quality and Patient Safety
Barbara Farrell, System Director of Quality and Patient Safety, noted that at the next Committee Meeting, she
will be presenting information that ties together the Quality and Patient Safety Dashboard with some of the
quality initiatives from all of the areas.
A. Quality report from the Ambulatory and Community Health Network of Cook County (Attachment
#1)
The following individuals from ACHN presented the quality report: Dr. Stephen Stabile, Associate Chief
Medical Officer; Irene Marks; Dr. Judith Neafsey, Director of Ambulatory Pediatrics; and Dr. Enrique
Martinez, Interim Chief Operating Officer and Chief Medical Officer.
Included in the presentation was information on the following subjects: Indicator Tracking; Cerner
Ambulatory-Prevention and Treatment Prompts; MERS-Patient Safety and Complaint Tracking; PPR,
Grants and Other Projects; Comparison of Phone Answering Rates; Building Community-Based Medical
Homes for ACHN; and data on ACHN Patient Flows.
