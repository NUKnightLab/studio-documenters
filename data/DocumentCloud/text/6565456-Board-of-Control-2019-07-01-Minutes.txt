BC2019-490
Department of Public Works, submitting an amendment to Contract No. CE1700151 with Quick
Employment, LLC for sourcing and staffing of temporary employees for the period 8/8/2017 – 12/31/2019
for additional funds in the amount not-to-exceed $19,152.00.
Funding Source: General Fund – Internal Service Fund
Michael Chambers, Department of Public Works, presented. Dale Miller asked if hiring practices are
contributing to the need for temporary staffing services and what portions of this amendment are related
to position vacancies and the Enterprise Resource Planning Project; and expressed his support for this
amendment due to the important work the department provides to the communities. Nan Baker asked
how much lead time is needed to prepare for staff that are retiring. Dennis Kennedy motioned to approve
the item; Dale Miller seconded. Item BC2019-490 was unanimously approved.

 BC2019-491
Department of Information Technology, submitting an amendment to Contract No. CE1600126 with
Mythics, Inc. for Oracle database software support and maintenance services for the period 6/1/2016 –
5/31/2019 to extend the time period to 5/31/2020, to expand the scope of services to add Oracle
Database Standard Edition One license, effective 6/1/2019 and for additional funds in the amount not-toexceed $327,236.85.
Funding Sources: General Fund; Domestic Relations Bureau of Support; DoIT Mainframe Operations and
Fiscal Real Estate Assessment, Tax Assessments
Jeanelle Greene and Dennis Sullivan, Department of Information Technology, presented. Nan Baker asked
if the contract is being extended due to the Enterprise Resource Planning Project (ERP) schedule; asked
for clarification on the end date and asked how that date and additional funding correlates with the
anticipated ERP roll out. Dennis Kennedy stated that the original proposed savings will not be realized
until current operating system costs can be eliminated and that the longer the ERP Project is delayed, the
longer it will take for savings to occur. Ms. Baker asked if funds are being tracked and that all costs,
including those caused by delays, will be identified when budgets are approved; and expressed the
importance that all project funds are being accounted for.
Mr. Kennedy stated that the current systems, such as FAMIS, must be in place for a period of time to
continue operations and that the County is not yet ready for services to be eliminated, due to delays with
ERP, as originally anticipated. Mr. Kennedy stated that the Harris Project has similar issues and additional
renewals are expected; and stated that the accounting of these expenses will be shared with the Board
members to get a better perspective of the current state.
Trevor McAleer asked if the Harris Project completion date has been determined. Dennis Kennedy
motioned to approve the item; Trevor McAleer seconded. Item BC2019-491 was unanimously approved.

amendment to Contract No. CE1900129 with Infor Public Sector, Inc. for the purchase of (88) Enterprise

amendment is extending time from 2018 or 2019 and asked for confirmation if this request is to accept
additional funds for this program. Mr. Miller also reflected upon the recent 50th anniversary of Stonewall.

McAleer asked how many youths are being served and if youths on a waiting list could be helped with
