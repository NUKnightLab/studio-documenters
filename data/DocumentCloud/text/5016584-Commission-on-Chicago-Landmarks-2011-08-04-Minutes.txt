MINUTES OF THE MEETING
COMMISSION ON CHICAGO LANDMARKS
AUGUST 4, 2011
The Commission on Chicago Landmarks held a regular meeting on August 4, 2011. The meeting
was held at City Hall, 121 N. LaSalle St., City Hall Room 201-A, Chicago, Illinois. The meeting
began at 12:50 p.m.
PRESENT:

Rafael Leon, Chairman
John Baird, Secretary
Anita Blanchard
James Houlihan
Tony Hu
Andrew Mooney, Commissioner of the Department of Housing and Economic
Development (arrived after agenda item #1)
Christopher Reed
Ernest Wong

ABSENT:

Mary Ann Smith

ALSO PRESENT:

Eleanor Gorski, Assistant Commissioner, Department of Housing and
Economic Development, Historic Preservation Division
Arthur Dolinsky, Department of Law, Real Estate Division
Members of the Public (The list of those in attendance is on file at the
Commission office.)

A tape recording of this meeting is on file at the Department of Housing and Economic Development,
Historic Preservation Division offices and is part of the permanent public record of the regular
meeting of the Commission on Chicago Landmarks.
Chairman Leon called the meeting to order. He announced that on July 28th, the City Council
approved the appointment of Dr. Anita Blanchard, James Houlihan, Tony Hu, and Mary Ann Smith
to the Commission. Chairman Leon welcomed the new Commission members and congratulated
them on their appointment.
1.

Approval of the Minutes of the July 7, 2011, Regular Meeting
Motioned by Wong, seconded by Mooney. Approved unanimously. (8-0)

2.

Final Landmark Recommendation to City Council
KENWOOD UNITED CHURCH OF CHRIST
4600-4608 S. Greenwood Avenue

WARD 4

Resolution to adopt the Final Landmark Recommendation to City Council that the
KENWOOD UNITED CHURCH OF CHRIST be designated as a Chicago Landmark.
Motioned by Baird, seconded by Reed. Approved unanimously. (8-0)

 3.

Progress Report on Proposed Designations - Announcements
NEIGHBORHOOD BANK BUILDING
(Former) Mid-City Trust and Savings Bank Building,
2 S. Halsted St.

WARD 27

ANNOUNCED: The tolling agreement entered into with the property owner has been
amended; the tolling period will expire on November 1, 2011. He added that staff
continues to work with the property owners to reach consent. If consent is not reached,
however, a public hearing will be scheduled.

RIVIERA MOTOR SALES COMPANY BUILDING
5948-60 N. Broadway

WARD 48

ANNOUNCED: The tolling agreement entered into with the property owner expires on
August 5, 2011. He added that staff continues to work with the property owners to reach

Permit Review Committee Report

Dijana Cuvalo presented the report from the Permit Review Committee meeting of July 7,
2011 (see attached).

5.

Other Business

Dijana Cuvalo presented the reports of the staff to the Commission (see attached).
B. Announcements
1. Permit Review Committee and Program Committee Membership
ANNOUNCED:
Permit Review Committee: Ernie Wong, Chair; John Baird; Jim Houlihan; Tony Hu;
and Mary Ann Smith.
Program Committee: Christopher Reed, Chair; John Baird; and Anita Blanchard.

 2. 2011 Preservation Excellence Awards
ANNOUNCED:
Date: September 1, 2011
Time: Ceremony to begin at 10:00a.m. (check-in at 9:15 a.m.)

4. Other Business
Chairman Leon acknowledged receipt of a letter dated August 4, 2011, from Paddy
Lauber of Save Our Boulevards, and two requests from members of the public to
comment on certain matters not contained on the Commission’s meeting agenda.
Chairman Leon announced that he would defer taking comments on these matters. He
added that the Commission staff will notify those requesting to comment should these
matters come before the Commission in the future.

6.

Adjournment
There being no further business, the meeting was adjourned at 1:15 p.m.
