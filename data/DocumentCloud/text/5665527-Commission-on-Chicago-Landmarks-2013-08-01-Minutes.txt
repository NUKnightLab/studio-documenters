MOTOR ROW DISTRICT
2207-2237 S. Michigan Ave.

WARD 3

Proposed incorporation of four historic buildings into a new-construction hotel.

The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:
1.

333 N. Michigan
(333 North Michigan Avenue Building – 42nd Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation of a 35-story office building
to include new storefronts, replacement of the Michigan Avenue entry,
masonry repair and cleaning, recladding of the existing rooftop addition,
and other alterations.

Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. Masonry repair and replacement details shall be included in the permit
plans. The masonry facades shall be cleaned using the gentlest means
possible. Cleaning specifications, including the Material Safety Data
Sheets, shall be submitted for Historic Preservation staff review and
approval prior to order and cleaning;
2. Enlarged storefront and Michigan Avenue entry details shall be
included in the permit plans. Storefront and Michigan Avenue entry
shop drawings shall be reviewed and approved by Historic
Preservation staff prior to order and installation;
3. Samples of the storefront finishes and any replacement masonry and
mortar shall be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff
prior to order;
4. The new louvers proposed on the south and east secondary elevations
are approved. Louver and decorative grille details shall be included in
the permit plans; and,

 5. The proposed design, materials, locations, and installation details of
any new façade lighting and signage shall be consistent with the
Commission’s guidelines, standards, and criteria and shall be reviewed
and approved by Historic Preservation staff prior to order and
installation.

2.

407 S. Dearborn
(Old Colony Building – 4th Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation to convert 17-story office
building for multi-unit residential use including two new 1-story rooftop
additions and a rooftop deck.

Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. Enlarged window and storefront details shall be included in the permit
plans;
2. Samples of the storefront finishes and any replacement masonry and
mortar shall be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff
prior to order and installation;
3. The synthetic stucco cladding material for the rooftop additions shall
match the color and finish of the existing penthouse. Material and
color samples to be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation
staff prior to order and installation;
4. The new cooling tower and any other mechanical equipment visible
from the public way shall be sized to be as small as reasonably
possible and shall be painted a neutral color in a non-reflective finish.
Color samples shall be reviewed and approved by Historic
Preservation staff prior to order and installation; and,
5. The proposed design, materials, locations, and installation details of
any new façade lighting and signage shall be reviewed and approved
by Historic Preservation staff when available.

4.

4753 N. Dover
(Dover Street District – 46th Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed new 2 ½-story masonry residential building and rear attached
garage on an existing vacant lot.

 Action:

Approved 2-1 (with Commissioner Dziekiewicz against and
Commissioner Hu recusing) with the following conditions:
1. The modular-sized face brick Hanson “Mulberry” for the front façade
and the utility-sized brick Acme “Sequoia Ridge” for the sides and
rear façade are approved as proposed. The cast stone and limestone
sample are approved as submitted;
2. Window and door details shall be included with the permit plans; and,
3. The project as proposed requires a zoning variation and/or
administrative adjustment review for the proposed side yard setbacks.
The Commission takes no position regarding any requested
variation/adjustment relative to the zoning code requirements. The
proposed overall width of the building may be reduced by up to 1’-6”
should the applicant wish to pursue as-of-right construction.
At the Committee meeting, the applicant expressed a willingness to
consider minor modifications of the design of the project to address some
of the public comments.

5.

2134 W. Haddon
(Ukrainian Village District – 2nd Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed rehabilitation of a single family residence including removal of
two existing dormers and installation of new gable dormers with shed
dormer extensions on the east and west roofs.

Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. Dormer siding shall be smooth-finish fiber cement siding to match the
size, type and profile of historic wood siding typically found in the
district. Details of the proposed siding shall be submitted as part of
the permit application; and,
2. New windows on the front façade shall be wood or clad-wood
windows, and shall retain or replicate any existing brick molds.
Details of the proposed windows and brick mold shall be submitted as
part of the permit application.

6.

6328 N. Louise
(Old Edgebrook District – 39th Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed construction of a new 1-story detached garage.

Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

 1. The proposed vinyl siding for the new garage is not a compatible
material with the district, the siding shall be wood or smooth-faced
fiber-cement siding with a 4” lap;
2. The proposed cultured stone product at the base of the new garage is
not a compatible material with the historic property and district. The
stone cladding base shall either be eliminated with the siding
extending down to the foundation or the stone cladding base shall
modified to be consistent with the main house’s stone base in overall
height, unit sizes, color, profile, texture, and finish;
3. The new garage windows shall be modified to 1/1 double-hung wood
or clad-wood windows. Window details and garage and man door
product cut sheets shall be submitted with the permit application; and,
4. Should the project require a zoning variation and/or administrative
adjustment, the Commission takes no position regarding any
requested.
