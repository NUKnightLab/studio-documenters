MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - RIVERBOAT
OPEN SESSION
November 20, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, November 20, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Riverboat of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois at 10:21 A.M. Present were the following: Chairman Aaron Jaffe, Members,
Michael Holewinski and Lee Gould.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES – Open Session
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of October 27, 2014. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S COMMENTS –
Administrator Ostrowski stated that Harrah’s Metropolis’ new facility has moved to a land based
like structure from a paddle boat. The Administrator also stated that the Grand Victoria Casino’s
business diversity expo is being held today and the Child Support Intercept Program is in full
force.
PUBLIC COMMENTARY Cindi Kowalski addressed the Board.

Midwest Gaming & Entertainment, LLC d/b/a Rivers Casino – Ownership Transfer
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that pursuant to Section
235(a) of the Board’s Adopted Rules to approve the following transfers of interests,
consistent with information provided to the Board and pursuant to terms approved
by the Administrator:

 1. From the 2002 AGB Family Dynasty Trust to the 2002 AGB Family
Dynasty Trust Non-Exempt and the 2013 AGB Family Dynasty Trust
Exempt #2;
2. From the 2002 LNB Family Dynasty Trust to the 2002 LNB Family
Dynasty Trust Non-Exempt and the 2013 LNB Family Dynasty Trust
Exempt #2; and
3. From the Meredith A. Bluhm-Wolf 2006 Family GST Trust to the
Meredith A. Bluhm-Wolf Family Non-GST Trust and the 2013 Meredith
A. Bluhm-Wolf Family GST Trust #2.
Member Holewinski further moved to approve and designate the following
individuals and trusts as Key Persons of Midwest Gaming & Entertainment,
LLC:
1. 2013 AGB Family Dynasty Trust Exempt #2, in place of the 2002 AGB
Family Dynasty Trust;
2. 2013 LNB Family Dynasty Trust Exempt #2, in place of the 2002 LNB
Family Dynasty Trust;
3. 2013 Meredith A. Bluhm-Wolf Family GST Trust #2, in place of the
Meredith A. Bluhm-Wolf 2006 Family GST Trust.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.
•

Hollywood Casino Aurora, Inc. – John A. Burbidge – Level One
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved to approve John A. Burbidge
as a Level One Occupational Licensee at Hollywood Casino Aurora, Inc. d/b/a
Hollywood Casino Aurora in his position as Security Manager.
Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

 Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion

DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS:
• Yvette Vega & Paul Lysy:
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved to issue Disciplinary
Complaints to revoke the occupational licenses of Yvette Vega and Paul Lysy
due to their convictions of statutorily ineligible offenses, said actions to take
effect 21 days from the dates of service of the Complaint unless the licensees file
an Answer within that time period.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.
•

Joel Konrardy:
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved to issue a Disciplinary
Complaint to revoke the occupational license of Joel Konrardy due to his
involvement in installing unapproved surveillance cameras at Jumer’s Casino,
said action to take effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaint unless
the licensee files an Answer within that time period.
Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Jo Ellen Wilcoxen:
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved to issue a Disciplinary
Complaint to revoke the occupational license of Jo Ellen Wilcoxen due to her
admitted conduct of exchanging casino comps for drugs, said action to take
effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaint unless the licensee files
an Answer within that time period.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.

•

Jeffrey Clanin:
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved to issue a Disciplinary
Complaint to revoke the occupational license of Jeffrey Clanin due to his
repeated arrests and guilty pleas to Retail Theft, said action to take effect 21
days from the date of service of the Complaint unless the licensee files an Answer
within that time period.
Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
