MINUTES OF SPECIAL MEETING
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
JUNE 23, 1993
EAST SAINT LOUIS, ILLINOIS

A Special Meeting of the Illinois Gaming Board was held on June 23, 1993
in the pavilion of Casino Queen, Inc., 200 Front Street, East Saint Louis,
Illinois. The meeting was called by Chairman William J. Kunkle, Jr., and notice
was timely given to each Board Member and to the general public in conformity
with the Illinois Open Meetings Act, Chapter 102, Paragraph 42.02, Illinois
Revised Statutes.
The following Board Members were present: William J. Kunkle, Jr.,
Chairman; and Board Members William J. Chamblin, Gayl S. Pyatt and Robert F.
Vickrey. Member J. Thomas Johnson was absent.
Also in attendance were the Honorable Gordon Bush, Mayor of East St.
Louis, Illinois; Administrator Morton E. Friedman; Deputy Administrators Joseph
McQuaid, Marcy L. Wolf and J. Thomas Hutchison; members of the media and the
general public.
Chairman Kunkle called the meeting to order at 11:08 A.M.
The first order of business concerned applicant Casino Queen, Inc.
Chairman recognized the Administrator.

The

Mr. Friedman requested Board authorization to conduct a Final Practice
Gaming Excursion. Ms. Wolf, Mr. McQuaid and Mr. Hutchison reviewed the
statutory and regulatory requirements met by the applicant and noted that the
internal controls of the applicant had been reviewed and approved by staff and
the outside auditor, the gaming devices and surveillance systems had been tested
and had met expectations satisfactorily, that insurance requirements were
adequate, a performance bond was on file and the tax remittance system had been
tested. It was noted that the affirmative action efforts of the applicant had
resulted in employment of twice the number percentage of women and minorities
than was available in the local labor market. It was also noted that the vessel
had been certificated by the United States Coast Guard and that the Department
of Rehabilitation Services had inspected the vessel for accessibility by
handicapped individuals.
Mr. Vickrey moved that the Board authorize the Administrator to conduct a
Final Practice Gaming Excursion with respect to the application received from
Casino Queen, Inc., and that upon successful completion that the Administrator
was authorized to issue a Temporary Operating Permit. Ms. Pyatt seconded the
motion.

 Mr. Chamblin moved that pursuant to Illinois Revised Statutes 102,
Paragraph 42 (g), (h) and (k) that the Board retire to Closed Session to discuss

Without objection, the Board retired to Closed Session at 11:16 A.M.
The Illinois Gaming Board resumed its Open Session at 1:36 P.M. with
Chairman Kunkle and Members Chamblin, Pyatt and Vickrey in attendance.

become an investor in Casino Queen, Inc. be approved. Mr. Vickrey seconded the
