C. Report on City Departmental Reorganization: Department of Zoning and Land Use
Development and Department of Community Development
Brian Goeken reported that, as part of the reorganization of the Department of Planning
and Development and three other City departments (Housing, Zoning and Mayor’s Office
of Workforce Development) under the 2009 Budget adopted by City Council in
November, two new departments have been created: Zoning and Land Use Planning
(DZLUP) and Community Development (DCD). The Landmarks Division will continue
to staff the Commission on Chicago Landmarks and will be part of the new DZLUP.
The City Council approved an ordinance establishing these two new departments as well
as updating the references found throughout the municipal code pertaining to the four
superceded departments. As part of these changes, the Chicago Landmarks Ordinance
was amended to expand the landmarks commission from nine to ten members to reflect
the new departmental organizational structure, with the ninth member being the DZLUP
Commissioner and the tenth member being the DCD Commissioner. These changes were
effective January 1, 2009.
D. Amendment to Rules and Regulations of the Commission on Chicago Landmarks
Brian Goeken discussed the technical revisions contained in the proposed amendment to
the Rules & Regulations which reflect the above-discussed changes to the landmarks
ordinance and the membership of the landmarks commission.
Motioned by Wo ng, seconded by Reed. Approved unanimously. (8-0)
The amendment to the Rules and Regulations, will be effective immediately upon
publication.
