The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:
1. 1961 W. Schiller
1st Ward
Wicker Park District
Proposed new construction of a 1-story masonry garage with alley access on a corner
property.
Action:

Approved unanimously as submitted.

2. 943 N. Hoyne
1st Ward
Ukrainian Village District Extension
Proposed new construction of a 3-story masonry residential building with a detached
rear garage with roof deck.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. Window and door details shall be submitted with the permit plans for review
and approval; and,
2. The applicant shall provide face brick, mortar and limestone samples for
Historic Preservation staff review and approval prior to permit submittal. The
brick and stone colors and textures shall match typical colors and textures
found within the district, and mortar shall have a narrow profile and match the
color of the face brick.

 3. 333 N. Michigan
42nd Ward
333 North Michigan Building
Proposed installation of LED-lit graphic window surrounds for 6 windows on the 2ndfloor of the new Blackhawks store.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. Given that the affected windows are on the 2nd-floor and only 6 out of a total
of 45 retail display windows at the base of the building would have this
proposed treatment, the LED window surrounds and graphic display
installations, are approved as proposed;
2. The graphics of these lit display window surrounds may be black-and-white
and/or in color as proposed. And as proposed, any merchandising within
these LED-lit display windows shall be minimal to reduce the sense of visual
clutter at the base of the historic building; and,
3. Proposed back-lit graphics and permanent retail window installations are
reviewed on a case-by-case basis, and the Commission’s approval of this
treatment for this project should not be considered indicative of how it may
consider other similar proposed treatments in the future.
4. 835 W. Newport
44th Ward
Newport Avenue District
Proposed replacement of deteriorated sandstone façade with new limestone cladding,
new front stair, new cornice, new rear addition and a 4th floor addition.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. As proposed, the deteriorated sandstone cladding may be replaced with new
limestone cladding to match the historic masonry sizes, coursing, joint size
and profile, rustication, details and as closely as possible the masonry color.
If necessary, the limestone may be stained. Replacement limestone samples
and sample of any required staining shall be reviewed and approved for color
and texture by Historic Preservation staff prior to order and installation;
2. New mortar shall match the color of the new masonry and a mortar sample
shall be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff prior to work;
3. As proposed, the existing sandstone façade and returns shall be fully
documented and enlarged elevation drawings of the front façade and returns
that show the masonry pattern, dimensions of the stone units, and details shall
be included in the permit documents;
4. As proposed, a new metal cornice shall be installed to match the size and
location of the missing cornice based on any physical evidence found on the
building. Dimensioned cornice details shall be submitted for Historic
Preservation staff review and approval with the permit plans;
5. Details of the proposed front stair and railing shall be submitted for Historic
Preservation staff review and approval with the permit plans;

 6. Window details shall be submitted with the permit plans;
7. The rooftop addition is approved as proposed. Brick and metal cladding
samples shall be submitted to Historic Preservation staff for review and
approval with the permit application, and,
8. The project as proposed would require a variation and/or adjustment, and the
Commission takes no position regarding any requested variance/adjustment
relative to the zoning code requirements.
