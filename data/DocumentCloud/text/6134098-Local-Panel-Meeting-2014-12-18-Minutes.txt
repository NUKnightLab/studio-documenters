STATE OF ILLINOIS
ILLINOIS LABOR RELATIONS BOARD
LOCAL PANEL
Minutes of the Local Panel Meeting
Held on December 16, 2014
I.

OPENING OF MEETING 10:00 A.M.—160 North LaSalle Street, Suite S-401, Chicago, IL

II.

PRESENT

Robert M. Gierut, Chairman; Charles E. Anderson, Member of the Board; Richard Lewis, Member of the Board; Jerald S.
Post, General Counsel; Melissa Mlynski, Executive Director; Sarah Kerley, Deputy General Counsel; Anna HamburgGal, Associate General Counsel; and staff members.
III.

APPROVAL OF MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING HELD November 18, 2014

Chairman Gierut called for approval of the minutes of the November 18, 2014 meeting. Member Anderson moved to
approve the minutes. Member Lewis seconded. The Board passed the motion unanimously, by a voice vote.
IV.

ADMINISTRATIVE MATTERS

Chairman Gierut asked Executive Director Mlynski to present the Local Panel administrative actions report. She
presented the report and summarized its highlights. The Board examined the report and discussed it.
V.

BOARD ACTIONS

1.
Case No. L-CB-09-066
Darryl Spratt/ Amalgamated Transit Union, Local 241
Chairman Gierut recused himself from this case and asked General Counsel Post to call the case and present the issues of
the case to the Board. General Counsel Post stated that on June 3, 2014, Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) Martin Kehoe
issued a Recommended Decision and Order (RDO), recommending that the Board dismiss a complaint filed against the
Respondent. Charging Party filed exceptions to the RDO and Respondent filed a response to those exceptions. After his
presentation, General Counsel Post recommended that the Board accept the ALJ’s RDO. After discussion, Member
Anderson moved to accept the RDO. Member Lewis seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a
voice vote.
2.
Case No. L-CA-13-027
Illinois Brotherhood of Teamsters, Local 700/ Forest Preserve District of Cook County
General Counsel Post called the case, noted that no party filed exceptions to ALJ Sanceda’s RDO and asked whether the
Board wished to review the decision on its own motion. The Board declined to do so.
VI.

EXECUTIVE SESSION

After a short discussion regarding whether there was a need for executive session, the Board concluded it was
unnecessary.
VII.

ADJOURNMENT

Chairman Gierut called for a motion to adjourn. Member Anderson made a motion to adjourn the meeting, and the Board
passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
Adopted and approved by the Chairman and Members of the Local Panel of the Illinois Labor Relations Board, in
Chicago, Illinois, this 13th day of January, 2015.
