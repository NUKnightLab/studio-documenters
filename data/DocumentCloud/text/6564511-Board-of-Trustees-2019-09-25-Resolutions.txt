RESOLUTION 19314

exceed $274,606,290 for a total amended agreement amount not to exceed $479,372,080 to be paid out

authorized to negotiate and execute agreements and other documents consistent with this resolution.

Economic Terms
•
Wages: 2019 = special market adjustment raising Patrol Officer start rate to $21.00
($23.00 for those who maintain a Peace Officer Certification)
2020 = 2.5% increase
2021 = 2% increase

Increased hourly premium for attaining/maintaining Peace Officer Certification from
$1.00 to $2.00, to incentivize achievement of the certification

New, streamlined wage step chart with elimination of Year 4 wage step, enabling Patrol
Officers to reach the top rate faster, to enhance recruitment and retention

External lateral hires may be hired at rates higher than the start rate, to enhance
recruitment

Other Core Economic Terms:
1. MetroHealthy wellness incentive
: During the three-year agreement, MetroHealth
agrees to maintain a wellness incentive program. For the 2020 incentive (which will be
based on wellness activities completed in 2019), incentive amounts will be available at
$200/$600/$800 levels for employee activities, with additional $100/$200/$300 incentive
levels available for wellness activities of an employee’s spouse/child(ren).

 Operational Terms

Title changed from “Patrolman” to more inclusive “Patrol Officers”

Elimination of option to receive compensatory time in lieu of overtime payments (resulting
in elimination of a manually administered program)

Efficiencies in processes regarding shift bidding and vacation selection

Efficiencies in processes regarding end-of-year sick time and holiday time cash-out elections

Revision of shift differential provisions such that shift differential premiums will paid only
for actual shift-differential hours (currently, Patrol Officers incidentally can be paid shift
differential for daytime hours)

Incorporation into the Collective Bargaining Agreement of the currently active Memorandum
of Understanding between the parties enabling management to schedule 10- and 12-hour
shifts

Management agreed to participate in informal labor-management committee meetings to
discuss the following issues raised by the Union: weekend scheduling/lunches at satellite
locations; processes around uniform purchasing; and processes around physical fitness

 Reaffirmation of Commitment to Maintain a Level I Adult and
Level II Pediatric Trauma Center at MetroHealth Medical Center
******
RESOLUTION 19319
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees of The MetroHealth System has, heretofore, by Resolution 18957,
dated January 27, 2016, reaffirmed to maintain a Level I Adult and Level II Pediatric Trauma Center at
MetroHealth Medical Center;
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees of The MetroHealth System and administrative and medical staff of
the institution have developed and supported a Level I Trauma Center since 1984;
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees of The MetroHealth System has been presented a recommendation to
reaffirm its commitment to maintain a Level I Adult and Level II Pediatric Trauma Center at MetroHealth
Medical Center according to the published standards and guidelines of the American College of Surgeons;
and
WHEREAS, the President and Chief Executive Officer has reviewed this recommendation and now
recommends its approval.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Board of Trustees of The MetroHealth System hereby
reaffirms its commitment to maintain a Level I Adult and Level II Pediatric Trauma Center at
MetroHealth Medical Center.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, the President and Chief Executive Officer is hereby authorized to
negotiate and execute agreements and other documents consistent with this resolution.
