MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
June 26, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, June 26, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”)
was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building, Chicago, Illinois.
The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 10:52 A.M. Present were
Chairman Aaron Jaffe, Members Maribeth Vander Weele and Michael Holewinski.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES – Open Session
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its
Regular Meeting of May 29, 2014. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
DISSEMINATION OF MINUTES –
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve the dissemination of the Closed
Session Minutes of meetings from January 2013 through March 2014. Member Holewinski
seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
Administrator Ostrowski stated that there are 4,311 live locations and 17,449 VGT’s up and
running.
PUBLIC COMMENTARY –
•
Melynd Litchfield – Partner with Stop Predatory Gambling as a National Victims
Advocate
Ms. Litchfield addressed the Board stating the need for an SEP program to be extended to Video
Gaming.
Administrator Ostrowski stated that difficulties exist to have the program in place for video
gaming whereas the casinos are a restricted area within view of surveillance cameras. He stated
that they are exploring different avenues.
•
Illinois Association of Licensed Terminal Operators –
William Bogot addressed the Board on behalf of the Illinois Association of Licensed Terminal
Operators related to the taxation of individual municipalities on the terminal operator in order to
do business within the municipality.

 SUPPLIER, DISTRIBUTOR AND MANUFACTURER LICENSEE ITEMS –

• Happ Controls, Inc. – Distributor License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board approve the
Distributor License of Happ Controls, Inc., effective June 26, 2014, for a term of one
year, expiring in June 2015. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

• NRT Technology Corp. – Supplier License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve
the Supplier License of NRT Technology Corp., effective June 26, 2014, for a term
of one year, expiring in June 2015. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The
Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

• Welch Gaming, LLC – Supplier License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board approve the
Supplier License of Welch Gaming, LLC, effective June 26, 2014, for a term of one
year, expiring in June 2015. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

TERMINAL OPERATOR LICENSEE ITEMS –
• A1 Vending Inc. - Initial Terminal Operator License
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board deny
A1 Vending Inc.’s application for a Terminal Operator License. Member
Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.

• AP Gaming LLC – Terminal Operator License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board renew the
Terminal Operator License of AP Gaming LLC, effective June 26, 2014 for a
period of one year, expiring in June 2015. Member Vander Weele seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

• Brass Ring Gaming, Inc. – Terminal Operator License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board
deny the renewal of the Terminal Operator License of Brass Ring
Gaming, Inc. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote
• Universal Gaming Group, LLC – Terminal Operator License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board
deny the renewal of the Terminal Operator License of Universal Gaming

TECHNICIAN & TERMINAL HANDLER LICENSEE ITEMS –
