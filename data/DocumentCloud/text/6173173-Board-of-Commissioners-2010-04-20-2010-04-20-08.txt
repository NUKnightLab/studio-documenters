GENERAL BACKGROUND
As part of the Capital Improvement Program, the Chicago Housing Authority (CHA) solicited
bids for general contractors to perform general renovation of sixty-two (62) Family Housing
units, including site work and landscaping, at Washington Park Homes Low Rises (IL2–034) –
Phase 5. The work will be performed at547 - 555 E. 40th St., 557 - 567 E. 40th St., 601 - 611 E.
40th St., 613 - 627 E. 40th St., 629 - 639 E. 40th St., 641 - 651 E. 40th St., 653 - 657 E. 40th St.,
4008 - 4012 S. Langley St., 4417 - 4427 S. Wabash Ave., 4441 - 4447 S. Prairie Ave. and 5132 5146 S. Calumet Ave.) and will be completed within one hundred eighty (180) calendar days of
the date set forth in the Notice to Proceed. This work includes, but is not limited to, demolition,
interior renovation, exterior envelope, and site improvements for Phase 5.
PROCUREMENT HISTORY
The CHA advertised Invitation for Bid (IFB) No. 09-00470 on January 8, 2010 for the
renovation of sixty-two (62) Family Housing units, including the associated site work and
landscaping, at Washington Park Homes Low Rises (IL2–034), Phase 5. The IFB was advertised
in the Chicago Sun-Times, the Chicago Defender and Extra, and on the CHA website on
BuySpeed.
The Pre-bid meeting and Site Visit were held on January 14, 2010. The CHA issued Addendum
No. 1 on February 1, 2010. It extended the bid due date from February 4, 2010 to February 19,
2010. Addendum No. 2 was issued on February 4, 2010 to respond to Requests for Information,
provide notes on updates to the Drawings and Technical Specifications included in the original
release of the solicitation, and distribute revised versions of the BF pages as well as selected
Drawings.
The CHA received and opened eleven (11) bids, three (3) from contractors certified as MBE
businesses, on February 19, 2010 at 11:00 am,
After reviewing the bid submittal materials, the Department of Procurement and Contracts and
the Capital Construction Department held pre-award survey meetings with the three (3) lowest
apparent bidders. Each firm responded to questions on the thoroughness of its bid and its
capacity to perform this work.
Based on their submitted bid, the Department of Procurement and Contracts has determined that
Old Veteran Construction. Inc. (“Old Vet”) is the lowest responsive and responsible bidder at
$8,538,000.00. Old Vet has considerable experience as a general contractor for CHA renovation
efforts, most recently as part of the Indefinite Delivery, Indefinite Quantity program.
Based on the foregoing, it is in the CHA’s best interest for the Board of Commissioners to
authorize the Chief Executive Officer or his designee to execute a contract with Old Veteran
