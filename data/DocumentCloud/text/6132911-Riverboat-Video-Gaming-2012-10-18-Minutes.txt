MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - RIVERBOAT
OPEN SESSION
October 18, 2012
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, October 18, 2012 a Regular Meeting - Riverboat of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 2nd floor at 110 W. Randolph at the JRTC Building, Chicago, Illinois
at 10:15 A.M. Present were the following: Chairman Aaron Jaffe, Members Maribeth Vander
Weele, Michael Holewinski and Lee Gould.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of September 20, 2012. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
CHAIRMAN’S COMMENTS –
Chairman Jaffe commented on the hard working staff that the Gaming Board has and what a
great job the staff does.
ADMINISTRATOR’S COMMENTS –
Administrator Ostrowski gave a brief update on the progress of the riverboat casinos.
OWNER LICENSEE ITEMS
•

Item for Final Consideration – Elgin Riverboat Resort d/b/a Grand Victoria
Casino – License Renewal
Based on staff's investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board renew the
Owner’s License of Elgin Riverboat Resort d/b/a Grand Victoria Casino for a
term of four years, expiring in October 2016, and designate the positions,
persons and entities listed in its licensure letter as Key Persons of the licensee.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.

 OCCUPATIONAL LICENSEE ITEMS
•

Licensure of Levels 2 and 3
Based on Staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board find
suitable and approve 33 applications for Level 2 Occupational Licenses and 97
applications for Level 3 Occupational Licenses. Member Gould seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

PROPOSED COMPLAINTS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS •

Cynthia R. Morales – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board issue a
Disciplinary Complaint against Cynthia R. Morales for failing to disclose all
material changes in information to the Board, and that the Board revoke her
occupational license, said action to take effect 21 days from the date of
service of the Complaint, unless she files an Answer to the Complaint or pays
a $250 fine within that time period. Member Vander Weele seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Ubaldo Rocha – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board issue a
Disciplinary Complaint against Ubaldo Rocha for failing to disclose all
material changes in information to the Board, and that the Board revoke his
occupational license, said action to take effect 21 days from the date of
service of the Complaint, unless he files an Answer to the Complaint or pays
a $500 fine within that time period. Member Vander Weele seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Hope R. Bowdler – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board
issue a Disciplinary Complaint against Hope R. Bowdler for her admission to
rating a patron for play that he did not perform, and revoke her
occupational license, said action to take effect 21 days from the date of
service of the Complaint unless she files an Answer within that time period.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Laticia M. Ingram – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board issue a
Disciplinary Complaint against Laticia M. Ingram for providing a
fraudulent casino paystub in order to secure a car loan, and revoke her

 occupational license, said action to take effect 21 days from the date of

Heliodoro Lopez – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board issue a
Disciplinary Complaint against Heliodoro Lopez for his admission of theft of
tip money from hotel rooms assigned to other employees to clean, and revoke
his occupational license, said action to take effect 21 days from the date of
service of the Complaint unless he files an Answer within that time period.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Dina M. Nawara – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board
issue a Disciplinary Complaint against Dina M. Nawara for her violation of
Hollywood Joliet Casino’s code of conduct regarding theft, and revoke her
occupational license, said action to take effect 21 days from the date of
service of the Complaint unless she files an Answer within that time period.
Member Holwinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Jeremy A. Thilk – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board issue a
Disciplinary Complaint against Jeremy A. Thilk for his admitted conduct of
entering false data into the valet computer system to indicate he was parking
more vehicles than he actually was, and revoke his occupational license, said
action to take effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaint unless
he files an Answer within that time period. Member Vander Weele seconded
the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

At 10:35 A.M. Member Holewinski motioned to adjourn and Member Gould seconded the
motion. All Members voted in favor of adjournment.
