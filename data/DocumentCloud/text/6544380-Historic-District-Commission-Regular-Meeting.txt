EXISTING CONDITIONS
As per the Detroit City Assessor’s Office, the building which was located at 4865 Cortland was erected in
1924. Please see the attached photos and Google Earth Streetview which indicate that the home was twostories in height and was clad with buff brick at the first story and vinyl siding at the second story.
Windows were vinyl double-hung and single-hung units. A review of the property’s designation slide and
HDC property file indicated that the siding and windows present at the time of designation. The building
featured a side-gabled, gambrel roof which was topped with a shed-roof, full-width dormer at the front
elevation roof surface. A review of Google Earth Streetview indicates that the building’s roof began to cave
in sometime between 2016 and 2019.
Please see the attached documents, which indicate that the building suffered a recent fire which rendered
the premises “…structurally unsound, unsafe, dangerous…” and in “…imminent danger of collapse.”
Therefore on September 9, 2019, the Director of the Detroit Building, Safety, Engineering, and
Environmental Department issued an Emergency Demolition Order for the building.
As per Detroit, Michigan - Code of Ordinances, PART IV – Detroit City Code, Sec. 21-2-74. –
Emergency issuance:
Where it is determined by the Director of the Buildings, Safety Engineering, and Environmental
Department that work is immediately necessary for the protection of public health and safety, the
Buildings, Safety Engineering, and Environmental Department may issue a permit for the
necessary work and shall immediately notify the Historic District Commission of that action.
The permit for the demo was issued on September 18, 2018 and the building has since been demolished.
