The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:
1. 1000 S. Michigan
4th Ward
Historic Michigan Boulevard District
Proposed new construction of a 73-story residential tower with a total height of 832
feet.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. The proposed size, shape, scale and siting of the building as shown on
drawings dated 1-9-16 is approved as proposed; and,
2. A report by a licensed structural engineer addressing how the Lightner
Building at 1006 S. Michigan should be supported, braced, and
protected, including any vibration monitoring, during the excavation
and construction process shall be included on the permit plans. The
recommended measures, sequencing, and protections shall be
incorporated in the structural and architectural drawings; and,
3. All enlarged flashing details and foundation wall details between 1000
S. Michigan and the side walls and foundations of 1006 S. Michigan
shall be included in the permit plans; and,
4. The final selection of materials should return to the Permit Review
Committee for approval; and,
5. Any major changes to the proposal will come back for review and
approval by the Commission on Chicago Landmarks or by its Permit
Review Committee.

 2. 834 W. Chalmers
43rd Ward
McCormick Row House District
Proposed rehabilitation of single-family residence including a new 1-story rear
addition and patio and enclosure of an open second floor balcony on the west (side)
elevation.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. Replacement windows shall be wood or clad-wood and fit within the
entire masonry opening and dimensioned existing and proposed
window details shall be submitted with the permit application.
Windows enclosing the second floor balcony are to have a dark finish;
and,
2. As proposed the brick, stone and slate on the additions shall match the
historic masonry size, color, texture and profiles. New mortar shall
match the historic color, type, texture and joint profile.

3. 411 W. Eugenie
2nd Ward
Old Town Triangle District
Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation of 1½-story wood frame cottage
including new basement and a new rear addition.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. Due to the condition of the existing aluminum siding and the extent of
exterior alterations proposed the project should incorporate removal of
the non-historic siding to expose any historic wood siding below.
Historic Preservation staff shall be notified to view any remnants of
historic wood siding, which, if salvageable, shall be repaired and
retained. Any required new siding shall be wood clapboard to match
the historic profile and lap exposure for the existing building and the
addition. Fiber-cement substrate siding may be used as a substitute
cladding material where the building code requires non-combustible
siding. The siding shall have a smooth finish and otherwise match the
existing wood siding in size, profile, and finish. Dimensioned details
of the existing and proposed siding shall be reviewed and approved by
Historic Preservation staff as part of the permit application;
2. As proposed the project includes excavation for a basement and
construction of new foundation walls. The existing height of the
building will not change. A report by a licensed structural engineer
addressing the proposed new foundation, relative to how the building
should be braced and protected during excavation and construction,
shall be submitted for Historic Preservation staff review and approval,
as part of the permit plans. The recommended measures, sequencing,
and protections shall be incorporated in the structural and architectural
drawings;
3. The non-historic 1-story east enclosure may be maintained and
rehabilitated as proposed. The non-historic window configurations
may be modified and simplified subject to staff review and approval.

4. 2117-2139 N. Milwaukee
1st Ward
Congress Theater
Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation of existing theater building with groundfloor retail, 41 dwelling units, a new 1-story rooftop addition, and a new rooftop deck.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
Proposed Exterior Work:
1. As documented by BTL Architects, Inc. in report dated 12/1/15, the
remaining original metal storefronts and wood transoms are
deteriorated beyond repair. As proposed, a new custom steel storefront
system may be installed to match the historic profiles as closely as
possible. The patterned glazing of the transoms shall be salvaged and
reinstalled wherever possible and new film or glazing treatment shall
be incorporated to be similar in appearance to the original transoms.
Storefront shop drawings and storefront finish/color samples to be
reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff prior to order
and installation;
2. As proposed, the historic entry doors will be salvaged for a condition
assessment. Any doors that can be repaired will be reinstalled and if
beyond repair, any new doors will replicate the historic doors in
appearance. The condition assessment and replacement door shop
drawings to be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff
prior to order and installation;
3. The new clad-wood double-hung windows, to be 8/1 and 6/1 to match
historic configurations, are approved as proposed with new brick
molds and SDL profiles matching the historic profiles;
4. The new 1-story rooftop addition and rooftop deck are approved,
setback from the parapets as shown on drawings dated 2/16/16 with
the following conditions:

 •

The metal cladding of the penthouse and the perforated metal
panels of the cooling tower enclosure shall be a dark grey color
in a non-reflective finish;
• The railing shall be clear vision glass;
• To ensure the addition will not detract from the historic
Milwaukee façade, the visible and large operable glass door
shall be eliminated from the southern façade and more solid
cladding shall be incorporated into this façade design;
• The new rooftop addition and/or rooftop deck will require a
zoning variation and/or adjustment, and the Commission takes
no position regarding any requested variance/adjustment
relative to the zoning code requirements;
5. The proposed signage, including the non-flashing digital marquee and
poster signs and the new “Congress” blade sign letters are not
approved at this time. Additional information, such as a complete
zoning analysis; type of digital sign images with specified colors and
quantity of image changes per day; sign depths; sign colors; methods
of illumination; and attachment details regarding the proposed signs
will be required for future review by the Permit Review Committee;
Proposed Interior Work:
6. The new pedestrian bridge proposed in the main lobby, spanning
above the vestibule and providing accessibility and exiting code
requirements, is approved. The railing shall use clear vision glass and
the underside shall be a non-reflective neutral color compatible with
the color/materials of the main lobby. The bridge should not be
illuminated to become a feature of the lobby and material samples will
be submitted for review and approval by Historic Preservation staff
prior to order and installation;
7. Enlarged details of the four new openings within the plaster walls for
access to the bridge and for ground-floor access to adjacent spaces
shall be included in the permit plans;
8. Samples and mock-ups of plaster repair, paint colors, and new acoustic
treatment(s) shall be submitted for review and approval of Historic
Preservation staff prior to order and installation; and,
9. Any new fire protection, mechanical, plumbing, and electrical
elements within the designated landmarked interior spaces should be
located so as not damage or obscure any ornamental features and
should be designed to blend in within the space as much as possible.
Drawings showing attachment locations and details should be
reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff prior to order
and installation.
5. 1911 W. Schiller
1st Ward
Wicker Park District
Proposed rehabilitation of two-story, mixed-use building into single-family residence
including a new front façade and other alterations, a new sunken patio in the front
yard, a new rooftop deck, and a new rear garage.

 Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. The re-cladding as proposed on plans dated 3/28/16 is approved as
submitted. The applicant shall provide face brick, mortar, and stone
samples with the permit application for Historic Preservation staff
review and approval before order and installation. The brick and stone
colors and textures shall match typical colors and textures found
within the district, and mortar shall have a narrow profile and match
the color of the face brick;
2. The sunken patio in the front yard and doors at basement level are not
approved and shall be eliminated. If openings into the lower level are
needed or desired, simple punched windows in a scale appropriate for
a lower level may be added;
3. Plans shall be modified to show simple metal stair railings and
balusters with decorative newel posts for the new front stairs;
4. Door and window details shall be submitted with the permit
application for Historic Preservation staff review and approval;
5. The new garage at rear to be clad in smooth clapboard with a 3”
exposure and a recessed-panel garage door with glazing as proposed in
the plans dated 3/28/16 is approved; and,
6. The project as proposed would require a zoning variation and/or
adjustment, and the Commission takes no position regarding any
requested variance/adjustment relative to the zoning code
requirements.
