PROPOSED CONTRACT
Transmitting a Communication from
JOHN H. STROGER, JR., President, Cook County Board of Commissioners
Co-Sponsored by
JOSEPH MARIO MORENO, JERRY BUTLER, FORREST CLAYPOOL, GREGG GOSLIN,
ROBERTO MALDONADO, JOAN PATRICIA MURPHY and PETER N. SILVESTRI,
County Commissioners
requesting authorization for the Purchasing Agent to enter into a contract with Risk Management
Solutions of America, Chicago, Illinois; Advent Medical Management, Chicago, Illinois and Spectrum
Health Services, Inc., Bolingbrook, Illinois, to provide an integrated Workers' Compensation case
management program designed to contain costs and accelerate the return to work process for injured
employees on a pilot program basis.
A start up fee of $45,000.00 will be used to hire the appropriate personnel to administer the plan for the
County. The fee will cover the costs of conducting implementation meetings, mailings and special
programming. A call center is where the initial intake of claimant information originates. All other costs
and expenses will be billed directly to the claimant's file .
Reason:

Historically, the County experiences approximately 1,700 work-related injury cases per year,
with an estimate of approximately 525 of these cases involving lost time from work and
another 490 cases involving complex medical issues. Therefore, it is estimated that 1,015
newly reported work-related injury cases would be managed by a case manager saving the
County $365,000.00 or more based on the number of annual medical users.

Estimated Fiscal Impact: $I 35,000.00 (Year 1: $90,000.00 - $45,000.00 for start up cost and $45,000.00
total annual call center fee; and Year 2: $45,000.00 total annual call center fee). Contract period:
January 15, 2005 through January 14, 2007. (542-845 Account). Requisition No. 55420001.

Note: This item is also listed under the Department of Risk Management.

This item was WITHDRAWN at the request of the sponsor.

Chainnan Hansen asked Wally S . Kos, Superintendent of Highways, to give a synopsis of the Highway
Transportation Plan for 2004 through 2008 and to indicate the total figures of the Motor Fuel Tax Fund.
Wally S. Kos, Superintendent of Highways stated that the State of Illinois collects the Motor Fuel Tax by
state statute. Local governments receive fifty-four percent of the motor fuel tax. That portion of the
motor fuel tax fund is distributed as follows: The municipalities throughout the state receive roughly half
of the funds; counties with populations over one million in population receive almost seventeen percent
(one hundred and four million in the motor fuel tax); counties with population under a million (one
hundred and one counties statewide) receive a little more than one hundred and four million, and may
receive about one hundred and ten million. The County provides thirty million dollars on an annual basis
to the public safety fund ; two and one-half million goes to the Circuit Court Clerk for the automation fund
and two million to the Chicago Transit Authority.

requesting authorization to amend to the "Cook County Living Wage Ordinance" (98-0-21 ),
approved by the Board on September 15, 1998, for your approval. This item will be introduced at
the May 18, 2004 Board meeting and I will request that it be referred to the Finance Labor
Subcommittee for further review and discussion.
Similar to the City of Chicago' s Living Wage Ordinance (Title 2, Chapter 92, Section 6 10), the
proposed amendment increases the living wage for lowest-wage individuals employed by
contractors or subcontractors doing business with Cook County. The county's current living
wage of $7.60 per hour will be increased to $9.43 per hour if health benefits are provided, or
$10.68 per hour with no health benefits. There will be an annual adjustment calculated as I 00%
of the federal poverty line for a family of four (as defined by the U.S. Department of Health and
Human Services) with health benefits and 125% of the federal poverty line for a family of four
without health benefits.
In addition, the proposed amendment applies to companies benefiting from property tax
incentives as set forth in the Cook County Real Property Assessment Classification Ordinance.
These amendments will ensure fair wages and will contribute to the county' s overall economic
well-being.
The following is a synopsis of the Proposed Ordinance Amendment.

RECOGNITION OF WORLD AIDS DAY
DECEMBER 1, 2004
WHEREAS, 2,344 people are living with HIV disease in suburban Cook County; and
WHEREAS, the number of reported living HIV cases in suburban Cook County has doubled every 5
years in the past decade; and
WHEREAS, the suburban Cook County HIV epidemic impacts all genders, ages, races, ethnicities,
sexual orientations and communities; and

WHEREAS, the HIV epidemic is fueled by denial of personal and partner risk, failure to reduce risky
behavior, and lack of knowledge of HIV status; and
WHEREAS, HIV stigma and discrimination against HIV-impacted persons and at risk communities
threatens the public health, undennining effective HIV prevention and care programs by discouraging
individuals from coming forward for testing, risk-reduction support and HIV medical care; and
WHEREAS, other curable sexually transmitted diseases which facilitate HIV transmission remain
prevalent in many suburban Cook County communities, fostered by inadequate public awareness of these
diseases; and
WHEREAS, to protect the residents of suburban Cook County from disease and prevent the spread of
HIV, the Cook County Department of Public Health offers free, anonymous, HIV testing, confidential
STD testing and treatment, and risk-reduction counseling to all suburban Cook County residents; and
WHEREAS, the Cook County Bureau of Health Services offers free HIV care to uninsured suburban
Cook County residents living with HIV through the Ruth M. Rothstein Core Center and free STD
screening and treatment through Ambulatory Care Network clinics.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Cook County Board of Commissioners, in
recognition of World AIDS Day 2004, strongly encourages:

discrimination against people living with and at risk for HIV; and

means and self-confidence to protect themselves in the midst of an epidemic; and

confidential STD services, and risk-reduction counseling offered by the Cook County Department
