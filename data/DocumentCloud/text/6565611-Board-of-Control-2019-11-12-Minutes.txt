BC2019-826
Department of Health and Human Services/Division of Children and Family Services, recommending an
award on RQ45810 and enter into a contract with Community of Hope (37-1) in the amount not-to-exceed
$150,000.00 for trauma informed team mentoring services for transition age young adults who have
and/or are aging out of the foster care system in Cuyahoga County for the period 1/1/2020 – 12/31/2020.
Funding Source: 30% Title IV-E Admin; 70% HHS Levy
Chris Alexander, Cuyahoga Job and Family Services, on behalf of the Division of Children and Family
Services, presented.
Nan Baker asked if this service will replace Open Table mentoring services and if any more funds will be
allocated to that model; asked how many young adults will be served under this contract and if a
percentage of these services will capture youth aging out of the foster care system.
Trevor McAleer asked if Community of Hope is part of Cleveland State University and if this provider is the
same as the one currently providing Open Table mentoring services; asked if this service is different from
the Open Table model and if there was evaluation data available from that model; asked what other
providers attended the pre-bid conference for this Request for Proposals (RFP); asked how the project
benchmarks were determined and how the cost for trauma informed team mentoring services compare
to the prior Open Table model contract; asked how the RFP was written if the Open Table model was
proprietary or if the RFP was written for general mentoring services; asked if the reimbursement amount
of 30% has been the same as in the past and what has changed relative to allowing the County to be
reimbursed; and asked the department to provide evaluation data on the Open Table model and
recommended to hold this item for one week until additional information can be provided.
The Board members concurred with Mr. McAleer’s recommendation to hold the item for one week.
