The meeting was called to order by Chairman Rahm Emanuel, and the presence of a
quorum was established.
Following the roll call, a public participation period was conducted pursuant to Section
2.06(g) of the Open Meetings Act. George Blakemore and Kenneth Newman had previously
registered to provide comments under the guidelines established by Resolution No. 7611
approved by the Board of Commissioners on January 11, 2011. George Blakemore provided
comments regarding the large number of African Americans who are leaving the City of Chicago
and the need for more contracts and employment opportunities for African Americans. Kenneth
Newman provided comments regarding the condition of various field and track facilities that are
in disrepair and the need for additional athletic facilities throughout the City.
Next, the Chairman presented to the Board of Commissioners for consideration of
approval the minutes of the regular board meeting held on November 13, 2018. The reading of
said minutes, which had previously been distributed, was dispensed with and upon motion duly
made and seconded, the minutes of the November 13, 2018 regular meeting were unanimously
approved.
The next item on the agenda was a report by Commissioner Maldonado regarding the
Administrative Operations Committee meeting held on December 5, 2018 which is summarized
as follows:
The Chief of Staff reported on the following recommendations:
Task order awards for Environmental Engineering services to Carlson Environmental,
Inc. in the amount of $120,882.65 for the Brooks College Prep Athletic Field
Improvement Project and Environmental Reno/Demo services for $25,272 to ATC Group
Services for the Dore Elementary Pre-K Expansion Project. Awards for Material Testing
services in the amount of 23,782 to SEECO Consultants, Inc. for the Douglas Park
Rehabilitation Project and ECS Midwest for the Harrison Park Rehabilitation Project in
the amount of $12,930.

The next item on the agenda was consideration of approval of the appointment of firms to
provide Job Order Contracting services for projects defined by value classifications consisting of
three tiers as follows: Tier 1 – Projects Equal to or Less Than $300,000; Tier 2 – Projects
Between $300,000.01 and $1,000,000; and Tier 3 – Projects Greater Than $1,000,000.01. The
Commissioners were advised that Requests for Proposals were issued by the Public Building
Commission in an effort to increase capacity building in the general contracting community.
Twenty-four responses were received and evaluated on the basis of technical competence and
past performance, qualifications and relevant experience of key personnel, references, Minority
Business Enterprise (MBE) and Women Business Enterprise (WBE) utilization, and pricing.
After review the qualifications of each firm, the following firms were recommended for
appointment to provide Job Order Contracting Services for the respective tiers, namely: Tier 1 –
Projects Equal to or Less than $300,000: ALFA Chicago, Inc. (MBE), ALL Chicago, Inc.
(MBE), Anchor Mechanical, Inc., Apex Construction Group, Inc., Davila-OVC JV (Davila
MBE/WEB), Impact Construction Services Division I (MBE), Montel Technologies, LLC
(MBE), and Tiles In Style, LLC; Tier 2 – Projects Between $300,000.01 and $1,000,000: BOWA
Construction (MBE), CCC Holdings, Inc. d/b/a Chicago Commercial Construction, Cordos
Development & Associates, LLC, Master Design Build & Ground Crew Joint Venture LP
(MBE), Robe, Inc., and Structures Construction, LLC; and Tier 3 – Projects Greater Than
$1,000,000.01: The George Sollitt Construction Company, K.R. Miller Contractors, Inc.,
McDonagh Demolition, Inc., Old Veteran Construction, Inc., Paschen Ashlaur Joint Venture II
(Ashlaur – MBE), Tyler Lane Construction, Inc., UJAMAA Construction, LLC – EBE (MBE).

The next item on the agenda was a report by the Executive Director regarding regular
reports, development status and other matters. She advised the Commissioners that the Chicago
Public Schools and the Public Building Commission had a ribbon-cutting ceremony on
November 27, 2018 for the historic Lincoln Park High School Renovation Project. The project
included classroom renovations and mechanical system repairs as well as repairs to the roof,
windows and masonry restoration. Her report also included the display and distribution of the
Public Building Commission’s quarterly external newsletter entitled, “Constructive News You
Can Use.” The newsletter continued to provide highlights of firms that have worked with the
Public Building Commission and made a positive impact in their respective community. The
latest issue featured the woman-owned firm, “husARchitecture,” a four-person studio that is
currently the LEED Consultant at the Ebinger Elementary School Annex Project and Daley
College’s Manufacturing, Technology and Engineering Center. The Executive Director thanked
the Chairman and the Board for a great year and extended holiday greetings to all.

Finally, the Commissioners were presented with consideration of approval of the
appointment of firms to provide Construction Manager at Risk services to the Public Building
Commission for various projects undertaken on behalf of the Chicago Public Schools (Package
1). The Commissioners were advised that four proposals were received in response to the PBC’s
public advertisement for construction manager at risk services. After an initial review, each firm
was interviewed to determine technical competence, past performance, project approach and
methodology, key personnel, pricing, and MBE/WBE past participation. By consensus of the
Evaluation Committee the following firms were recommended for appointment to provide
Construction Manager at Risk services at Various Chicago Public Schools (Package 1), namely:
Project 1: Decatur Classical Elementary School – Berglund Construction Company; Project 2:
Dirksen Elementary School – Paschen Bowa (MBE) Joint Venture; Project 3: McDade Classical
Elementary School – Paschen Bowa (MBE) Joint Venture; Project 4: Palmer Elementary School
– Blinderman ALL (MBE) JV; Project 5: Poe Elementary Classical School – Berglund
Construction Company; Project 6: Rogers Elementary School – Henry Bros. Co. Further, the
Commissioners were advised that a Guaranteed Maximum Project (GMP) Cost, including the
Construction Manager’s fee, will be negotiated for each project. Upon motion duly made and
seconded, the following resolution was adopted:
