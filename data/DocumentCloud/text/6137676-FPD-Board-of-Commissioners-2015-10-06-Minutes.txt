15-0558
Sponsored by: TONI PRECKWINKLE, President, Forest Preserve District of Cook County Board
PROPOSED RESOLUTION
HONORING THE FOREST PRESERVE DISTRICT OF COOK COUNTY WITH THE
ILLINOIS ASSOCIATION OF PARK DISTRICT BEST OF THE BEST AWARDS
WHEREAS, on October 2, 2015, the Forest Preserve District of Cook County (“The Preserves”) was the
recipient of three (3) awards from the Illinois Association of Park Districts (IAPD) in recognition of its
100 year anniversary and in the categories of Intergovernmental Cooperation and Outstanding Citizen
Volunteer of the Year; and
WHEREAS, IAPD’s Best of the Best Awards is an opportunity to acknowledge those going above and
beyond to advance recreation and conservation across the state of Illinois; and
WHEREAS, the IAPD recognized the Preserves for its Centennial and its reaffirmation to ensure that
the forest preserves continue to protect natural diversity and provide the residents of Cook County with
the many benefits that come from spending time in nature; and
WHEREAS, the Cal-Sag Trail Coalition has been awarded with the Best of the Best Award in the
Intergovernmental Category. This award recognizes successful intergovernmental agreements or
partnerships that maximize tax dollars and benefit resident through the community. The Cal-Sag Trail
Coalition represents the successful collaboration among federal, state, local and community
organizations and individuals that has resulted in a 26 mile multi-use path from Lemont in the west to
Burnham in the east. The trail links more than 330,000 people in fourteen communities within Cook
County and celebrates this region’s rich cultural and natural assets; and
WHEREAS, Kathleen Garness has received the Outstanding Citizen Volunteer of the Year Award, an
award that recognizes citizens’ volunteer efforts to enhance forest preserves, parklands and other open
spaces. Kathleen was selected for her exceptional contributions to nature and conservation efforts on
behalf of the Forest Preserves. She volunteers throughout the county, is a certified stewardship workday
leader, rare plant monitor, and skilled artist who excels at botanical illustrations. Kathleen is a model
conservationist and volunteer of extraordinary generous and nurturing spirit and contributes significantly
to the wellbeing of the Forest Preserves; and
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that Toni Preckwinkle, President; the Board of
Commissioners; and Arnold L. Randall, General Superintendent of the Preserves thank and congratulate
the Preserves, the Cal-Sag Trail Coalition, and Kathleen Garness for their steadfast commitment to
protect and preserve the remarkable nature of the Preserves.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a suitable copy of this Resolution be presented to the members of the
Cal-Sag Coalition and Kathleen Garness as a token of appreciation for their service to the Preserves and
the residents of Cook County.

PROPOSED ORDINANCE AMENDMENT
BE IT ORDAINED, by the Forest Preserve District of Cook County Board of Commissioners, that Title
1 - Administrative, Chapter 8 - District Finances, Section 2B - Signatures Required on Contracts and
Section 2 H Contracts for Supplies, Material and Work are hereby amended as Follows:
TITLE 1 - ADMINISTRATIVE
CHAPTER 8 - DISTRICT FINANCES
1-8-1: RECEIPTS OF MONEY
1-8-2: CONTRACTS AND PURCHASES
1-8-3: WAGE RATES FOR PUBLIC WORKS EMPLOYEES
1-8-4: PROPERTY TAX RELIEF
1-8-5: MINIORITY - AND WOMEN-OWNED BUSINESS ENTERPRISES
***
Sec 1-8-2.B. Signatures Required on Contracts: All contracts for supplies, material or work, over one
hundred fifty thousand dollars ($150,000.00) twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00),shall be signed by
the President of the Board of Commissioners and or by any such other officer as the Board in its
discretion may designate. All contracts for supplies, material or work that are one hundred fifty thousand
dollars ($150,000.00) or less, may be signed by the General Superintendent if such contracts do not
require board approval and/or do not specifically require the signature of the President of the Board of the
Forest Preserve District Commissioners under this Code.
***
1-8-2.H Contracts for Supplies, Material and Work: All contracts for supplies, materials and equipment and
contractual services for the District shall be let as herein provided. All contracts for supplies, materials and
equipment and contractual services for the District which involve an expenditure of more than one hundred
fifty thousand dollars ($150,000.00) shall be approved by the Board of Commissioners and signed by the
President of the Board or his/her designee in accordance with Section 1-8-2.B of this Code.
Effective date: This ordinance shall be in effect on January 1, 2016.
