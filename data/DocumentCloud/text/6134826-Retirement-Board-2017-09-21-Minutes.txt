Report of Monthly Meeting No. 2017-09-02 held on Thursday, September 21, 2017
PUBLIC COMMENT
An annuitant member of the Fund requested a hearing regarding the status of his current benefits.
Ms. Mary Pat Burns, the Fund’s Attorney informed the annuitant that a Notice had just been sent him and to
the attention of his attorney setting his matter for Hearing in October 2017.
The annuitant thanked the Board and departed the meeting.
LEGISLATION
Legislative Update
Ms. Burns informed the Board that State Senate Hearings will occur on October 16-17, 2017.
The following topics were taken out of order:

INVESTMENTS
Monthly Performance Report
Mr. Brian Wrubel, Marquette Associates, reviewed the Fund’s investment performance for the month of
August.
Market Environment
Mr. Jamie Wesner, Marquette Associates, updated the Board on the current status of the market.
MEABF Investment Report
Mr. Yoon presented the staff investment report.
Liquidity Needs
Mr. Yoon reported on the current liquidity needs of the Fund. Based on this report and the recommendation
of staff and the Fund consultant, Trustee McMahon made a motion to liquidate up to $90 million to assist in
the payment of September benefit payments. Second by Trustee Johnson.
Carried: Ayes – Trustee Guest, Trustee Keane, Trustee Johnson and Trustee McMahon. Nays – None.
Watchlist
Mr. Yoon reported that Keeley, FIS and Cornerstone remain on watch.
Stress Test Discussion
Mr. Wesner reviewed a stress test of the Fund which analyzes the potential response the fund’s current
portfolio could have in various market environments with the anticipated changes in funding over the next
several years.

 MUNICIPAL EMPLOYEES' ANNUITY AND BENEFIT FUND OF CHICAGO
Report of Monthly Meeting No. 2017-09-02 held on Thursday, September 21, 2017
Pension Verification Update
Ms. Kristin DeWald, Supervisor of Annuitant Services, reported on the outstanding responses to the annual
Pension Verification mailing. As of the meeting date, approximately 1800 responses have not been received
with a final deadline for response of September 30, 2017.
FOIA Update
Ms. Ruffolo reported on requests made under the Illinois Freedom of Information Act.
Social Media Implementation Update
Mr. Mohler reported that the disclaimer language for the Fund’s Facebook page is finalized and the page
would be live by September 30, 2017. The Board confirmed in response to a question from Counsel that the
Board did not want there to be discussion on the Facebook page and that the Fund’s involvement be limited
to providing information.
Experience Study - Segal
Mr. Matt Strom, Mr. Dan Reusz and Mr. Jacob Libauskas of Segal presented an Experience Study to the
Board and their recommendations for actuarial assumptions for future valuations. The Board took the study
under advisement.
Medical Services RFP Update
Ms. Stacey Ruffolo, Projects Manager, reported on the status of the current Medical Services RFP.
2017 Trustee Election
Ms. Ruffolo reviewed the requested information on potential voting operations for Trustee Elections in 2018
and beyond. Robust discussion among the Trustees ensued. Based on these discussions, Trustee
McMahon made a motion to hold the 2017 Trustee Election in multiple locations. Seconded by Trustee
Keane.
Motion Failed: Ayes – Trustee Johnson. Nays – Trustee Guest, Trustee Keane and Trustee McMahon.
The Board further directed Fund staff to develop a plan to automate the voting operation of the Trustee
Election beginning in 2018.
Employee Service Credit Discussion
Ms. Burns reviewed a member request to purchase Service Credits that do not appear on a current City
work history form. Ms. Burns recommended, based on Article 8 of the Pension Code that the purchase of
service credits be allowed was permissible based on the documentation presented which consisted of a
City issued W2 for the period in question. The Board accepted that recommendation and directed Counsel
and staff on the matter.
Federal Turnover Order
Ms. Burns reviewed the Federal Turnover Order received in the Matter of McDaniels. The Fund was
directed to comply with the Turnover Notice based on the Court’s decision that the Federal Statute
supersede the anti-alienation provisions in Illinois Pension Code. Based on this review and the advice of
Fund Counsel, Trustee McMahon made a motion to comply with the Federal Turnover Order. Second by
Trustee Johnson.
Carried: Ayes – Trustee Guest, Trustee Keane, Trustee Johnson and Trustee McMahon. Nays – None.
