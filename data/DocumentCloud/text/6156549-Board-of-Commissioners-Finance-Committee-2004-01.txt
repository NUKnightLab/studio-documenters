01-C-796. These class-action lawsuits, brought on behalf of African-American
and Hispanic-American voters, challenge Cook County's use of punch-card
voting equipment under the Voting Rights Act and the Equal Protection Clause
of the Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution. All of the
defendants, which include the Illinois State Board of Elections and its
members, the Chicago Board of Election Commissioners, the East St. Louis
Board of Election Commissioners, the Counties of Alexander, Will, Whiteside,
and Sangamon, together with their County Clerks, have entered into separate
settlement agreements. There are two portions to the settlement agreements:
(i) plaintiff's attorney's fees and costs, and (ii) replacement of each defendant's
current voting system by taking advantage of federal funds under the Help
America Vote Act. Cook County has settled the attorney's fees and costs
portion of these lawsuits for $190,000.00, which is within the settlement
authority granted to this office by the Finance Committee's Subcommittee on
Litigation at its meeting of April 8, 2003. During a polling in July 2003, the full
Board approved the settlement terms requiring replacement of Cook County's
punch-card voting system with federal funds and ratified its approval at the
September 4, 2003 meeting of the County Board of Commissioners (see New
Item #6). State's Attorney recommends payment of $190,000.00, made
payable to Harvey Grossman (Legal Director of the Roger Baldwin Foundation
of the ACLU , Inc.) and Judson H. Miner. Please forward the check to Donna
M. Lach, Assistant State's Attorney, for transmittal.
