TO CALL A SPECIAL MEETING TO FILL VACANCY IN OFFICE OF PRESIDENT
WHEREAS, Cook County Board President, John H. Stroger, Jr. tendered a signed letter of resignation to
the Secretary of the Board and the Board of Commissioners on July 5, 2006; and
WHEREAS, Pursuant to 55 ILCS 5/2-6003, " Vacancy in office of president," reads in pertinent part, as
follows:

464

In case of the ... resignation .. .from office ... of the president so elected .. . If less than two
years and sixty days of the unexpired tenn remain, the board of commissioners shall elect
one of their number to serve the unexpired term of the president.
WHEREAS, President Stroger' s resignation from the office of the President becomes effecti ve on July
31, 2006, with approximately four months remaining in his tenn as president; and
WHEREAS, the vacancy in the office of the President is as of August I, 2006; and
WHEREAS, the Cook County Commissioner who is selected as the new president will need time for a
transition before taking office; and
WHEREAS, the public should be fully informed of the platfonn and qualifications of all board members
seeking to serve as President.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, THAT:
Pursuant to Rule 4-1 of the Rules of the Cook County Board of Commissioners, a Special Meeting of the
Board of Commissioners be called for July 1.2, 2006 to select a member of the Cook County Board of
Commissioners to serve as President from August I, 2006 until December 4, 2006 (the unexpired tenn of
President Stroger) pursuant to 55 ILCS 5/2-6003 and that the public be given an opportunity to participate
in such meeting aeeording to the Board's rules in accordance with this Resolution.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that at any special meeting to elect a member to serve the unexpired
tenn of President, the President shall be elected by a simple majority of those present and voting on the
question; provided that a vote of " present" shall not be counted as voting on the question; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that at such special meeting, nominations shall be made from the floor
at the call of the Presiding Officer, which nominations shall not require a second; after nominations are
closed by the Presiding Officer, the nominees, in alphabetical order, shall be allowed ten minutes to speak
to the nomination; after which voting shall be by roll call pursuant to Chapter XIV of Robert's Rules of
Order, Newly Revised, I 0th Edition, wherein each member when called upon shall declare his or her vote
for a single nominee or may vote "present;" the Clerk of the Board shall maintain a tally of the votes and
announce the tally at the Presid ing Officer's direction: if, after a vote upon the nominations, a single
nominee receives a simple majority of those present and voting on the question, the Presiding Officer
shall declare that member to have been elected President effective August I, 2006, upon taking the oath
of office, to serve until the newly elected President takes the oath of office on December 4, 2006; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that if, after a vote upon the nominations, a single nominee does not
receive a simple majority of those present and voting on the question, the Presiding Officer shall declare
no election and all previous nominations shall be cancelled; the Presiding Officer shall again call for
nominations from the floor, which nominations shall not require a second; after nominations are closed by
the Presiding Officer, the nominees who were not previously nominated shall be allowed ten minutes to
speak to the nomination, in alphabetical order; voting shall again be by roll call pursuant to Chapter XlV
of Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised, I0th Edition, wherein each member when called upon shall
again declare his or her vote for a single nominee or may vote "present;" the Clerk of the Board shall
maintain a tally of the votes and announce the tally at the Presiding Officer's direction; this process shall
continue until a member receives a simple majority of those present and voting on the question; and

wish to express their views on this important matter, public participation shall be limited to written

members.
