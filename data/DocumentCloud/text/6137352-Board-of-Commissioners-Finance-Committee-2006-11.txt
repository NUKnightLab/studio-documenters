WHEREAS, the Bureau of Health Services has failed to pay bills from 2003 to
2005 totaling $84 7 ,392.41; and
WHEREAS, it is estimated that the Bureau of Health Services has lost $1.4
billion in operating income due in part to the failure of its current billing
operation; and
WHEREAS, the Bureau of Health Services has been plagued for years with
expenses exceeding revenue, creating perpetual operating and budget deficits;
and
WHEREAS, recurring deficits can negatively affect Cook County's bond rating;
and
WHEREAS, the Bureau of Health Services has not been forthcoming about
divulging the true condition of its financial situation; and
WHEREAS, the Bureau of Health Services may have been engaged in
deceptive accounting practices by extending the time it takes to pay its
Accounts Payables; and
WHEREAS, by extending the time it takes to pay its Accounts Payables, the
Bureau of Health Services effectively rolls over an Accounts Payable deficit
each fiscal year; and
WHEREAS, by delaying payment, the Bureau of Health Services creates an
undue financial hardship for some county vendors; and
WHEREAS, the Bureau of Health Services claims payment delays are due to
insufficient funds; and

WHEREAS, the problem of insufficient funds is often due to the failure of the
Bureau of Health Services to encumber enough money to match their initial
expenditure projection; and
WHEREAS, the Bureau of Health Services fails to inform the Cook County
Board of Commissioners when it runs out of encumbered money to pay its bills;
and
WHEREAS, other reasons for non payment include lost billing invoices and
invalid/incomplete 29A voucher forms; and
WHEREAS, the Bureau of Finance has a staff of fourteen employees
responsible for the accounting and billing operation of 168 county departments;
and
WHEREAS, putting the Bureau of Health Services purchasing , bill payment
and accounting functions under the direct supervision of the Cook County
Bureau of Finance will provide better internal quality control, minimize payment
delays, and ensure the accurate transmittal of financial information to the
county board on a timely basis.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the President of the Cook County
Board of Commissioners shall begin the process of placing the Bureau of
Health Services purchasing, bill payment and related accounting functions
under the direct supervision and authority of the Cook County Bureau of
Finance. The President of the Cook County Board of Commissioners, the
Chief of the Bureau of Health Services, and the Cook County Chief Financial
Officer shall reallocate county resources and personnel to ensure that the
Bureau of Health Services purchasing, bill payment and accounting function is
under the direction of the Bureau of Finance.
*Referred to the Committee on Finance as amended on 09/07/06.

AN ORDINANCE CREATING A SPECIAL REVENUE FUND FOR THE
COLLECTION AND DISBURSEMENT OF THE FEE TO FINANCE THE PEER
OR TEEN COURT (PROPOSED ORDINANCE). Submitting a Proposed
Ordinance sponsored by Larry Suffredin, County Commissioner.
PROPOSED ORDINANCE

AN ORDINANCE CREATING A SPECIAL REVENUE FUND
FOR THE COLLECTION AND DISBURSEMENT
OF THE FEE TO FINANCE THE
PEER OR TEEN COURT
WHEREAS, on March 1, 2005, the Cook County Board of Commissioners
enacted Ordinance No. 05-0-15 (Section 18-37 of the Cook County Code)
which enables the Clerk of the Circuit Court of Cook County to collect a
mandatory fee of $5.00 by a defendant on a judgment of guilty or grant of
supervision under Section 5-9-1 of the Unified Code of Corrections; and
WHEREAS, the fee must be deposited by the Clerk of the Circuit Court of
Cook County into an account specifically for the operation and administration of
a teen court, peer court, peer jury, youth court, or other youth diversion
program, monthly, less five percent, which is to be retained as fee income to
the Office of the Clerk of the Circuit Court of Cook County; and
WHEREAS, a specific special revenue fund must be created so that the fees
may be collected and then disbursed for its stated purposes.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED, by the Cook County Board of
Commissioners that Chapter 18, Article 11, Sec.18-40 of the Cook County Code
is hereby enacted as follows:
Sec. 18-40. Peer Court Special Revenue Fund.
Beginning on or before December 1, 2006, the Comptroller shall create a
special revenue fund to be entitled, "The Peer Court Special Revenue Fund ."
The Circuit Court Clerk of Cook County shall deposit the fees generated from
Chapter 18, Section 37, into such Fund which shall only be disbursed to
appropriate entities for the operation and administration of a teen court, peer
court, peer jury, youth court or other youth diversion program within Cook
County. With the advice and consent of the Board, the Judicial Advisory
Committee shall be responsible for the disbursement of the Funds to
appropriate programs within Cook County on an annual basis.
*Referred to the Committee on Finance on 10/17/06.
