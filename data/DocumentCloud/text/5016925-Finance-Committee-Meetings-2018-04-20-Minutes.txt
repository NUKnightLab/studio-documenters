III. Action Items
B. Contracts and Procurement Items (continued)
With regard to request number 13, Douglas Elwell, Chief Executive Officer of Finance and Strategy, provided
additional information. CCHHS administration is working to move more of the organization’s specialists out into
the community; this is expected to make it easier for patients to be able to access those services. CCHHS is
essentially purchasing space from PCC Wellness; the space will be used for CCHHS specialists and diagnostic
equipment, and for building a lifestyle center.
Director Driscoll inquired as to how this relates to the goal of capturing more primary care patients; she asked
whether this initiative might help CCHHS to lose primary care patients to PCC Wellness. She asked whether
there is a way to monitor this and be cautious. Director Richardson-Lowry indicated that she had similar
concerns. She stated that a way to assuage her concerns would be to submit through the Chair information on
CCHHS’ tracking mechanism for whatever strategy they plan to use to track the patients. If the Committee had a
way to look at that data, perhaps on a semi-annual basis, they can assess the status – if there is a need to pivot at
the time of assessment, the Committee will have the information to make that decision.
With regard to request number 4, Mr. Elwell indicated that this is not for the provision of interim Chief Medical
Officer (CMO) services for the CountyCare Health Plan; the individual will assist the System’s CMO, Dr.
Claudia Fegan, as she covers the CountyCare CMO role while the administration recruits for that position. This
person was brought in to help CCHHS as a provider, by organizing the physicians a bit more so they are better
able to compete in a managed care world. This individual previously had experience with Blue Cross, and had a
tenure with Advocate running their physician group.
Director Richardson-Lowry noted for the record that it is good to know that this is not intended to be a CMO
substitute; if this is a person who works hand-in-glove with Dr. Fegan, then her voice should be the dominant
voice with respect to whomever the CMO happens to be. She is concerned that this may not be an individual who
has a lot of depth in the underserved community. Additionally, there is always a concern when it is a sole source
contract. Dr. John Jay Shannon, Chief Executive Officer, clarified for the record that the individual has served for
many years, both as a practicing physician and as a member of the Board, at Community Health, which is the free
clinic on the Northwest Side of Chicago. While his day job has been in a corporate risk-based insurance role, he
has kept himself very aware.
With regard to request number 5, Director Junge inquired whether information is tracked on the number of people
who are served by this grant. Dr. Shannon responded that information is occasionally received from them; he
indicated that it is a good idea to have key performance indicators for the contract.
With regard to request number 7, Mr. Jones noted that a request was made for a blank Press Ganey customer
satisfaction survey to be sent to the Directors for their information. This will be provided.
With regard to request number 11, Director Junge indicated that she has concerns with the proprietary nature of
the contractual arrangement; she inquired when the platform website will be moved to County ownership.
Chairman Reiter noted that, in his experience, when dealing with technology issues like this, it takes a while to
get off of the proprietary system and transition; from his perspective, it is not unusual to see this type of contract
as a sole source contract.
Chairman Reiter, seconded by Director Richardson-Lowry, moved the approval
of request numbers 1 through 18, as amended, and subject to completion of
review by Contract Compliance, where appropriate. THE MOTION CARRIED
UNANIMOUSLY.

Mr. Elwell presented the proposed Transfers of Funds for the Committee’s consideration. The Committee
reviewed and discussed the matter.
Director Richardson-Lowry inquired regarding the transfers to accounts for registry services for Stroger and
Provident Hospitals; she asked whether this relates to previously held discussions regarding moving some of those
services in-house. And, if that is the case, can the administration assure the Board that this is not something they
will have to see again or in a repeated fashion? Mr. Elwell responded affirmatively; the more staffing that they
can bring in-house makes it less likely that unanticipated transfers to pay for registry services will be needed.
Director Richardson-Lowry inquired regarding the transfers to accounts for professional services; she asked for
information on what the proportional share of that is based on sole source contracts. She requested that the
information be provided to the Committee through the Chair.
