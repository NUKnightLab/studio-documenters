CHICAGO DEPARTMENT OF AVIATION (“CDA”) REPORT
A. Presentation: 4th Quarter 2013 Airport Noise Management System
(“ANMS”) Report
Mr. Frame asked Mr. Jackson of Landrum & Brown to present the 4th Quarter
2013 Airport Noise Management System Report.
Mr. Jackson noted that the Midway Noise Management web page,
www.flychicago.com, appears on the bottom of the report cover page. He
provided a brief synopsis of the various topics found on the site and then
proceeded with the details of the report.
Runway Use Report
In the 4th quarter the most used departure runway was 31C with 45% departing
to the northwest. 31C was also the most used arrival runway with 52% arriving
from the southeast.
October – 75% of all departures were to the west. The most used departure
runway was 31C with 40%, followed by 22R/22L with 35%, 04L/04R had 18%
and 13C had 7%.
70% of arrivals came from the east going west. The most used arrival runway
was 31C with 49%, followed by 04L/04R with 23%, 22R/22L with 20% and 13C
with 8%.
November – 90% of all departures were to the west. The most used departure
runway was 31C with 45%, the next was 22R/22L with 44%, 04L/04R with 6%
and 13C with 5%.
85% of arrivals came from the east going west. The most used arrival runway
was 31C with 56%, 22R/22L with 28%, 13C with 9% and 04L/04R with 7%.
December – 80% of traffic departed west. 31C had 49%, followed by 22R/22L
with 31%, 04L/04R with 17% and 13C with 3%.
53% of arrivals landed on 31C, 26% on 04L/04R, 13% on 22R/22L and 8% on
13C.
Airline Fleet Mix Report
Southwest Airlines had the highest operations with an average of 417 per day.
The total operations for the quarter for all airlines averaged 658 per day, 53 of
which were at night.

The B737, used almost exclusively by Southwest, was the most used aircraft with
an average of 429 operations per day, 33 of which occurred at night.
Permanent Noise Monitor Locations
The report indicates the addresses of the 12 noise monitor locations near the
airport as reflected in relation to the 2018 Noise Contour. The report shows the
results for January 2013 through December 2013 with quarterly breakdowns and
a 12 month average. The 4th quarter 2013 results are compared to those of the
4th quarter 2012.
Noise Hotline Report
There were 566 total complaints for the 4th quarter, 280 of those were at night. Of
the total complaints, 547 came from Chicago with 500 of those coming from one
address. Taking that into consideration, it brings down the number of complaints
to around 70 which is comparable to quarters in previous years.
Mr. Jackson went over several additional handouts. The first was the Annual
Airport Operations which compared the 2013 yearly operation total to previous
years. The 2013 average operations per day were approximately 690.
The next handout showed the permanent noise monitor locations, the following
one showed the 2018 Noise Contour, and the last graphic showed the previous
65 DNL noise contours superimposed on the current 2018 contour for
comparison.
Ms. Dulaj asked for the meaning of overflight. Mr. Jackson responded that
operations consist of arrivals and departures and Mr. Frame added that
overflights are planes that fly over the airport but do not use the airport as their
destination.
Mr. Frame informed the Commission that new flight procedures would be going
into effect in the northeast corner for 22L arrivals within the next two weeks.
Chairman Baliga asked if helicopter flights are taken into account under the 2018
contour and Mr. Frame responded that they are. Chairman Baliga asked that
helicopters be identified and broken away from other general aviation in the Fleet
Mix Reports.
Mr. Lihosit asked if the report showing the number of homes participating by
ward were broken down by the current ward boundaries. Mr. Frame responded
affirmatively, the ward boundaries are those in effect until 2015.

NEW BUSINESS
A. Election of Chair and Vice Chair
The meeting was called back to order and Chairman Baliga turned it over to Mr.
Lombaer of the City Department of Law to conduct the election for the 2014
Chair and Vice Chair positions. He opened the floor for nominations. Mr.
Lazansky nominated Mr. Baliga for Chair and Ms. Pinion seconded the motion.
Chairman Baliga was re-elected unanimously. Mr. Lombaer then requested
nominations for the Vice Chair position and Mr. Lihosit nominated Mayor Klein.
Mr. Lazansky seconded the motion and it was passed unanimously.
Mr. Lombaer concluded the election.
Chairman Baliga asked if there was anything to include further under New
Business. Ms. Dulaj inquired about the response rate of the homeowner

he would provide her with those numbers.

Ms. Conwell. The motion passed.
