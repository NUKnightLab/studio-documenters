REPORT OF THE CHICAGO DEPARTMENT OF AVIATION (“CDA”)
A. Fly Quiet Program outreach to airlines
Mr. Frame reported that a letter was sent on September 19, 2016 to all of the
airlines operating at Midway (Southwest, Delta, Volaris, Frontier and Porter).
There were discussions with the two largest carriers, Southwest and Delta, about
the communities’ desires to minimize flights between the hours of 12:00 a.m. and
6:00 a.m. The airlines recommitted to striving to minimize the number of flights in
that time period.
Also discussed were the schedule challenges that the airlines faced during the
3rd Quarter. The main issue being afternoon summer storms that often pushed
flights to later in the evening.

B. 3rd Quarter 2016 Airport Noise Management System (ANMS) Report
Mr. Frame asked Mr. Jackson of Landrum & Brown to present the 3rd Quarter
2016 Airport Noise Management System Report.
Mr. Jackson provided a brief overview of the information contained in the report
and proceeded with the details.
Runway Use Report
In the 3rd quarter the most used departure runway heading to the northeast was
04L/04R with 34% of all departures. The most used arrival runway was also
04L/04R coming from the northeast with 37% of all arrivals.
July – The most used departure runway was 04L/04R going northeast with 37%
of all departures and 22L/22R with 33%, followed by 31C with 28% and last 13C
with 2%.
The most used arrival runway coming in from the southwest was 04L/04R with
40%, then 22R/22L with 35%, followed by 31C with 25% and last 13C with 0%.
August – The most used departure runway was 31C with 35% followed by
04L/04R with 31%, next 22R/22L with 27% and last 13C with 7%.

Airline Fleet Mix Report
There was an average of 717 operations per day with 89 of those occurring at
night. Southwest Airlines had the highest operations with an average of 482
operations per day with 70 of those occurring at night. The B737, used almost
exclusively by Southwest, was the most used aircraft with an average of 483
operations per day. General Aviation followed with 180 operations per day, 9 of
which were helicopters.
Permanent Noise Monitor Locations
The report indicates the addresses of the 14 permanent noise monitor locations
near the airport as reflected in relation to the Midway 2018 Noise Contour. The
report shows the results for July 2016 through September 2016 with quarterly
breakdowns and a twelve-month rolling average. The 3rd quarter 2016 results
are consistent when compared to those of the 3rd quarter 2015.
Noise Hotline Report
There was a significant increase in the number of communities submitting
complaints in the 3rd quarter compared to previous years. The total number of
complaints for the 3rd quarter was 30,220 of which 2,390 were at night. The
highest number of complaints came from Burbank with 23,427 and Justice with
3,420, with 80% of the complaints coming from three addresses.
C. Three-year composite of Fleet Mix reports
Mr. Jackson provided a brief overview of the fleet mix reports from the 1st quarter
2014 to the 2nd quarter 2016. The report showed that there has been a steady
increase in activity that fluctuates based on the season. Activity at night has
stayed constant with a 12% average.
D. Short discussion of Midway air traffic configurations
Mr. Jackson gave a brief description of the basic air traffic patterns that are most
used at Midway.

REPORT OF THE STANDING COMMITTEES
A. Residential Sound Insulation Sub-Committee Report
Ms. Conwell presented the Residential Sound Insulation Sub-committee Report
(see attached).
Mr. Frame announced that Phase 15 has concluded and that the program
received its 3rd grant from the FAA to help with the reimbursement of the homes
completed during this phase. With the completion of Phase 15, the program has
helped to sound-insulate over 10,000 homes over the last 20 years. We would
like to continue the program and start planning Phase 16 to include the remaining
1,000 to 1,600 homes from Phase 15 that did not previously sign up.
