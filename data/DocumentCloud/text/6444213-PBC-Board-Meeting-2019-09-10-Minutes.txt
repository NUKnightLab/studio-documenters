The meeting was called to order by the Chairman, Lori E. Lightfoot, and the presence of
a quorum was established.
Following the roll call, a public participation period was conducted pursuant to Section
2.06(g) of the Open Meetings Act. George Blakemore and Kenneth Newman had previously
registered to provide comments under the guidelines established by Resolution No. 7611
approved by the Board of Commissioners on January 11, 2011. George Blakemore provided
comments about opening up public meetings including committee meetings to various speakers
and the need maintain democratic procedures for participation by citizens. Kenneth Newman

 2

provided comments regarding the need to provide 20 additional athletic facilities and the use of
advertising to underwrite the cost of future improvements. He also urged the Public Building
Commission to work with the Chicago Public Schools and the Chicago Park District to obtain
additional recreational facilities.
The next item on the agenda was consideration of approval of the minutes of the regular
meeting held on August 13, 2019. The reading of said minutes, which had previously been
distributed, was dispensed with and upon motion duly made and seconded, the minutes of the
August 13, 2019 meeting were unanimously approved.

The next item on the agenda was a report by Executive Director Sánchez regarding
regular reports, development status and other matters. She presented photographic displays of
five new school projects that were delivered by PBC prior to the new school year, namely: a new
STEM High School located at 6835 S. Normal in the Englewood community; the new Taft
Freshman Academy located at 4071 N. Oak Park Avenue; school renovation projects for the
Lake View High School at located 4015 N. Ashland Avenue, and Dore Early Learning Center
located at 6108 S. Natoma; and updated High School Athletic Facilities for the Gwendolyn
Brooks College Prep Academy located at 250 East 111th Street.
The Executive Director provided in her report a detailed description and the development
teams for each project including MBE and WBE participants. She advised the Commissioners
that Public Building Commission projects were creating meaningful and significant economic
opportunities for the neighborhoods in which they were developed.
At the conclusion of the discussion, the report by the Executive Director was accepted.

WHEREAS, pursuant to the provisions of an Act to Authorize the
creation of public building commissions and to define their rights, powers and
duties, as amended, presently codified at 50 ILCS 20/1 et seq. (the “Act”), the
City Council (the “City Council”) of the City of Chicago (the “City”) created the
Public Building Commission of Chicago (the “Commission”) for the purpose of
facilitating the acquisition, development and construction of public buildings and
improvements; and
WHEREAS, the Commission has heretofore undertaken various programs
involving the acquisition, development and construction of buildings and facilities
for use by various public agencies including the Board of Education of the City
and the City (the “Board”); and
WHEREAS, the Board has requested that the Commission designate and
acquire certain properties commonly known as 941-943 West Castlewood
Terrace, Chicago, Illinois and legally described in Section 3 hereinbelow (the
“Property”) for the purpose of constructing an addition to the John T.
McCutcheon School at 4865 N. Sheridan Road (the “Project”); and

authorized and directed to negotiate for and on behalf of the Commission with the owner or

clear and merchantable title to the Property, Legal Counsel shall institute and prosecute
condemnation proceedings in the name of and on behalf of the Commission for the benefit of the
City for the purpose of acquiring title to the Property pursuant to the exercise of the
Commission’s powers of eminent domain.

If any provision of this Resolution shall be held to be invalid or unenforceable for
