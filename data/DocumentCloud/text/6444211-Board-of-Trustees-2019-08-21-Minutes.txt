Re: Victoria Hardeman v. James O. Hardeman, Ill
Ingham County Circuit Court Case No. 17-001171-DO
Resolution Moved By: Trustee Hall-Wagner - Supported By: Trustee Sheehan
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees is in receipt of an Amended Domestic Relations
Order for the General Retirement System of the City of Detroit (the "Order"), dated
December 5, 2017, wherein Victoria Hardeman, the Alternate Payee, is awarded certain rights
to the benefits of James 0. Hardeman, Ill, the Participant, and
WHEREAS, p u r s u a n t to the terms of the Order, the Alternate Payee is entitled to a
portion of the Participant's defined benefit portion of his retirement allowance payable from
the Retirement System,
WHEREAS, at the time of his retirement, Mr. Hardeman selected the Plan's straight life form
of benefit; however, before receiving his first payment, Mr. Hardeman completed an Option
Change form and selected the Plan's Option II, Joint and Survivor 100% form of benefit, and
WHEREAS, the Order incorrectly provides that the Alternate Payee is not entitled to a
surviving spouse benefit, and
WHEREAS, said matter had been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that the
applicable terms of said court order are consistent with the provisions of the Retirement
System and applicable law, therefore be it
RESOLVED, that the Board acknowledges receipt of said court order and the Board will pay
pension benefits consistent with the order as soon as administratively feasible, and further
RESOLVED, that the Alternate Payee will continue to be the beneficiary of the Option II, Joint
and Survivor 100% form of benefit, and further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this resolution be immediately attached as the top sheet of the
pension file and other appropriate records be kept for the Retirement System relative to
this matter, and further
RESOLVED, t h a t copies of this resolution be sent to James 0. Hardeman, Ill, t he
Participant; Victoria Hardeman, the Alternate Payee; and Kellie M. Foster, Esq.,
attorney for the Alternate Payee.

Yeas: Brown, Cowan, Hall-Wagner, Mclellan, Naglick, Perkins, Sheehan,
and Chairperson Nickleberry– 8
Nays: None

GENERAL COUNSEL’S REPORT- continued
Re: Tyrone Russell v. Flossie Mae Woodson
Wayne County Circuit Court Case No. 12-113412-DO
Resolution Moved By: Trustee Hall-Wagner - Supported By: Trustee Sheehan
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees is in receipt of an Eligible Domestic Relations Order
("EDRO"), dated July 9, 2019, wherein Flossie Mae Woodson, the Alternate Payee, is
awarded certain rights to the retirement allowance of Tyrone Russell, the Participant, and
WHEREAS, the Retirement System consists of both a defined benefit plan and a defined
contribution (a/kla the Annuity Savings Fund) plan, and
WHEREAS, pursuant to the EDRO, the Alternate Payee is entitled to a portion of both
the Participant's defined benefit plan and defined contribution plan benefits payable from
the Retirement System which has accrued in the Legacy Plan, and
WHEREAS, the payments from the plan to the Alternate Payee shall begin when the
Participant commences benefits from the Retirement System, and
WHEREAS, the Board's policy is to require that the cost for the actuary's calculations are to
be borne by the parties to the domestic relations proceedings and the parties' EDRO
provides that the parties are to proportionally share responsibility for any and all additional
costs for actuarial services, and
WHEREAS, said matter had been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that the
applicable terms of said court order are consistent with the provisions of the Retirement
System and applicable law including Public Act 46 of 1991 (MCLA 38.1701) as amended,
therefore be it
RESOLVED, that the Board acknowledges receipt of said court order, will pay pension
benefits consistent with said order upon application by the Participant, and further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this resolution be immediately attached as the top sheet of
the pension file and other appropriate records be kept for the Retirement System relative to
this matter, and further
RESOLVED, that copies of this resolution be sent to Tyrone Russell, the Participant;
Janice L. Burns, Esq., attorney for the Participant; Flossie Mae Woodson, the Alternate
Payee; and the Board's Actuary.

Yeas: Brown, Cowan, Hall-Wagner, Mclellan, Naglick, Perkins, Sheehan,
and Chairperson Nickleberry– 8
Nays: None
