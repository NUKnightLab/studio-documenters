MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - RIVERBOAT
OPEN SESSION
March 27, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, March 27, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Riverboat of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois at 10:09 A.M. Present were the following: Members Maribeth Vander Weele,
Lee Gould, and Michael Holewinski. Chairman Aaron Jaffe participated via telephone.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of February 26, 2014. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
The Administrator stated that Jacqueline Dowdy was taken off the agenda for this month and
announced that the Mark Osterhaus will be leaving his position as General Manager of Harrah’s
Metropolis Casino.

OCCUPATIONAL LICENSE APPROVALS AND DENIALS –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board find suitable
and approve 39 applications for Level 2 Occupational Licenses and 87 applications
for Level 3 Occupational Licenses. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The
Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
PROPOSED COMPLAINTS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS –
•

Antonio Gomez, James Kowalski & Joseph Villardito – Occupational Licensees
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved to issue Disciplinary
Complaints to revoke the Occupational Licenses held by Antonio Gomez, James
Kowalski and Joseph Villardito due to their admitted participation in providing a
patron with false ratings while employed at the Hollywood Joliet Casino, said
actions to take effect 21 days from the dates of service of the Complaints unless the
licensees file Answers within that time period.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.

•

Chad Thompson – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved to issue a Disciplinary Complaint
to revoke the Occupational License held by Chad Thompson due to his conduct of
theft while employed at the Par-A-Dice Casino, said action to take effect 21 days
from the date of service of the Complaint unless he files an Answer within that time
period.
