adult population in the U.S. had ever enrolled in high school, Trinity was sending approximately 75 percent
of its students to college; and
WHEREAS, in December of 1941, Trinity students, now numbering 700, sold War Bonds and Stamps
and were frequently recognized by the U.S. government for their impressive efforts; and
WHEREAS, in 1961, ground was broken for a new addition, and by 1968 the faculty consisted of 37
sisters, 24 lay teachers, two priests and a student population of 1,200; and
WHEREAS, with the influence of the Women’s Movement in the 1970’s, Trinity’s curriculum grew to
meet the challenges of a rapidly changing society, and this effort was rewarded with scholarships and
other recognition; and
WHEREAS, Trinity’s tradition of academic excellence continued with the introduction of the prestigious
International Baccalaureate IIB) program inaugurated in 1994; and
