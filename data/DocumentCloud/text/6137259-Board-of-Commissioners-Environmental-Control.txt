PROPOSED ORDINANCE
AIR POLLUTION OPERATING PERMITS/SITE FEES AND REGULATIONS CALLING
FOR THE REDUCTION IN REGULATED AIR POLLUTANT EMISSIONS FROM ALL
MINOR AND MAJOR SOURCE FACILITIES IN COOK COUNTY
WHEREAS, the Cook County area has been designated as an area of non-attainment
of suitable air quality and ozone standards by the United States Environmental
Protection Agency partially due to the air pollution emissions of the expansive industrial
businesses within its boundaries; and
WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners of the County of Cook have introduced a
number of air pollution reduction activities as an effort to diminish the detrimental effects
of air pollution on the residents of Cook County; and
WHEREAS, the accumulation of greenhouse gases and criteria pollutants in the
atmosphere are forming extremely high levels of ozone and adding to global warming;
and
WHEREAS, as the levels of pollutants increase in the Cook County area the number of
respiratory and cardiovascular related ailments proportionally increase; and
WHEREAS, the United States Environmental Protection Agency and the Illinois
Environmental Protection Agency have established regulatory standards in the areas of
permitting and reporting of air pollution sources in the efforts to track and reduce air
pollution levels; and

WHEREAS, in order to preserve, protect and improve the air resources of Cook County
so as to promote the health, safety, welfare and comfort of its residents, Cook County
shall take appropriate action in regulating air pollution within Cook County and take
efforts to promote the reduction in greenhouse gas emissions from all air pollution
source facilities.
