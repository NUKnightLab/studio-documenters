IV.

ADMINISTRATIVE MATTERS

Chairman Zimmerman asked Executive Director Brosnan to present the State Panel administrative actions
report for the period of time from February 1 to February 28, 2011. He presented the report, along with a
report on the agency's efforts at mediation, and summarized the highlights in each. The Board examined
and discussed the reports.
V.

BOARD ACTIONS
1.
Case No. S-RC-10-162
American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees, Council 31/
State of Illinois, Department of Central Management Services

Chairman Zimmerman called the case. General Counsel Post reviewed the issues presented by the
exceptions to Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) Tarver's Recommended Decision and Order (RDO), and
ALJ Harvey's intermediate order incorporated therein. As to the first part of the case, regarding the
intermediate order, General Counsel Post recommended that the Board uphold the determination on the
"managerial" finding, but reverse on the "confidential" issue and remand that portion for hearing. After
discussion, Member Washington moved to reverse ALJ Harvey's findings on both the managerial and
confidential issues and remand them for hearing. Member Kimbrough seconded the motion, and the Board
passed it unanimously, by a voice vote. As to the second part of the case, General Counsel Post
recommended that the Board reject ALJ Tarver's findings and find the employee at issue to be a supervisor.
After discussion, Member Hade moved to reject ALJ Tarver's determination. Member Kimbrough
seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
2.
Case No. S-RC-09-202
American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees, Council 31/
State of Illinois, Department of Central Management Services
Chairman Zimmerman called the case. Counsel Tansino reviewed the issues presented by the exceptions to
ALJ Bell's RDO, and recommended that the Board accept the ALJ's recommendation. After discussion,

 Member Kimbrough moved to reject the ALJ's recommendation and remand the case for hearing regarding
whether the employees at issue are managers. Member Coli seconded the motion, and the Board passed it
unanimously, by a voice vote.
3.
Case No. S-RC-10-194
International Union of Operating Engineers, Local 150/State of Illinois, Department of
Central Management Services
Chairman Zimmerman called the case. General Counsel Post, aided by Counsel Tarver's memorandum,
reviewed the issues presented by the exceptions to ALJ Tansino's RDO, and recommended that the Board
accept the ALJ's recommendation to find the employees in dispute were not supervisors. After discussion,
Member Hade moved to accept the ALJ's recommendation. Member Washington seconded the motion,
and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
4.
Case No. S-RC-11-005
Laborers Int'l Union, Local No. 751/County of Kankakee/Coroner of Kankakee County
Chairman Zimmerman called the case. Counsel Bell reviewed the issues presented by the exceptions to
ALJ Tansino's RDO, and recommended that the Board accept the ALJ's recommendation in its entirety.
After discussion, as to the first part of the case, regarding whether the ALJ should have conducted a hearing
on the status of the employee in the Chief Deputy Coroner title, Member Hade moved to accept the ALJ's
determination. Member Washington seconded the motion. The motion passed with four ayes and one nay;
Chairman Zimmerman dissented. As to the second part of the case, regarding the ALJ's finding as to the
status of the employee in the Administrative Assistant title, Member Washington moved to reject the ALJ's
determination. Member Hade seconded the motion. The motion passed with four ayes and one nay;
Member Kimbrough dissented.
5.
Case No. S-CA-10-307
Homero Bautista/State of Illinois, Department of Central Management Services
Case No. S-CB-10-079
Homero Bautista/American Fed. of State, County and Municipal Employees, Council 31
Chairman Zimmerman called the cases, noting that Executive Director Brosnan issued dismissals on
August 9, 2010, with appeals filed. General Counsel Post, aided by Counsel Wells' memorandum,
reviewed the issues presented by the appeals and recommended that the Board accept the Executive
Director's dismissals. After discussion, Member Hade moved to accept the Executive Director's dismissals.
Member Washington seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
6.
Case No. S-CA-11-027
Service Employees International Union, Local 73/Village of Carpentersville
Chairman Zimmerman called the case, noting that Executive Director Brosnan issued a partial dismissal on
October 15, 2010, with an appeal filed. Counsel Tansino reviewed the issues presented by the appeal and
recommended that the Board accept the Executive Director's dismissal. After discussion, Member
Kimbrough moved to accept the Executive Director's dismissal. Member Coli seconded the motion. The
motion passed with four ayes and one nay; Member Washington dissented.
7.

VI.

OTHER BOARD MATTERS

Executive Director Brosnan presented the fiscal report, reviewing in detail the general ledger sheet and the
disbursement record. Thereafter, he updated the Board as to the status of legislation that could affect the
agency and addressed various operational concerns previously raised by the Board. After a short
discussion regarding whether there was a need for executive session, the Board concluded that it was
unnecessary at this time.
VII.

ADJOURNMENT

Chairman Zimmerman called for a motion to adjourn. Member Kimbrough made a motion to adjourn the
meeting, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
