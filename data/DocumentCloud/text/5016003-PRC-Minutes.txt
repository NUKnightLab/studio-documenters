The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:
1. 2156 N. Cleveland
43rd Ward
Mid-North District
Proposed 1-story masonry rear addition to a corner single-family residence.
Action:

Approved unanimously as submitted

2. 2946-52 W. Armitage
1st Ward
Logan Square Boulevards District
Proposed removal of temporary containers and canopy and construction of two new
permanent patio roofs to an existing 1-story non-contributing restaurant structure.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The temporary storage containers and the existing rear patio roof shall be
removed as proposed and the permit application drawings shall be revised to
show their removal accordingly;
2. The proposed two new patio roofs facing Armitage Avenue and at the rear of
the property are approved as shown on drawings dated 9/12/14; and,
3. As agreed by the applicant, the covered patios will not have any temporary
plastic or other material enclosures.

 3. 1360 N. Milwaukee
Milwaukee Avenue District
Proposed 3-story rear addition to an existing 3-story masonry building.
Action:

1st Ward

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The paired windows on the south façade of the rear addition shall be modified
to eliminate the windows closest to the rear façade of the masonry structure to
be more compatible with the character of the building and more consistent
with the proportions of the historic openings of the property and district; and,
2. The rear addition’s proposed 8”-wide rain screen board cladding is approved
given its location at the rear of the property. The color of the siding should be
a neutral tone consistent with the color palette of the masonry of the subject
building and a sample shall be reviewed and approved by Historic
Preservation staff prior to order and installation.
19th Ward
4. 1724 W. 104th Place
Walter Burley Griffin Place District
Proposed new entry vestibule/seating area side addition to a single-family residence.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. All stucco to match color of existing but to be differentiated from the old in
finish (smoother than the existing stucco’s highly textured surface);
2. As submitted, all wood clapboard to match color, finish, and profile of
existing but to be differentiated from the old by not duplicating the distinctive
flared profile at the base of the home; and,
3. Door details to be submitted with the permit application.

5. 3334 S. Giles
Calumet-Giles-Prairie District
Proposed new 2-story masonry residence with a roof deck
Action:

4th Ward

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The height of the building at the front façade shall not exceed the height of the
adjacent historic building at 3332 S. Giles (23’-5 ¼”);
2. On the south elevation the height of the parapet at the front façade shall be
maintained and a simple open metal railing be installed for the roof deck, no
closer than 20’ to the front facade, painted black, and set in at least one foot
from the side parapet;
3. Material samples shall to be provided for staff review and approval prior to
order and installation. The cast stone on the front façade shall match color of
the adjacent Joliet limestone and the brick shall be comparable to common
brick;

 4. The permit plans shall include a structural report indicating how the adjacent
historic building will be protected during construction; and,
5. The Commission shall request in writing that the Zoning Administrator
approve an administrative adjustment to reduce the required side yard setback
from 2’ (combined 4’-1”) to 0’ for the proposed residence pursuant to Section
17-13-1003-G “Setbacks in Landmark Districts” of the Chicago Zoning
Ordinance.
6. 101 S. State
42nd Ward
Palmer House Hotel
Proposed tenant build-out within former C.D. Peacock and Co store space for new
jewelry store including a new entrance and vestibule, interior alterations, and new
light fixtures.
Action:

The motion to approve the project per the staff recommendations did not
pass (vote was 2 for and 3 against, with Commissioner Dziekiewicz,
Houlihan, and Smith voting against)
The Committee recommended that the applicant restudy and revise the
proposal and resubmit for a future Committee meeting. The revised
submission should address the following concerns discussed at the
meeting:
 Relocate entry vestibule to non-historic area of retail space
 Expose terrazzo flooring in vestibule
 Provide new solution for lighting that does not adversely impact
plaster ceiling or chandeliers
 Preserve historic ambiance as much as possible

7. 740 W. Randolph
27th Ward
Proposed Fulton-Randolph Market District
Proposed interior and exterior renovation of an existing 2-story masonry commercial
building with 1-story rear additions, including new storefronts and exterior cladding.
Action:

Approved 4-0 (Commissioner Wong recused) with the following
conditions:
1. The applicant shall remove a portion of the paint and use the
underlying color of the brick to inform the new paint color for the twostory portion of the project. Any proposed paint shall be vapor
permeable and formulated for use with brick;
2. Color samples of the metal paneling and cementitious siding shall be
submitted for Historic Preservation staff review and approval prior to
order and installation. Colors shall have precedent in the historic
masonry colors found within the district;
3. Any new windows on the second floor shall be wood or clad-wood
windows with brick molds. Dimensioned details shall be submitted
for staff review and approval prior to order and installation; and,

 4. Applicant to work with staff to reconfigure mullions and windows
arrangement for two enlarged first floor openings on Halsted Street
elevation to better relate to existing second floor masonry window
openings.
8. 1740 N. Wells
43rd Ward
Old Town Triangle District
Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation of a 4-story single-family residential
building including a new 2-story rear addition, a new entry canopy, masonry opening
alterations, and window and door replacement.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The existing non-historic entry opening on the Wells (front) façade may be
increased in height to incorporate a new transom and stone lintel as proposed,
consistent with the architectural character of the property and scale and design
of historic openings in the district. Enlarged details of the proposed new entry
canopy and front door cut sheet shall be included in the permit plans;
2. The existing large non-historic operable terrace-door opening on the Wells
(front) façade may not be enlarged as proposed. The opening may be retained
as existing or divided into two taller and narrower terrace door openings with
stone lintels, consistent with the width of the upper-floor historic window
openings of the property;
3. The new double-hung windows may be 2/2 configuration as evidenced by
similar building features and types in the district. The simulated divided-lite
muntins shall incorporate a spacer bar and exterior and interior profiles to
match typical historic muntin profiles. Enlarged window and brick mold
details to show the exterior profiles will match typical historic profiles shall be
included in the permit plans;
4. The north (side) parapet wall may be modified as proposed to align with the
top of the east (front) cornice. Brick removed from the rear and front walls
shall be salvaged and reused for the parapet work; and,
5. Details of the new entry gate in the masonry garden wall shall be included in
the permit plans.
9. 658 W. Belden
43rd Ward
Mid-North District
Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation including storefront replacement and
modification of existing window openings on the west elevation to accommodate new
doors and storefront windows.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. As proposed the enlarged window openings on the West elevation shall retain
the existing limestone sill for reinstallation, or match the typical limestone sill
in regard to size, color, and texture;

 2. The transom mullions above the fixed window sash in the four enlarged
window openings on the West elevation (currently 7’-6” above grade), shall
be raised to match the height of the storefront transom mullions along Belden
(9’-6” above grade); and,
3. Dimensioned details of the proposed storefronts shall be submitted with the
permit plans for Historic Preservation staff review and approval. As
proposed, new storefront elements shall have a dark, factory-applied finish.
8th Ward
10. 932-944 E. 79th
Chatham/Greater Grand Crossing Commercial District
Proposed masonry repairs and coating of the entire first floor terra cotta cladding with
architectural paint.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. Applicant may coat entire ground floor terra cotta with proposed Aquathane
UA-210 in a color to match terra cotta. Applicant agrees to monitor the
property for continued maintenance and reapplication of coating as necessary.
This permission is granted due to a unique situation of deteriorated conditions
at this particular property within the district;
2. Any cracks in the terra cotta shall be permanently sealed with an expandable
material such as butyl sealant or acrylic latex caulk in a color to match the
terra cotta; and,
3. As proposed, the applicant may coat the non-historic concrete block storefront
infill a color to match the white terra cotta and the concrete block knee to
match the black granite base of the piers.
11. 632 W. Belden
43rd Ward
Mid-North District
Violation: Proposed modification of elements constructed contrary to approved
permit, demolition of a 1-story rear garage, and revisions to previously approved
2½-story rear addition to existing single-family residence.
Action:

Approved 4-1 with Commissioner Dziekiewicz voting against and with
the following conditions:

1. The overall size, scale, height, roof shape and materials of the rear addition
are approved as submitted on plans dated September 12, 2014. A sample of
the proposed brick shall be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation
staff as part of the permit application;
2. The reconstruction, due to structural issues, and modification of the main
house roof is approved as submitted on plans dated September 12, 2014;
3. The demolition of the rear 1-story garage is approved;
4. Window details shall be included with the permit plans;
5. A revised permit shall be obtained prior to any further work occurring at the
property; and,

 6. The Historic Preservation staff shall approve the revised permit application
plans provided the approval of the permit would not forfeit any remedies for
the violation.
