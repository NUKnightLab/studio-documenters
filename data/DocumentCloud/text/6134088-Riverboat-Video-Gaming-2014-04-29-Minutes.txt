MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - RIVERBOAT
OPEN SESSION
April 30, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Wednesday, April 30, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Riverboat of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois at 10:20 A.M. Present were the following: Chairman Aaron Jaffe, Members
Maribeth Vander Weele, Lee Gould, and Michael Holewinski.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of March 27, 2014. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ITEM FOR INITIAL CONSIDERATION –
•

Des Plaines Development Limited Partnership d/b/a Harrah’s Joliet Casino & Hotel
– Waiver of the Two Meeting Rule Related to Approval of a New Game – High Card
Flush
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board waive the
Two Meeting Rule related to the request by Harrah’s Joliet Casino & Hotel for the
approval of a new game of High Card Flush. Member Gould seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

ITEMS FOR FINAL ACTION –
•

Des Plaines Development Limited Partnership d/b/a Harrah’s Joliet Casino & Hotel
– New Game-High Card Flush
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve
the game of High Card Flush as proposed by Harrah’s Joliet Casino & Hotel.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.

•

Penn National Gaming, Inc. – Jay A. Snowden – Sr. Vice President & Chief
Operating Officer – Key Person
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board approve Jay A.
Snowden as a Key Person of Alton Gaming Company, Empress Casino Corporation
and Hollywood Casino Aurora in his role as Senior Vice President & Chief
Operating Officer of Penn National Gaming, Inc. Member Vander Weele seconded
the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

 SUPPLIER LICENSEE ITEMS –
•

GEMACO, Inc. – License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board renew the
Supplier’s License of GEMACO, Inc. for a period of four years, expiring in April
2018 and designate the following entity, positions, and individuals as Key Persons of
the licensee:
•
GemGroup, Inc.
•
Chief Executive Officer
•
Chairman
•
Diana Kaye Summers
•
Danny R. Carpenter
Further, Member Holewinski moved that the Board authorize GEMACO, Inc. to
manufacture, sell or lease the following products in the State of Illinois:
•
Playing Cards;
•
Table Game Layouts;
•
Table Gaming Chips; and
•
The Attendant-Automatic Hopper Fill System.
Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

OCCUPATIONAL LICENSE APPROVALS AND DENIALS –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board find
suitable and approve 51 applications for Level 2 Occupational Licenses and 75
applications for Level 3 Occupational Licenses. Member Gould seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATIVE REQUESTS FOR HEARING/REQUESTS TO REAPPLY –
•

In Re: the Disciplinary Action of Judith Christensen – DC-13-07
Pursuant to Section 1155 of the Board’s Adopted Rules, Member Gould moved to adopt
a Final Board Order adopting the Administrative Law Judge’s Order and Finding
of Default, and Revoking the Occupational License held by Judith Christensen.
Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

•

In Re: the Disciplinary Action of Nyphodora Gonzalez – DC-13-27
Pursuant to Section 1155 of the Board’s Adopted Rules, Member Holewinski moved to
adopt a Final Board Order adopting the Administrative Law Judge’s Order and
Finding of Default, and Revoking the
Occupational License held by Nyphodora
Gonzalez. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

PROPOSED COMPLAINTS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS –
2

 •

Ernest Lee Marbley – Occupational Licensees
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved to issue a Disciplinary
Complaint to revoke the Occupational License held by Ernest Lee Marbley for his
statutorily disqualifying conviction, said action to take effect 21 days from the date
of service of the Complaint unless the licensee files an Answer within that time
period. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

At 10:29 A.M. Member Gould motioned to adjourn and Member Vander Weele seconded the
motion. All Members voted in favor of adjournment.
