MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
February 21, 2013
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, February 21, 2013 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 2nd floor at 110 W. Randolph at the JRTC Building, Chicago, Illinois.
The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 1:50 P.M. Present were Members
Lee Gould, Michael Holewinski and Maribeth Vander Weele. Chairman Jaffe participated via
speaker phone.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES – Open Session
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its
Regular Meeting of January 24, 2013. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
Administrator Ostrowski gave a summary of the past month’s progress in video gaming.
PUBLIC COMMENTARY – Tom Gray
Tom Gray addressed the Board referencing the legalization of video gaming and its effect on the
general public.
MANUFACTURER, DISTRIBUTORS AND SUPPLIERS LICENSEE ITEMS –
•

Initial Supplier Licenses
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board approve the
Supplier Licenses of Ares Gaming Technologies LLC and Patriot Gaming &
Electronics, Inc., effective February 21, 2013, for a term of one year, expiring in
February 2014. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

TERMINAL OPERATOR LICENSEE ITEMS –
•

Initial Terminal Operator Licenses
o Skyhigh Gaming, LLC

 Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board
approve the Terminal Operator License of Skyhigh Gaming, LLC,
effective February 21, 2013 for a period of one year, expiring in February
2014. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
o Laurel Gaming, LLC
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board approve
the Terminal Operator License of Laurel Gaming, LLC, effective
February 21, 2013 for a period of one year, expiring in February 2014.
Member – seconded the motion. Member Holewinski seconded the motion.
Chairman Jaffe and Member Vander Weele were opposed to the motion. The
motion did not pass.
o KH Vending, Inc.
Based on a review of staff’s investigation and recommendation, Member
Vander Weele moved that the Board deny KH Vending, Inc.’s application
for a Terminal Operator’s License. Member Holewinski seconded the
motion. Member Gould abstained from the motion. The Board approved the
motion unanimously by roll call vote.
•

Terminal Operator License Renewals
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board renew
the Terminal Operator Licenses of the following entities, effective February 21,
2013, for a period of one year, expiring in February 2014: All American
Gaming, Inc.; Allied Gaming, LLC; American Video Gaming, LLC; Awesome
Hand Services, LLC; Gold Rush Amusements, Inc.; Lattner Entertainment
Group Illinois, LLC; Melody Gaming, LLC; Morris Gaming, Inc.; Renville
Gaming, LLC; Slot Kings Gaming, LLC; Triple 7 Illinois, LLC; and VIC
Entertainment, LLC. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the
motion unanimously by roll call vote.

•

B&B Harris Management, LLC
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board deny the
renewal of the Terminal Operator License of B&B Harris Management, LLC.
Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

ADMINISTRATIVE REQUESTS FOR HEARING –
•

Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the requests for
hearing from Kevin J. Kapp; Geno’s Bar & Grill, Inc.; Four G’s Inc.; Valex, Inc.; and
The Spirit House, Inc. be denied based on the fact that none has offered facts to
establish a prima facie case. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
