Testimonial Resolution of Appreciation for Trustee Lori Cetlinski

Re: Testimonial Resolution of Appreciation for Trustee Lori Cetlinski
Resolution Moved By: Trustee Sheehan

- Supported By: Trustee Hall-Wagner

WHEREAS, Lori Cetlinski has loyally served the General Retirement System of the City
of Detroit as a Trustee on the Board of Trustees from January 28, 2014 to June 30,
2019, and
WHEREAS, Lori routinely displayed an understanding of the fiduciary responsibilities a
Trustee must have to serve the members and beneficiaries of the Retirement System
and
WHEREAS, has used her understanding of the fiduciary responsibilities of a Trustee to
service the members and beneficiaries of the Retirement System, and
WHEREAS, Lori carried out her responsibilities as Trustee with dedication, sincerity,
and diligence and has provided distinguished service to the Board and the members
and beneficiaries of the Retirement System, and
WHEREAS, the Trustees and the members and beneficiaries of the Retirement System
owe a debt of gratitude to Lori for her service to the Retirement System, therefore be it
RESOLVED, that the Board of Trustees, on behalf of itself and all concerned, hereby
expresses its gratitude and appreciation to Lori Cetlinski for her honorable and
distinguished service as Trustee to the General Retirement System of the City of
Detroit, and be it further
RESOLVED, that the Board of Trustees, on behalf of all the members and beneficiaries
of the General Retirement System of the City of Detroit, says "Thank You Lori for a
Job Well Done".
Yeas: Benson, Cetlinski, Cowan, Hall-Wagner, Naglick, Sheehan
and Chairperson Nickleberry – 7
Nays: None

Re: Victoria Hardeman v. James O. Hardeman, III
Ingham County Circuit Court Case No. 17-001171-DO
Resolution Moved By: Trustee Sheehan - Supported By: Trustee Hall-Wagner
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees is in receipt of an Amended Domestic
Relations Order for the General Retirement System of the City of Detroit (the “Order”),
dated December 5, 2017, wherein Victoria Hardeman, the Alternate Payee, is
awarded certain rights to the benefits of James O. Hardeman, III, the Participant, and
WHEREAS, pursuant to the terms of the Order, the Alternate Payee is entitled to a
portion of the Participant’s defined benefit portion of his retirement allowance payable
from the Retirement System,
WHEREAS, at the time of his retirement, Mr. Hardeman selected the Plan’s straight
life form of benefit; however, before receiving his first payment, Mr. Hardeman
completed an Option Change form and selected the Plan’s Option II, Joint and
Survivor 100% form of benefit, and
WHEREAS, the Order incorrectly provides that the Alternate Payee is not entitled to a
surviving spouse benefit, and
WHEREAS, said matter had been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that
the applicable terms of said court order are consistent with the provisions of the
Retirement System and applicable law, therefore be it
