Regular Meeting, Open Session Minutes – Riverboat and Video Gaming
Illinois Gaming Board
Chicago, Illinois
January 30, 2019
Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”) convened for a Regular Meeting – Riverboat and Video Gaming – on
Wednesday, January 30, 2019 in the Conference Room on the 5th Floor of the Michael Bilandic Building
Center, in Chicago, Illinois pursuant to the Illinois Open Meetings Act, 5 ILCS 120/1 et seq.
The following Board members were physically present: Chairman Don Tracy, Members Hector
Alejandre, Member Steve Dolins. Due to illness, Member Dee Robinson participated telephonically.
Three members of the Board physically being present, a quorum was satisfied.
APPROVAL OF OPEN SESSION MINUTES
Member Alejandre moved that the Board approve the open session minutes from its Regular Meeting
of December 12, 2018. Member Dolins seconded the motion. The motion passed unanimously via
voice vote.
CHAIRMAN’S REPORT/COMMENTS
Chairman Tracy thanked staff and members of the public for braving the bitterly cold outside
temperatures and for allowing the meeting to take place as scheduled. Chairman Tracy briefly
commented about some recent press coverage regarding the Illinois Gaming Board. The Chairman
acknowledged the recent departures of Mark Stevens and Frank Spizziri from the Illinois Gaming Board
and thanked them for their years of service to the agency. The Chairman then announced that the Board
would be proposing a new Video Gaming rule on inducements at the day’s meeting. The Chairman also
expressed his desire and intent to develop a stronger Self-Exclusion program for Video Gaming in the
near future.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT
Acting Administrator Agostino Lorenzini reported that there are approximately 6,843 licensed video
gaming location establishments operating approximately 31,046 video gaming terminals.
RIVERBOAT PROPOSED RULE
Administrator Lorenzini announced that a proposed draft of a Rule to implement an amendment to the
Riverboat Gambling Act with respect to veterans’ percentages would not be presented at today’s meeting
due to some recently raised questions and concerns that staff needs additional time to investigate before
1

 presenting to the Board.
