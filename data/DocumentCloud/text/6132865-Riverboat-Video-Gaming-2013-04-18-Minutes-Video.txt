MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
April 18, 2013
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Wednesday, April 18, 2013 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois. The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 10:26 A.M.
Present were Members Michael Holewinski, Maribeth Vander Weele and Chairman Jaffe.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES –
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its
Regular Meeting of March 20, 2013. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The
Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
•

Redacted Closed Session Minutes – July 2011 – December 2012

Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve the Dissemination of the
Closed Session Minutes of Meetings from July 2011 through December 2012.
Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.
BOARD MEMBERS’ COMMENTS –
Member Vander Weele stated that on licensing applications, a problematic relative of an
applicant will not disqualify an applicant based solely upon a familial relationship. However,
she will look at connections such as a business relationship between an applicant and his/her
problematic relative.
ADMINISTRATOR’S COMMENTS –
Administrator Ostrowski announced the live number of video gaming ocations and VGT’s.
PUBLIC COMMENTARY –
Richard Meitzler, of Bally Technologies appeared before the Board voicing opposition to
legislation that would allow Scientific Games International to be the central communication
system vendor and WMS to supply VGT’s to the Illinois video gaming maker.
MANUFACTURER, DISTRIBUTORS AND SUPPLIERS LICENSEE ITEMS –
•

Safe Pay Services, LLC – Supplier Licensee Renewal

 Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board renew
the Supplier License of Safe Pay Services, LLC, effective April 18, 2013, for a
term of one year, expiring in April 2014. Member Holewinski seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call.

TERMINAL OPERATOR LICENSEE ITEMS –
•

Initial Terminal Operator Licenses
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board approve
the Terminal Operator Licenses of the following entities, effective April 18, 2013
for a period of one year, expiring in April 2014: Advantage Gaming, LLC;
Allstate Gaming, LLC; and Sinbad’s Castle Arcade, Ltd. Member Vander Weele
seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Platinum Gaming Illinois LLC
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board deny
the Terminal Operator License application of Platinum Gaming Illinois LLC.
Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Terminal Operator License Renewals
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board renew the
Terminal Operator Licenses of the following entities, effective April 18, 2013 for
a period of one year, expiring in April 2014: A.S.A.P. Gaming, Inc.; Brass Ring
Gaming, Inc.; Da Route LLC; DSG Amusement, Ltd.; Gaming & Entertainment
Management - Illinois, LLC; Grand River Jackpot, LLC; Great Lakes Vending,
Corporation; Illinois Operators, Inc.; Midwest Electronics Gaming, LLC; MJ
Solutions, LLC; Schaffner Gaming LLC; T’s Gaming, LLC; Tangent Gaming
Capital, LLC; Torch, LLC; Universal Gaming Group, LLC; and Velasquez
Gaming, LLC. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

•

TJM Vending, Inc.
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board deny
the renewal of the Terminal Operator license of TJM Vending, Inc. Member
Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.
