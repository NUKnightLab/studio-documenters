19 Notices
Famicos Foundation, owner, proposes to establish use as administrative offices, afterschool child
educational programming, meeting/assembly space (indoor spaces for 93 occupants) and outdoor
event performances for a non-profit community arts organization in an A1 One-Family Residential
District. The owner appeals for relief from the strict application of the following sections of the
Cleveland Codified Ordinances:
1. Section 337.02(g)(3)(G) which states that the proposed use is allowed in a One Family District
if approved by the Board of Zoning Appeals after public notice and public hearing, and if
adequate yard spaces and other safeguards to preserve the character of the neighborhood are
provided, and if in the judgment of the Board such building and uses are appropriately located
and designed and will meet a community need without adversely affecting the neighborhood,
and if located not less than thirty (30) feet from any adjoining promises in a Residence
District. Proposed use is within 30 feet of adjoining premises in a Residence District.
2. Section 349.04(d) which states that accessory off street parking is required at the rate of one
space per 500 square feet of office space, and one space per 150 square feet of assembly
space therefore 13 accessory off street parking spaces are required. Any proposed off
premises parking requires approval of the Board of Zoning Appeals. (Filed September 25,
2018)

Ward 6
Blaine A. Griffin
21 Notices
Blossom 24 Hour We Care Center, Inc., owner, proposes to establish use as assisted living for the
aged in a C2 Residence-Office District. The owner appeals for relief from the strict application of the
following sections of the Cleveland Codified Ordinances:
1. Section 337.11 which states that in a Residence-Office District, every building or structure
erected, altered or remodeled and every use of land occupied on or after December 16, 1959,
shall provide the total number of accessory off-street parking spaces as required the Zoning
Code.
2. Section 337.12 which states that an application for a building permit shall not be approved
unless a parking plan is submitted with it that shows the location, dimensions and graded of all
required parking spaces.
3. Section 337.13 which states that the required parking for homes for the aged is 1 for each staff
member, including doctors and nurses, plus 1 for each 3 other employees expected on the
premises during the largest work shift period, plus 1 for each 6 beds. 6 spaces are required
and no off-street parking proposed.(Filed October 1, 2018)
