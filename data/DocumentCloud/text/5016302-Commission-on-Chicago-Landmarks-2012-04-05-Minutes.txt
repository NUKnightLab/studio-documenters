81 E. Van Buren
(Historic Michigan Boulevard District – 2nd Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed construction of one-story rooftop addition, canopies, and deck

Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. The massing, siting and configuration of the one-story rooftop addition –
proposed to be setback 52’-0” from the exterior face of the parapet on the
east side of the building and 15’-7” from the exterior face of the parapet on
the north and south sides of the building; the large canopy – proposed to be
setback 28’-5” from the exterior face of the parapet on the east side of the
building, 15’-7” from the exterior face of the parapet on the north side and
5’-0” from the exterior face of the parapet on the south side of the building;
and the small canopy located behind the proposed addition, with a
maximum height of 9’-10” above the top of the historic parapet – are
approved as submitted;
2. The brick for the addition, corrugated metal for the back wall of the small
canopy, and black powder-coat finish for the structure of both canopies are
approved as proposed; and,
3. Attachment details and cut-sheets for the sconces and attachment details for
the handrails shall be submitted with the permit application.

2.

2036 W. Thomas
(Ukrainian Village District – 32nd Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed replacement of damaged outer wythe of face brick on the front
façade with salvaged and new brick, and restoration of previously altered

 second floor masonry opening.
Action:

3.

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. The exterior wythe of masonry at the front facade, much of which is
structurally unsound and pitted, may be removed and replaced to match the
historic design, masonry coursing, profiles and details of the facade. As
proposed, the inner wythe and stone water table shall remain in place and
all decorative masonry and other salvageable face brick shall be retained
and reinstalled. Any new masonry, where necessary, shall match the
historic masonry in size, color, texture, finish, and joint profile. A plan
outlining the proposed process of documentation, removal and rebuilding
of the masonry shall be submitted with the permit application. Masonry
and mortar samples, details of existing and proposed joint widths, as well
as a control mock-up on site, shall be reviewed and approved by Historic
Preservation staff prior to permit approval;
2. The arched masonry window opening at the second floor shall be
reconstructed using the neighboring buildings as a model for detailing. The
new windows shall be wood or clad-wood, and shall replicate the existing
brick molds found on the first floor windows. Window details shall be
submitted with the permit plans;
3. The new front doors shall match size, configuration and profiles of existing
front doors. Existing and proposed drawings shall be submitted with the
permit plans; and,
4. Reconstruction of the non-historic front stairs and canopy shall be subject
to staff review and approval with the permit plans. In general, historic
stairs within the district are wood with closed risers with metal pipe railings
and newel posts.

203 N. Wabash
(Old Dearborn Bank Building – 42nd Ward)
Proposal:

Proposed rehabilitation of office building into a hotel including exterior
masonry repairs, window and storefront replacement, rooftop addition and
other alterations.

Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
Masonry Facade
1. As proposed, replacement masonry, where required, shall match the size,
color, profile, finish and texture of the historic masonry. Masonry repair
and replacement details shall be included on the permit plans. Material
samples shall be submitted for review and approval prior to order and
installation;
2. As proposed, the new fixed aluminum windows shall match typical historic
double-hung wood windows in exterior profiles and dimensions. Window
details and finish sample shall be submitted with the permit application;

 3. The terra cotta base of the building shall be cleaned with the gentlest means
possible. Cleaning product specifications and material safety data sheets
shall be submitted with the permit application. A test cleaning sample shall
be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff prior to any work;
Storefronts
4. The first floor storefront mullions shall incorporate a decorative cap more
in keeping with the historic character of the building. Storefront details and
finish samples shall be submitted for review and approval with the permit
application;
5. The main entrance exterior doors shall be revised to incorporate transom
panel divisions as seen on the historic interior vestibule and decorative caps
as recommended for the storefront mullions;
Awnings, Canopy, Lighting Fixtures and Signage
6. As proposed, awnings shall be canvas, triangular, open-ended with a freehanging valance and shall be attached to the new storefronts rather than the
historic terra cotta. Awning details and attachment details shall be
submitted with the permit application;
7. The canopy proposed above the main entry shall be located above the
decorative terra cotta of the entry and below the decorative terra cotta sills
of the 2nd floor windows. Canopy details and attachment details shall be
submitted with the permit application;
8. The cut-sheets, attachment details, and finish of the proposed first floor
lighting sconces shall be included with the permit application;
9. The proposed exterior architectural lighting of the facades shall be further
studied to ensure that lighting fixtures shall not be attached to or damage
historic decorative masonry and shall not be located to be visible from the
public rights-of-way. Historic Preservation staff shall review final details,
fixture cut-sheets and a site mock-up of the lighting scheme prior to permit
approval;
10. Any proposed signage shall be subject to Historic Preservation staff review
and approval when available;
Lobby
11. As proposed, as much as possible of the original decorative mullions for
the interior vestibule shall be retained and details of the reconstruction
process shall be included with the permit application;
12. Changes to the historic cigar stand and sprinkler and lighting installation
details shall be included with the permit plans;
Rooftop
13. The rooftop additions for the 26th-floor terrace and the elevator override
shall be no larger or taller than shown in the submitted drawings dated
March 15, 2012; and,
14. Metal cladding sample shall be reviewed and approved prior to permit
approval.
