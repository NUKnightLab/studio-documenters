Resolution Moved By: Trustee Hall-Wagner - Supported By: Trustee Cetlinski

GENERAL COUNSEL’S REPORT- continued
Lori M. Hill v. Gary T. Hill
Macomb County Circuit Court Case No. 18-8183-DO
Resolution Moved By: Trustee Hall-Wagner - Supported By: Trustee Cetlinski
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees of the General Retirement System of the City of
Detroit is in receipt of a Domestic Relations Order (“DRO”) dated March 26, 2019,
wherein Lori M. Hill, the Alternate Payee, is awarded certain rights to the retirement
benefits of Gary T. Hil, the Participant, and
WHEREAS, Section 9 of the DRO, titled “Death of Retirant”, incorrectly states that “[i]n
the event that the Retirant predeceases the Former Spouse, such Former Spouse shall
continue to be designated as the surviving spouse of the Retirant for purposes of
establishing Former Spouse’s entitlement to receipt of a monthly post-retirement
survivor annuity, as elected by the Retirant when he retired.”, and
WHEREAS, the Participant retired on August 5, 2013, withdrew his contributions from
the Annuity Savings Fund, and elected the Retirement System’s Straight Life form of
benefit, and
WHEREAS, said matter having been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that
the terms of said DRO are inconsistent with the provisions of the Retirement System
and applicable law, and
WHEREAS, the DRO does not meet the requirements of the Retirement System and
applicable law, therefore be it
RESOLVED, that the Board acknowledges receipt of said court order, rejects said court
order as inconsistent with Plan provisions and applicable law, and hereby indicates that
it will not pay pension benefits in accordance with the terms of said order until such time
as an acceptable order is presented to the Retirement System, and further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this Resolution be immediately attached as the top sheet of
the Participant’s pension file and other appropriate records be kept for the Retirement
System relative to this matter, and further
RESOLVED, that the Board’s Legal Counsel will correspond directly with the parties
and, if applicable, their legal counsel explaining the reasons that the Board has rejected
the DRO.
Yeas: Benson, Cetlinski, Cowan, Hall-Wagner, Mclellan, Naglick, Perkins, Sheehan and
Chairperson Nickleberry– 9
Nays: None
