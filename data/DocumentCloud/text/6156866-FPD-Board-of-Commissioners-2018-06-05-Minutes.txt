PROPOSED RESOLUTION
CONGRATULATING AND THANKING THE FRIENDS OF THE FOREST PRESERVES ON
ITS 20-YEAR ANNIVERSARY
WHEREAS, the Forest Preserves of Cook County manages nearly 70,000 acres of public land including
some of the highest quality natural lands in the State of Illinois that provide residents and visitors
opportunities for recreation, education, and pleasure; and
WHEREAS, the mission of Friends of the Forest Preserves is to be “the voice of a diverse community
inspiring and organizing people to protect, restore, and expand the forest preserves in Cook County”; and
WHEREAS, Friends of the Forest Preserves is the only independent non-profit organization solely
focused on the Forest Preserves of Cook County and strives to safeguard and improve the forest
preserves for generations to come; and
WHEREAS, Friends of the Forest Preserves was founded in 1998 by a small group of volunteers who
cared deeply about Cook County’s natural and public lands and has since grown to be a force of more
than 2,000 members and each year inspires and engages thousands of people to enjoy and care for the
forest preserves; and

WHEREAS, Friends of the Forest Preserves helped develop the Next Century Conservation Plan, an
ambitious blueprint to make Cook County a national leader in urban conservation, and now plays a
leadership role in the plan’s implementation; and
WHEREAS, Friends of the Forest Preserves played an instrumental role in developing the Conservation
Corps, a program that provides high school students and young adults with hands-on conservation
projects to help restore land, including raising more than $5 million in support that made possible
engaging more than 950 young people who have helped care for and improve over 1,000 acres of Forest
Preserves’ land; and
WHEREAS, the Forest Preserves of Cook County deeply values its long-standing partnership with
Friends of the Forest Preserves and all that the organization does to leverage resources and opportunities
for the benefit of the Forest Preserves of Cook County in the areas of restoration, education, volunteer
stewardship and building a community of support; and
WHEREAS, the Forest Preserves of Cook County appreciates the spirit of collaboration, generosity, and
enthusiasm that Friends of the Forest Preserves brings to everything it does.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that Toni Preckwinkle, President, and the Board of
Commissioners of the Forest Preserves of Cook County, on behalf of ourselves and the staff of the Forest
Preserves, do hereby congratulate and thank the board, staff, and members of Friends of the Forest
Preserves on its 20th anniversary.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that suitable copies of this resolution be presented to the Friends of
the Forest Preserves.

PROPOSED RESOLUTION
HONORING THE JOHN G. SHEDD AQUARIUM IN ITS PARTNERSHIP WITH THE
FOREST PRESERVES OF COOK COUNTY
WHEREAS, the John G. Shedd Aquarium is a world-renowned aquarium, research and conservation
institution, and Chicagoland destination that partners and collaborates with the Forest Preserves of Cook
County; and
WHEREAS, this partnership and collaboration reflects a commitment to improving the health of our
local waters, our native flora and fauna and a recognition of the importance of actively engaging Cook
County residents; and
WHEREAS, since 2010, Shedd Aquarium has partnered with the Forest Preserves to engage hundreds of
local teens to learn and care about the Forest Preserves via stewardship and litter clean-up activities; and
WHEREAS, in 2015, the Shedd Aquarium partnership expanded with the creation of the “Amphibians”
exhibit; where global and local amphibians and the Forest Preserves were highlighted, raising the profile
of these important assets to Shedd visitors; and

WHEREAS, Shedd Aquarium created “Bringing Light to Amphibians”, a 5-minute video that celebrates
local amphibians and the importance to steward and protect their habitats; the video has been viewed by
audiences around the region; and
WHEREAS, Shedd Aquarium, as part of their “Great Lakes Action Days” initiative, has adopted three
Forest Preserves’ sites intersecting three waterways: McClaughrey Springs Woods and Mill Creek
Stream; Somme Woods and the North Branch of the Chicago River; and Bob Mann Woods and Salt
Creek Stream; and
WHEREAS, Shedd Aquarium has made a significant effort and organizational investment to engage
communities in stewardship along our local waterways by hosting stewardship volunteer days and
engaging thousands of volunteers, who are contributing thousands of volunteer hours to the Forest
Preserves on an annual basis; and
WHEREAS, Shedd Aquarium engages the public in stewardship practices including invasive species
removal, seed collection and community science activities such as water quality monitoring and
amphibian monitoring, which encourages learning and caring about our local natural resources; and
WHEREAS, Shedd Aquarium shares the Forest Preserves’ commitment to protect the integrity of the
natural environment and wildlife from litter and pollution, and its “Shedd the Straw” campaign is a
commendable effort to encourage businesses and government entities to commit to provide biodegradable
straws upon request, discontinue use of plastic straws, and to promote sustainable benefits of reducing
single-use plastic pollution.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that Toni Preckwinkle, President, and the Board of
Commissioners of the Forest Preserves of Cook County thank and congratulate the trustees, staff, and
supporters of the John G. Shedd Aquarium for their commitment to the mission of the Forest Preserves
and the natural treasures of the region.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that a suitable copy of this Resolution be presented to the Shedd
Aquarium as a token of appreciation for its service to the Forest Preserves and residents of Cook County.

PROPOSED RESOLUTION
TO APPROVE AND ADOPT AMENDED EMPLOYEE TRAVEL POLICY
WHEREAS, the Forest Preserve District of Cook County (the “Forest Preserves”) recognized the
occasional need for employees to travel to various conferences, seminars, meetings, and other work
related events; and
WHEREAS, the Forest Preserves created the Employee Travel Policy to provide guidelines on the
following: (1) the types of official business for which travel, meal, and lodging expenses are allowed; and
(2) maximum allowable reimbursement for travel, meal, and lodging expenses; and
WHEREAS, the Employee Travel Policy was approved by Forest Preserves Board of Commissioners on
October 25, 2016; and
WHEREAS, the Forest Preserves now would like to amend such Employee Travel Policy to (1) establish
pre-approval process for overnight travel; (2) to specify when travel to and from alternate work locations
would be reimbursed; and (3) to otherwise clarify rules and bring consistency to the reimbursement
process; and
WHEREAS, the Employee Travel Policy, as amended, continues to be in compliance with the Local
Government Travel Expense Control Act, 50 ILCS 150/1 et seq. (the “Act”).
NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, by the President and the Board of Commissioners of the
Forest Preserve District of Cook County that the Employee Travel Policy, as amended, is hereby adopted
by resolution effective immediately.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Forest Preserves is authorized to further modify such
Amended Employee Travel Policy for the efficient operation of the Forest Preserves without further
board approval as long as such modification does not increase the reimbursement rates referenced in the
policy.

Summary: The Forest Preserve District of Cook County (the “Forest Preserves”) operates and
maintains the North Branch Bicycle Trail in Northern Cook County. The Village of Niles proposes to
construct two trail segments, each approximately 300 linear feet in length, to connect residents and
businesses to the trail as part of a larger bike and pedestrian improvement program.
