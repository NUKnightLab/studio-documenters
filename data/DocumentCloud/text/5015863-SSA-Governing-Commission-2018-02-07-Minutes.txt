Incident Report
Incident Report was read by Ina Stankeviciene. From October 1, 2017, to January 31, 2018 the
Illinois Homeland Security addressed 1350 security events of varied nature: there were 387 security events in
October, 325 – in November, 308 – in December and 330 in January. 448 requests for assistance came directly
from the SSA#14 dispatch central, other calls – from the CPD dispatch. Disturbance is still the most prevalent
incident addressed by the Illinois Homeland Security – 46% (624 for all four month). Most of them 27%
occurs in Zone 3, and 11% – in Zone 4. Domestic violence and Burglary alarm rise to 11% both. Assault
reported 6 times for all four month. For all four months burglary reported 68 times, battery – 19 times, robbery
– 8 times, theft – 26. Suspicious activity, person or auto were reported 88 times. Laud reports/Shots fired – 22.
Weapons offence or person with a gun was reported 33 times, person shot – 3 in October, 3 in November, 2 in
December and 1 in January. The same three zones (3 and 4) always require the greatest amount of effort, with
zone 3 being the highest with 610 (45%) incidents and the zone 4 – 276 (20%) incidents for the past four
months.

Expiration of SSA#14 Ordinance
Joe Polikaitis explained that the present Marquette Park SSA#14 Ordinance expires at the end
of 2018. The Department of Planning and Development (DPD) has reviewed SSA Feasibility Study
package submitted by Lithuanian Human Service Council of USA, Inc. on November 1st, 2017 for
consideration of Special Service Area in Marquette Park community. DPD grants approval to proceed
with a 2019 SSA designation application with the following conditions:
1. The proposed boundaries shall not include any residential blocks.
2. The proposed commercial area boundaries may remain, but are subject to change
based on further review during the designation process.
3. The sponsoring agency shall now be the Greater Southwest Development Corporation,
which will be responsible for completing the required designation steps leading to a
formal SSA application.
The Lithuanian Human Services Council will continue to be the 2018 Service Provider for
SSA#14, which will expire on December 31, 2018.
There were a lot concerns and strong disappointments expresed by atendees about this City of
Chicago decission. Patrick Brutus explained the policy of the Department of Planing and
Development and answered questions. Joe Polikaitis stated that it is City‘s final desicion and nothing
can be done to change it by the Lithuanian Human Services Council. Joe indicated that Greater
Southwest Development Corporation is in the renewal process. No details were available. Part of the
SSA#14 businessess will be incorporated into the new SSA and thus, taxes collected from these
businessess will be forwarded to the new SSA. The balance of taxes collected will remain with
SSA#14 and will be used for additional security purposes
CPA Audits
Joe Polikaitis explained that as required by City of Chicago, SSA financial books are audited
every year and report submitted to the Department of Planning and Development by May 1st. This
year audit should be completed by April 25,2018. Request for auditing service proposals (RFP) has
been issued.
Audit report will be presented to the Commissioners for approval prior to submission to the City.
