STATE OF ILLINOIS
ILLINOIS LABOR RELATIONS BOARD
STATE PANEL
Minutes of the State Panel Meeting
Held on June 12, 2018
I.

OPENING OF MEETING 1:00 P.M.— by video conference at 160 N. LaSalle Street, Room N703,
Chicago, IL and 801 S. 7th Street, Room 5A, Springfield, IL

II.

PRESENT

John Hartnett, Chairman; Christopher Glynn, Member of the Board; Keith Snyder, Member of the Board; Kimberly
Stevens, Executive Director; Helen Kim, General Counsel; and staff members participated via video conference from
the Board’s Springfield office. John Samolis, Member of the Board; Anna Hamburg-Gal, Associate General Counsel;
and staff members participated via video conference from the Chicago location.
III.

APPROVAL OF MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING HELD May 8, 2018

Chairman Hartnett called for approval of the minutes of the May 8, 2018 meeting. Member Glynn moved to approve
the minutes. Member Samolis seconded. The Board passed the motion unanimously, by a voice vote.
IV.

ADMINISTRATIVE MATTERS

Chairman Hartnett asked Executive Director Stevens to present the Executive Director’s reports. She presented the
reports and summarized the highlights.
V.

REVIEW OF PROPOSED WRITTEN DECISIONS AND ORDERS

There were none.
VI.

BOARD ACTIONS
1. Case Nos. S-CA-16-028, 029, 030, 031, 032, 044
Int’l Brotherhood of Electrical Workers, Local 193, American Federation of State,
County and Municipal Employees, Council 31, Int’l Union of Painters and Allied Trades,
District Council 58, Local 90, District 9, Int’l Association of Machinists and Aerospace
Workers, Plumbers and Steamfitters, Local 137, Carpenters, Local 270/City of
Springfield

General Counsel Kim called and presented the issues of the case to the Board. After discussion, Member Samolis
moved to modify the Administrative Law Judge’s recommendation regarding Case No. S-CA-16-032, to reflect
withdrawal of the charge; to accept the ALJ’s recommendations regarding Case Nos. S-CA-16-028, 031, and 044
dismissing them in their entirety and his recommendations dismissing the allegations involving AFSMCE Local 3417
and Local 3738 in Case No. S-CA-16-029; and reject his recommendations finding unfair labor practices in Case No.
S-CA-16-029 involving AFSCME Local 337, and in S-CA-16-30 and dismiss them in their entirety.
Member Glenn seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
2. Case No. S-RC-18-022
American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees, Council 31/Chief Judge of the
Eighteenth Judicial Circuit
General Counsel Kim called the case, and noted that no party filed exceptions to Administrative Law Judge Purcell’s
Recommended Decision and Order. The Board declined to review the decision on its own motion.

 3. Case No. S-CA-17-146
American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees, Council 31/City of Harvey
General Counsel Kim called the case, and noted that no party filed exceptions to Administrative Law Judge HamburgGal’s Recommended Decision and Order. The Board declined to review the decision on its own motion.
4. Case No. S-CA-16-006
State of Illinois, Department of Central Management Services/American Federation of State,
County and Municipal Employees, Council 31
Executive Director Stevens summarized the mandate of the Illinois Appellate Court, Fifth District, to the Board. She
then presented the Motions filed by the parties. After discussion, Member Samolis moved to deny Respondent’s motion
to strike portions of AFSCME’s response. Member Snyder seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously,
by a voice vote. Member Glynn then moved to deny Respondent’s motion to set case for hearing. Member Samolis
seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote. Member Samolis moved that the Board
find that an unfair labor practice was committed, order a make-whole remedy, and refer the matter to the Board’s
compliance process. Member Glynn seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
VII.

EXECUTIVE SESSION

After a short discussion regarding whether there was a need for executive session, the Board concluded it was
unnecessary.
VIII.

PUBLIC COMMENT

No members of the public sought to make comments.
IX.

ADJOURNMENT

Chairman Hartnett called for a motion to adjourn. Member Samolis moved to adjourn the meeting, and the Board
passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
Adopted and approved by the Chairman and Members of the State Panel of the Illinois Labor Relations Board,
in Chicago, Illinois, this 10th day of July 2018.
