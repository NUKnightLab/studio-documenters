A RESOLUTION CALLING ON THE ILLINOIS GENERAL ASSEMBLY TO CREATE AN
INDIANA BORDER TAX AND REGIONAL ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT STRATEGY
WHEREAS, Cook, Will, Iroquois and Kankakee Counties (the “Counties”) share a border with the
State of Indiana; and

WHEREAS, the Counties compete with Indiana for economic development, revenue, and employees,
among other resources; and
WHEREAS, differing tax structures between Illinois and Indiana create disparate incentives for some
landowners at the expense of Illinois business; and
WHEREAS, there is little coordination or strategy to determine how to ensure the Counties maintain a
competitive advantage over Indiana; and
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Board of Commissioners of Cook County call on
the Illinois General Assembly to create an Indiana Border Tax and Regional Economic Development
Strategy to encompass Cook, Will, Iroquois and Kankakee Counties through Illinois Senate Bill 3285
or a similar bill; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, the Indiana Border Tax and Regional Economic Development
Strategy shall include a strategy to attract and maintain businesses within the Cook, Will, Iroquois and
Kankakee Counties;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, the Indiana Border Tax and Regional Economic Development
Strategy shall include participants from various affected municipalities and elected officials from Cook,
Will, Iroquois and Kankakee Counties.

A motion was made by Commissioner Boykin, seconded by Commissioner Daley, to accept as amended 182658. The motion carried by the following vote:
