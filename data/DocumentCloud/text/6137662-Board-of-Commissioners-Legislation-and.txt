NOTICE AND AGENDA
There will be a meeting of the Committee or Subcommittee of the Board of Commissioners of Cook County at the
date, time and location listed above to consider the following:
PUBLIC TESTIMONY
Authorization as a public speaker shall only be granted to those individuals who have submitted in writing, their
name, address, subject matter, and organization (if any) to the Secretary 24 hours in advance of the meeting. Duly
authorized public speakers shall be called upon to deliver testimony at a time specified in the meeting agenda.
Authorized public speakers who are not present during the specified time for public testimony will forfeit their
allotted time to speak at the meeting. Public testimony must be germane to a specific item(s) on the meeting
agenda, and the testimony must not exceed three minutes; the Secretary will keep track of the time and advise
when the time for public testimony has expired. Persons authorized to provide public testimony shall not use
vulgar, abusive, or otherwise inappropriate language when addressing the Board; failure to act appropriately;
failure to speak to an item that is germane to the meeting, or failure to adhere to the time requirements may result
in expulsion from the meeting and/or disqualify the person from providing future testimony.

PROPOSED APPOINTMENT
Appointee(s): Velisha L Haddox
Position: Chief of the Bureau of Human Resources
Department/Board/Commission: Bureau of Human Resources
Effective date: Immediate
Expiration date: N/A
Summary: Pursuant to Section 2-141 of the Cook County Code, I hereby appoint Velisha L. Haddox to the
position of Chief of the Bureau of Human Resources.
Ms. Haddox most recently served in various legal capacities in the Office of the Governor of the State of Illinois,
including Deputy General Counsel. Her responsibilities there included advising the Governor on legal matters
and policy decisions; coordinating with State agencies, boards, and commissions regarding labor relations and
other personnel matters; and consulting on labor matters. Prior to joining State government she worked in the
litigation practice at multiple law firms.
Ms. Haddox holds a degree from the Beasly School of Law at Temple University and a Bachelor’s degree from
the University of Michigan.
Velisha Haddox’s experience operating at high levels of State government and her history of collaboration with
government stakeholders will be an asset to Cook County as the Chief of the Bureau of Human Resources. I
submit this communication for your approval.
