Department of Public Works, recommending an award on RQ44292 and enter into a contract with
Honeywell International Inc. dba Honeywell Building Solutions (14-1) in the amount not-to-exceed
$298,700.00 for Jail II fire alarm system testing, inspection and maintenance services at Jail II for the

Funding Source: General Fund – Internal Service Fund
Matt Rymer, Department of Public Works, presented. Trevor McAleer asked approximately how many of

BC2019-703
Department of Public Works,
a) Submitting an RFP exemption on RQ46288, which will result in an award recommendation to PSx, Inc.
in the amount not-to-exceed $193,500.00 for the purchase and installation of a parking access and
revenue control system for the parking facilities located at the Juvenile Justice Center.
b) Recommending an award on RQ46288 to PSx, Inc. in the amount not-to-exceed $193,500.00 for the
purchase and installation of a parking access and revenue control system for the parking facilities located
at the Juvenile Justice Center.
Funding Source: Enterprise Fund - Parking Operations
Michael Chambers, Department of Public Works, presented. Nan Baker asked if this system will connect
with the main server and will not be a stand-alone system; and asked if this is a new contract with PSx,
Inc. Dennis Kennedy motioned to approve the item; Nan Baker seconded. Item BC2019-703 was
unanimously approved.
BC2019-704
Department of Information Technology,
a) Submitting an RFP exemption on RQ46754, which will result in an award recommendation to Dell
Marketing LP in the amount not-to-exceed $32,656.50 for the purchase of (615) Microsoft Exchange P2
licenses.
b) Recommending an award on RQ46754 to Dell Marketing LP in the amount not-to-exceed $32,656.50
for the purchase of (615) Microsoft Exchange P2 licenses.
Funding Source: General Fund
Jeanelle Greene, Department of Information Technology and Holly Woods, Department of Human
Resources, presented. Dale Miller asked how the employees without County workstation computers will
access their email accounts. Trevor McAleer asked if there have been issues with employees not
participating in benefits enrollment; asked if the cost is a one-time or recurring annual charge for licenses;
asked if all County Sheriff’s Corrections Officers have County email addresses and asked for confirmation
as to whether Corrections Officers are issued cell phones; and asked if 615 new licenses will be sufficient.
Nan Baker asked what prompted the purchase of additional licenses at this time and have some
employees been working without email accounts; asked if the majority of the licenses for this purchase

 will be used for Corrections Officers; asked if the timing of this purchase is in alignment with the expiration
of the Enterprise Agreement and asked if the County can expect a price break with the next bulk purchase,
upon expiration of the Enterprise Agreement. Dennis Kennedy motioned to approve the item; Dale Miller
seconded. Item BC2019-704 was unanimously approved.
BC2019-705
Department of Human Resources, recommending an award on RQ44252 and enter into a contract with
WageWorks, Inc. (24-6) in the amount not-to-exceed $180,000.00 for Consolidated Omnibus Budget
Reconciliation Act Administrative (COBRA) services for the period 7/1/2019 – 6/30/2022.
Original Funding Source: Self-Insurance Fund Hospitalization/Self-Insurance Fund and Benefits
Administration Fund
Holly Woods, Department of Human Resources, presented. Nan Baker asked if the department had
anticipated the delay from the incumbent provider in submitting their documentation to get the contract
approved; asked if there were other respondents to the Request for Proposals and if the proposal
submitted by WageWorks, Inc. was the best. Trevor McAleer asked if WageWorks, Inc. submitted the
lowest priced proposal and if the second lowest proposal was considered while negotiations occurred
with the incumbent provider and resulted in the delay. Dennis Kennedy asked if any services have been
provided or paid for from 7/1/2019 to present and asked how the current pricing compares to the prior
contract. Nan Baker asked how services would have been provided from 7/1/2019 if another provider that
was not the incumbent, had been selected. Dennis Kennedy motioned to approve the item; Trevor
McAleer seconded. Item BC2019-705 was unanimously approved, as amended.
