PUBLIC COMMENTS (2 minutes per speaker)
Commissioner Kinloch joined the meeting at 6:08 p.m.
1. Lisa Johnson came to the board about department protocol. She asked why
responsible parties are not held accountable for pulling the necessary permits and is
seeking resolution to an open issue.
2. Mike Shane came before the board with several concerns: water quality; request for
the department to conduct a water analysis and publish for the public; does water
interruptions create stagnation in the service line to cause contamination in homes and
facilities; and does water main breaks cause damage to the interior of the pipes and
lead to pollution.

3. Gerald Carson of 17645 Grandville has come before the board on numerous
occasions regarding his water bill. Director Brown suggested that the case be referred
to the Office of the Magistrate.
4. Willliam Davis came before the board to obtain information on how to dispute the
drainage fee.
5. James Parker came before the board to inquire about the drainage fee on vacant
lots. Mr. Parker was referred to the drainage team.
6. Darrell Stewart came before the board with the following issues: an alley drain
cleaning; an explanation on how sewage is calculated; and how to determine the
sewage usage based on water usage.
7. Tiffani Ashley-Bell of the Human Utility Organization came to inquire about how its
organization can collaborate with the department to help provide funding to citizens for
their water bills.
8. Mr. Pratt was not available during public comment to have his matter addressed.
9. Jennifer Fussbender came to voice her concern about water quality and the lead
issue in the Detroit Public Schools (DPS). She asked how the community can protect
itself from this issue without further increases on water bills. She also asked how can
the department assure that it is safe to drink the water without bearing the burden of
water testing at the customer's expense.
10. Patrice Moore came before the board regarding a water main installation aftermath;
property damage, restoration, and sewer cleaning.
11. Spartacus Habkuscha came before the board to ask how do you justify a drainage
fee on properties where it's not feasible.
