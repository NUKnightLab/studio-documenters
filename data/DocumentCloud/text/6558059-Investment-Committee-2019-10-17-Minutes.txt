Report from Marie Racine
Deputy Chief Investment Officer Compensation

There was an update with respect to whether the issue related to DCIO compensation could impact
certain certifications and reporting requirements imposed on the Investment Committee under the
Plan of Adjustment (“POA”), the State Contribution Agreement and City’s reporting obligations
under the Omnibus Transaction Agreement (“OTA”). The IC certification under the OTA relate
to whether the City has met its obligations under the POA for purposes of receiving funding from
the Foundation for Detroit’s Future. The purpose of this informational discussion was to set forth
the various reporting requirements in order to inform the Board with respect to the DCIO
compensation matter.

Mt Clemens, Michigan
By: Trustee McClellan – Supported By: Trustee Pincheck
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees for the Police and Fire Retirement System for the City of
Detroit (“Board”) is in receipt of an Eligible Domestic Relations Order (“EDRO”) entered on June
11, 2019, which supplements a Judgment of Divorce entered on December 19, 2018, wherein
Alternate Payee VIRGINIA C. COBB is awarded certain rights to the retirement benefits of
Participant MARCUS C. MCCLUNG, an active employee,
WHEREAS, the EDRO provides that the Alternate Payee VIRGINIA C. COBB receive a
monthly benefit from the Retirement Allowance portion of Participant’s Component I benefit in
the amount of 50% of the martial portion of the Participant’s Retirement Allowance, including a
pro-rata share of any guaranteed automatic pension increases, which has accrued as of
December 19, 2018, and
WHEREAS, the marital portion is defined as the increase in accrued benefit from the date of
marriage, October 25, 2012, through the date of divorce, December 19, 2018, and
WHEREAS, the Alternate Payee VIRGINIA C. COBB IS entitled to receive a monthly benefit
from the Retirement Allowance portion of Participant’s Component I benefit in the amount of 50%
of the martial portion of the Participant’s Retirement Allowance, including a pro-rata share of any
guaranteed automatic pension increases, which has accrued as of December 19, 2018, when the
Participant is eligible to receive such benefits and as set forth in the EDRO and SUBJECT TO
ALTERNATE PAYEE FILING AN APPLICATION FOR SAME, and
WHEREAS, the EDRO awards the Alternate Payee VIRGINIA C. COBB a distribution from the
Voluntary Employee Contributions of Participant’s Component I benefit in an amount equal to
50% of the accumulated balance under the plan as of December 19, 2018, including a pro-rata
share of any annual adjustments made after December 19, 2018 and before the date of distribution
to reflect gains or losses allocated to the Participant’s Voluntary Employee Contributions Account,
and
WHEREAS, the Alternate Payee VIRGINIA C. COBB IS entitled to a distribution from the
Voluntary Employee Contributions of Participant’s Component I benefit in an amount equal to
50% of the accumulated balance under the plan as of December 19, 2018, including a pro-rata
share of any annual adjustments made after December 19, 2018 and before the date of distribution
to reflect gains or losses allocated to the Participant’s Voluntary Employee Contributions Account,
when the Participant is eligible to receive such benefits and as set forth in the EDRO and SUBJECT
TO ALTERNATE PAYEE FILING AN APPLICATION FOR SAME, and
WHEREAS, the EDRO awards the Alternate Payee VIRGINIA C. COBB a monthly benefit from
the Pension portion of Participant’s Component II benefit in an amount equal to 50% of the marital
portion of the Participant’s pension, including any pro-rata share of any guaranteed automatic
pension increases, which have accrued as of December 19, 2018, and
