ANNOUNCEMENTS
Chairman Papadimitriu welcomed members of the public and staff.

ROLL CALL
The Clerk of the Board called the roll. All five Board Members answered present.

PUBLIC REMARKS
None.

APPROVAL OF MINUTES
The minutes of the February 14, 2019 Regular Meeting and Brown Bag Lunch were approved by
a vote of 5-0.

 RULEMAKINGS
R19-1

Proposed New 35 Ill. Adm. Code 204, Prevention of Significant
Deterioration, Amendments To 35 Ill. Adm. Code Parts 101, 105,
203, 211, And 215 – The Board granted the People’s motion
under 35 Ill. Adm. Code 101.112(b) to accept the appearance of
Jason E. James in this proceeding.

5-0

ADJUSTED STANDARDS
ADMINISTRATIVE CITATIONS
AC 19-14

County of Jackson v. Phillip Qualls (Land) – The Board
dismissed this administrative citation for lack of jurisdiction for
failure to file the citation with the Board within 10 days after
serving the citation on respondent.

5-0

AC 19-15

IEPA v. Shelby L. and Shannon D. Walsh (Land) – The Board
found that respondents had violated Sections 21(p)(1), (p)(3), and
(p)(7) of the Act (415 ILCS 5/21(p)(1), (p)(3), (p)(7) (2016)).
Because there were 3 violations of Section 21(p), the Board
ordered respondents to pay a civil penalty of $4,500.

5-0

AC 19-17

IEPA v. Harold Poignant, Jr., River Valley Pipe, LLC., and
Ironhustler Excavating, Inc.(Land) – The Board found that
respondents had violated Sections 21(p)(1) and (p)(7) of the Act
(415 ILCS 5/21(p)(1), (p)(7) (2016)). Because there were 2
violations of Section 21(p), the Board ordered respondents to pay
a civil penalty of $3,000.

5-0

ADJUDICATORY CASES
PCB 15-157

Gary Polchow v. Village of Rankin (Water, Air – Enforcement,
Citizens) – The Board found that complainant failed to meet his
burden to prove that the Village violated Section 9(a) or 9(c) of
the Act (415 ILCS 5/9(a), (c) (2016)), and dismissed this
enforcement action.

5-0

PCB 19-70

Susanne M. Kirkham and Steven C. Kirkham v. Village of Poplar
Grove (Water – Enforcement, Citizens) – Finding the alleged
violations frivolous, the Board granted respondent’s motion to
dismiss the complaint. The Board allowed complainants to file
on or before March 29, 2019 an amended complaint curing the
deficiencies specified in the order.

5-0

2

 PCB 19-78

Sierra Club v. Illinois Power Generating Company, Illinois Power
Resources Generating, LLC, Electric Energy, Inc., and Vistra
Energy Corporation (Water – Enforcement, Citizens) – The
Board found that the complaint is neither duplicative nor
frivolous and accepted it for hearing. The Board granted
respondents’ unopposed motion for an extension of time to April
15, 2019 file their answer to the complaint.

5-0

PCB 19-87

James C. Niebrugge Marital Share Two Trust v. IEPA (UST
Appeal) – The Board accepted for hearing this underground
storage tank appeal involving a site in Wayne County.

5-0

PCB 19-88

Ron Mueller, Jr. v. IEPA (Water – Tax Certification) – The
Board found and certified that specified facilities of Ron
Mueller, Jr. located in Ford County are pollution control facilities
for the purpose of preferential tax treatment under the Property
Tax Code (35 ILCS 200/11-10 (2016)).

5-0

PCB 19-89

Derek Menold v. IEPA (Water – Tax Certification) – The Board
found and certified that specified facilities of Derek Menold
located in Stark County are pollution control facilities for the
purpose of preferential tax treatment under the Property Tax Code
(35 ILCS 200/11-10 (2016)).

5-0

OTHER ITEMS
