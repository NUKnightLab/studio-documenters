Re: Melissa Anne Mayo v. David Todd Mayo –

(Con’t)

WHEREAS, the EDRO is directed to the City of Detroit Police and Fire Retirement
System and references the City of Detroit Police and Fire Retirement System
throughout, including a reference to the Police and Fire Retirement System of the City
of Detroit Act, and
WHEREAS, the Participant is a member of the General Retirement System of the City
of Detroit and is not a member of the City of Detroit Police and Fire Retirement, and
WHEREAS, the EDRO also refers to the Retirement System’s former address and does
not reference the Retirement System’s current address, and
WHEREAS, Section 6 D. of the EDRO, titled “Death of the Participant after the
Alternate Payee’s Benefit Commencement Date,” states that the “Alternate Payee shall
continue to receive the Alternate Payee’s benefit regardless of the timing of the death of
the Participant,” and
WHEREAS, Section 8 G. of the EDRO, titled “Actuarial Costs,” the Order incorrectly
states that the actuarial fees are $400.00, and
WHEREAS, said matter having been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that
the terms of said EDRO are inconsistent with the provisions of the Retirement System
and applicable law, and
WHEREAS, the EDRO does not meet the requirements of the Retirement System and
applicable law; THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED, that the Board acknowledges receipt of said court order, rejects said court
order as inconsistent with Plan provisions and applicable law, and hereby indicates that
it will not pay pension benefits in accordance with the terms of said order until such time
as an acceptable order is presented to the Retirement System, and further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this resolution be immediately attached as the top sheet of
the Participant’s pension file and other appropriate records be kept for the Retirement
System relative to this matter, and further
RESOLVED, that copies of this resolution be sent to Jeffrey J. Franklin, Esq. for the
Participant, Dana L. Vandrew, Esq., attorney for the Alternate Payee and Melissa Anne
Mayo, the Alternate Payee.
Yeas: Anthony, Cetlinski, Cowan, Johnson, Naglick, Nickleberry, Sheehan, Szymanski - 8
Nays: None

Re: James W. Jackson v. Patricia Cheney-Jackson
Wayne County Circuit Court Case No. 14-103819-DO
Resolution Moved By: Trustee Szymanski – Supported By: Trustee Cetlinski
WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees is in receipt of an Eligible Domestic Relations Order
(“EDRO”), dated October 2, 2015, wherein Patricia Cheney-Jackson, the Alternate
Payee, is awarded certain rights to the retirement allowance of James W. Jackson, the
Participant, and
WHEREAS, the Retirement System consists of both a defined benefit plan and a
defined contribution (a/k/a the Annuity Savings Fund) plan, and
WHEREAS, pursuant to the EDRO, the Alternate Payee is entitled to a portion of both
the Participant’s defined benefit plan benefits from the Retirement System and a portion
of the Participant’s Annuity Savings Fund balance, and
WHEREAS, the payments from the plan to the Alternate Payee shall begin when the
Participant commences benefits from the Retirement System, or upon the Alternate
Payee’s election no time before the Participant’s earliest retirement eligibility, and
WHEREAS, the Board’s policy is to require that the cost for the actuary’s calculations
are to be borne by the parties to the domestic relations proceedings and the EDRO
states that the Alternate Payee and the Participant shall equally share the additional
actuarial costs, and
WHEREAS, said matter had been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that the
applicable terms of said court order are consistent with the provisions of the Retirement
System and applicable law including Public Act 46 of 1991 (MCLA 38.1701) as
amended; THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED, that the Board acknowledges receipt of said court order, will pay pension
benefits consistent with said order subject to an application by the Participant or the
Alternate Payee, and further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this resolution be immediately attached as the top sheet of
the pension file and other appropriate records be kept for the Retirement System
relative to this matter, and further
RESOLVED, that copies of this resolution be sent to Patricia Cheney-Jackson, the
Alternate Payee; Lavonne Bannister-Jackson, Esq., attorney for the Alternate Payee;
James W. Jackson, the Participant; Roderick K. Green, Esq., attorney for the
Participant; and the Board’s Actuary.
