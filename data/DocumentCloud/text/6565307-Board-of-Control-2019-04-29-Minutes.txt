BC2019-323
Department of Public Works, recommending an award on RQ44473 and enter into a contract with
Advance Door Company (12-1) in the amount not-to-exceed $300,000.00 for overhead garage door
maintenance services for various locations for the period 4/29/2019 – 4/28/2022.
Funding Source: General Fund – Internal Services Fund
Thomas Pavich, Department of Public Works, presented. Dale Miller asked why there was only one
response to the bid. Lenora Lockett stated, that despite attempts to contact vendors who received the
bid package, there was no feedback from vendors who elected to not bid. Mr. Miller asked if the pricing
submitted by the recommended vendor is reasonable, as there is no basis for comparison. Trevor McAleer
asked if this is a new contract and if Advance Door Company was the prior vendor. Nan Baker asked if the
existing garage doors are new and are covered under warranty. Mr. McAleer asked for clarification if

 services include repairs and maintenance, as needed, in addition to door inspections. Matt Carroll
motioned to approve the item; Trevor McAleer seconded. Item BC2019-323 was unanimously approved.
BC2019-324
Department of Public Works,
a) Submitting an RFP exemption on RQ45205, which will result in an award recommendation to Belenky,
Inc. in the amount not-to-exceed $101,907.00 for the purchase of (2) UniMac 180lb and (1) UniMac 125lb
Capacity Washer Extractor Machines to be used for inmate laundry in Jail II.
b) Recommending an award on RQ45205 to Belenky, Inc. in the amount not-to-exceed $101,907.00 for
the purchase of (2) 180lb and (1) 125lb Capacity Washer Extractor Machines to be used for inmate laundry
in Jail II.
Funding source: Internal Service Fund
Matt Rymer, Department of Public Works, presented. Dale Miller asked how many responses to the
informal bid were received and why did the two vendors that were contacted during the market research
phase, not respond to the informal bid. Trevor McAleer asked how the pricing from the recommended
vendor compares to the pricing from the two vendors contacted during the market research phase and
asked whether the warranties were included in the evaluation and compared to the unofficial quotes;
asked if there is currently an annual maintenance services contract with Belenky, Inc. and commented
that he was surprised that the two vendors initially contacted did not bid. Nan Baker asked for clarification
on the location of where the washing machines will be located. Mr. Rymer clarified that the General Fund
– Internal Service Fund as listed on the agenda is a temporary funding source until the Capital Project
Fund is set up by the Office of Budget and Management. Matt Carroll motioned to approve the item;
Michael Dever seconded. Item BC2019-324 was unanimously approved.

BC2019-334
Department of Health and Human Services/Division of Children and Family Services, submitting an
amendment to Contract No. CE1600142 with Community of Hope for group mentoring services,
previously referred to as the Open Table Model, for transition age young adults who were previously or
are currently in foster care for the period 7/1/2016 – 6/30/2019 to extend the time period to 6/30/2020
and for additional funds in the amount not-to-exceed $150,000.00.
Funding Source: 30% Title IV-E Admin; 70% HHS Levy
Robert Math, Cuyahoga Job and Family Services on behalf of the Division of Children and Family Services,
presented. Dale Miller asked if program data related to the Open Table Model could be provided to the
Board members. Trevor McAleer asked if the original contract was a sole source purchase and stated that
there may be value in conducting a Request for Proposals, if this is no longer a sole source service, due to
the number or prior amendments approved and the total dollar value for these services. Matt Carroll
asked if there was a sense that there may be potential vendors to provide these services. Mr. McAleer
expressed his concern that this is no longer a sole source service and that pricing has doubled over the
last two years and believes that there needs to be a process to compare pricing for services; and asked if
there is a reason this service could not be bid. Mr. Miller asked why the cost has doubled for services.
Matt Carroll recommended that this item be held for further discussion and receipt of additional
information. The board members concurred with Mr. Carroll’s recommendation to hold the item.
The following item was amended to change the AREA number listed in the funding source from AREA 5 to
AREA 1.
