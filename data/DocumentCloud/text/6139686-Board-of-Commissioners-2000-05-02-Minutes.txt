requesting authorization for the Purchasing Age nt to enter into a contract with Yerio Chicago, Chicago,

Reason:

about their own, and neighboring properties. Any break in this service could create a
problem for taxpayers who rely on the Assessor' s web s ite to find critical information for
filing appeals on their assessments.

communication setting forth its fi ndings and recommendations following public hearings held thereon,
and recommended that said application be granted subject to conditions as set forth in the findi ngs.

Commissioner Hansen, seconded by Commissioner Butler, moved to deny the recommendation of
the Zoning Board of Appeals on the above Item 230928 for the following reasons:
I.

The proposed use (storage of towed junk, disabled, or abandoned cars) would be injurious to the
enjoyment of surrounding property because of visual clutter of the cars, and the open yard
fac ility, lacking on-site human supervision and maintenance, wou ld create the appearance of an
abandoned business.

2.

The proposed use wou ld impede the normal and orderly development and improvement of the
surroundin g property, which is envisioned as suitable for commercial businesses such as hotels or
office parks.

3.

The proposed use does not conform to app licable Cook County regulations for a C-4 General
Commercial District. The C-4 al lows accessory parking lots as a permitted use and commercial
parking lots as a Special Use, but open storage yards and junk yards are specifically prohibited.

4.

The proposed use would be incompatible with the Comprehensive Plan of the Village of Roselle .

T he motion carried unanimously.

OO-R-271
RESOLUTION
Sponsored by
THE HONORABLE JOHN H. STROGER, JR., PRESIDENT AND CARL R. HANSEN
COUNTY COMMISSIONERS

WHEREAS, there exists a national debate on the issue of whether and how to collect sales and use taxes
on remote sales transactions such as those which occur over the Internet, telephone or via mail; and
WHEREAS, out-of-state merchants who conduct sales via the Internet, mail order and telephone are not
currently required by law to collect sales and use taxes imposed by state and local governments, and
WHEREAS, Congress enacted a moratorium on the imposition of sales and use taxes on interstate sales
over the Internet and established an Advisory Commission on Electronic Commerce ("the Commission")
charged with making recommendations to Congress on federal, state, local, and international taxation and
tariffs on transactions using the Internet and Internet Access; and
WHEREAS, the Commission 's final report was submitted to Congress on April 12, 2000. Of particular
note are the recommendations to Congress proposed by a majority of the members of the Commission,
