MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
April 30, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Wednesday, April 30, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois. The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 10:29 A.M.
Present were Chairman Aaron Jaffe, Members Lee Gould, Maribeth Vander Weele and Michael
Holewinski.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES –
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of March 27, 2014. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
The Administrator stated that there are over 4,184 “live locations”. There are over 16,642
machines in these locations. Mr. Ostrowski also stated that the Board issued fines against
locations engaging in illegal gambling. In the future if this problem persists, the Board will take
more progressive actions which may include revocations of licenses.
PUBLIC COMMENTARY Illinois Association of Licensed Terminal Operators –
Tommie Wofford President of the Illinois Association of Licensed Terminal Operators
introduced Mr. Derek Harmer, Secretary of the Illinois Association of Licensed Terminal
Operators. Mr. Harmer went on to brief the Board and staff referencing the unauthorized
taxation of video-gaming terminal operators doing business in Elmwood Park. A lawsuit that
was filed on behalf of the terminal operators against the Village of Elmwood Park, Illinois.
BOARD POLICY ITEM –
• Proposed Amendments to Video Gaming Rules: Section 250
Member Holewinski moved to amend section 250 of the Board’s rules on video
gaming regarding Duties of Licensed Video Terminal Operators by authorizing staff
to submit a proposal for First Notice Filing with the Secretary of State upon final
review and approval by the Administrator. Member Vander Weele seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

 SUPPLIER, DISTRIBUTOR AND MANUFACTURER LICENSEE ITEMS –

• Safe Pay Services, LLC: Supplier License Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved to renew the Supplier
License of Safe Pay Services, LLC, effective April 30, 2014, for a term of one year,
expiring in April 2015. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the
motion unanimously by roll call vote.

ADMINISTRATIVE REQUESTS FOR HEARING/REQUESTS TO REAPPLY –
 Requests for Hearing
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved to deny the requests for
hearing from J.J. Schiro, Inc., Gaming Management (Illinois) LLC, B.C.T.I Inc.,
and Keith Eric Myrick as they have failed to establish a prima facie case. Member
Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call
vote.
 Request to Reapply
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved to approve the request to
reapply from Streator Post Number 1492, VFW. Member Gould seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
PROPOSED COMPLAINTS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS –
 Marlin Palmer – Terminal Handler
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board issue a
Disciplinary Complaint against Marlin Palmer for his failure to disclose all material
changes in information to the Board and that the Board revoke his license, said
action to take effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaint, unless he files
an Answer to the Complaint or pays a $250 fine within that time period. Member
Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.
