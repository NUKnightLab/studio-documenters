(“Board”) was held on the 3rd floor at 160 N. LaSalle, Chicago, Illinois.

present: Members Maribeth Vander Weele, Michael Holewinski and Lee Gould. Chairman
Jaffe participated via telephone. At 10:00 A.M. on Thursday, March 15, 2012, Chairman Jaffe
called the meeting to order. Pursuant to Section 2(c), paragraphs (1), (14) and (21) of the Open
Meetings Act and Section 6(d) of the Riverboat gambling Act, Member Gould moved that the
Board retire to Closed Session to discuss the items listed under Closed Session on today’s
agenda and relating to the following subject matters:

MGM Resorts International – Subsidiary Guarantor
Based on staff’s investigation and recommendation, and pursuant to Section 230(d) of the
Board’s Adopted Rules, Member Gould moved to approve MGM Resorts
International’s request for approval of Nevada Landing Partnership as a Subsidiary
Guarantor of the principal amount of $850 million of MGM Resorts International’s
8.65% Senior Notes due in 2019, which were sold by MGM Resorts International in
January 2012 to qualified institutional buyers. Member Vander Weele seconded the
motion. Chairman Jaffe opposed the motion. Member Holewinski was in favor of the
motion. The motion passed 3 to 1.

DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
●

Steven W. Donaho, Kiel C. Francis, Christi E. Herrera, Raquel Rodriguez –
Occupational Licensees
Based on staff’s investigation and recommendation, Member Vander Weele moved that
the Board issue Disciplinary Complaints against Steven W. Donaho, Kiel C.
Francis, Christi E. Herrera, and Raquel Rodriguez for failing to disclose all
material changes in information to the Board and that the Board revoke the
occupational licenses of these individuals, said actions to take effect twenty-one (21)
days from the dates of service of the complaints unless the Licensees file Answers or
pay fines within that time period. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

●

Nora Almanza – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation and recommendation, Member Gould moved that the
Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint against Nora Almanza due to her failure to
disclose all material changes in information to the Board and her failure to comply
with an Order of the Board. Further, I move that the Board revoke Nora
Almanza’s Occupational License, said action to take effect twenty-one (21) days
from the date of service of the complaint unless the Licensee files an Answer within
that time period. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the
motion unanimously by roll call vote.

 ●

Patricia A. Dolnak – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation and recommendation, Member Vander Weele moved that
the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint against Patricia A. Dolnak due to her
failure to comply with an Order of the Board and her termination from the
Hollywood Casino in Joliet for theft. Further, I move that the Board revoke
Patricia Dolnak’s Occupational License, said action to take effect twenty-one (21)
days from the date of service of the complaint unless the Licensee files an Answer
within that time period. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the
motion unanimously by roll call vote.

●

Bruce E. Zielke – Occupational Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation and recommendation, Member Gould moved that the
Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint against Bruce E. Zielke due to his admitted
theft from Hollywood Casino in Joliet, Illinois. Further, I move that the Board
revoke Bruce E. Zielke’s Occupational License, said action to take effect twenty-one
(21) days from the date of service of the complaint unless the Licensee files an
Answer within that time period. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The
Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

●

Midwest Gaming & Entertainment, LLC d/b/a Rivers Casino – Owner Licensee
Based on staff’s investigation and recommendation, Member Vander Weele moved that
the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint against Midwest Gaming &
Entertainment, LLC d/b/a Rivers Casino, an Owner Licensee, for violations of the
Boards Rules regarding the Self Exclusion Program. Further, I move that the
Board fine Midwest Gaming & Entertainment, LLC d/b/a Rivers Casino $25,000.00,
said action to take effect twenty-one (21) days from the date of service of the
complaint unless the Licensee files an Answer within that time period. Member
Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call
vote.
