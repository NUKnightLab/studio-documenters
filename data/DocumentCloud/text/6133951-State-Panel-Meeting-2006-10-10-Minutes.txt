IV.

ADMINISTRATIVE MATTERS

Chairman Gallagher asked Executive Director Brosnan to present the State Panel administrative actions
for the period of time from September 1 through September 30, 2006. He distributed the report to the
Board for review, and the Board examined it. The Executive Director summarized the report's highlights,
noting that during the month, the agency issued one certification pursuant to a representation election,
eight certifications pursuant to majority interest petitions, one certification of voluntary recognition, one
certification pursuant to a unit clarification petition, and one certification pursuant to a directed unit
clarification. The agency also received withdrawals in connection with four representation petitions, and
the agency's Administrative Law Judges (ALJs) issued Recommended Decisions and Orders (RDOs) in
two representation cases. The Executive Director also noted that with regard to unfair labor practice
cases, the agency issued ten complaints for hearing and one administrative dismissal, had twenty-four
charges withdrawn, and the agency's ALJs issued RDOs in two unfair labor practice cases.
V.

BOARD ACTIONS
S-RC-06-042
1.
State of Illinois, Department of Central Management Services/American Federation of
State, County and Municipal Employees

General Counsel Zimmerman began by summarizing the case, noting that the issue presented concerned
the supervisory status of the employees in the title of Public Aid Quality Control Supervisor. The ALJ
determined the title was not supervisory within the meaning of the Illinois Public Labor Relations Act
(Act), and General Counsel Zimmerman recommended that the Board uphold the ALJ's determination.
Member Hade moved to accept the General Counsel's recommendation. Member Coli seconded the
motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
2.
S-RC-07-032
State of Illinois, Department of Central Management Services/American Federation of
State, County and Municipal Employees
General Counsel Zimmerman began by summarizing the case, noting that the ALJ dismissed the petition
on the basis that Petitioner, American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees (AFSCME),
failed to respond to the ALJ's order directing it to show cause why the petition should not be dismissed as

 seeking an inappropriate bargaining unit. General Counsel Zimmerman recommended that the Board
consider the case on its merits and uphold the ALJ's determination. Member Hade moved to accept the
General Counsel's recommendation. Member Coli seconded the motion, and the Board passed it
unanimously, by a voice vote.

6.
S-CA-05-085
Downers Grove Professional Firefighters, IAFF Local 3234/Village of Downers Grove
General Counsel Zimmerman began by summarizing the case, noting that the Executive Director issued a
Dismissal on August 29, 2006, with an appeal filed. The General Counsel explained that the case
concerns the Fire Fighter Promotion Act and the Respondent Village's obligation to bargain, and noted
that the Executive Director issued a complaint for hearing on the other issues raised by the charge. The
General Counsel recommended that this portion of the charge be remanded for the issuance of a
complaint and likewise set for hearing. Member Hade moved to accept the General Counsel's
recommendation. Member Piper seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice
vote.
