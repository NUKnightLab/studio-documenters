MINUTES OF THE MEETING
COMMISSION ON CHICAGO LANDMARKS
September 6, 2018
The Commission on Chicago Landmarks held its regularly scheduled meeting on
September 6, 2018. The meeting was held at City Hall, 121 North LaSalle Street, Room 201-A,
Chicago, Illinois. The meeting began at 12:45 p.m.
PHYSICALLY PRESENT:
Rafael Leon, Chairman
Jim Houlihan, Vice Chairman
David Reifman, Deputy Commissioner, Department of Planning and Development
Gabriel Dziekiewicz
Carmen Rossi
Richard Tolliver
ABSENT:
Juan Moreno
Mary Ann Smith
Ernest Wong
ALSO PHYSICALLY PRESENT:
Dijana Cuvalo, Architect IV, Department of Planning and Development
Members of the Public
(The list of those in attendance is on file at the Commission office.)
A recording of this meeting is on file at the Historic Preservation Division offices of the
Department of Planning and Development and is part of the public record of the regular meeting
of the Commission on Chicago Landmarks.
Chairman Leon called the meeting to order.
1. Approval of the Minutes of Previous Meeting
Regular Meeting of August 2, 2018
Motioned by Tolliver, seconded by Rossi. Approved unanimously (6-0).
2. Final Landmark Recommendation
Lisa Misher, Department of Law, joined the meeting.
WEST SIDE YMCA/YWCA COMPLEX
1513-1539 West Monroe Street and 101-109 South Ashland Avenue

WARD 28

Cindy Roubik presented the landmark recommendation report. Motion to adopt the final
landmark recommendation for the West Side YMCA/YWCA Complex.

 Motioned by Dziekiewicz, seconded by Tolliver. Approved unanimously (6-0).
3. Preliminary Landmark Recommendation
MUNICIPAL TUBERCULOSIS SANITARIUM COMPLEX
WARD 39
Area bounded by Pulaski Road, Peterson Avenue, Central Park Avenue, and Bryn
Mawr Avenue
Matt Crawford presented the report. Motion to adopt the preliminary landmark
recommendation to City Council for the Municipal Tuberculosis Sanitarium Complex.
Motioned by Dziekiewicz, seconded by Houlihan. Approved unanimously (6-0).
4. Report from the Department of Planning and Development
COOK COUNTY HOSPITAL ADMINISTRATION BUILDING
1835 West Harrison Street

WARD 27

Commissioner Reifman presented the report. Vote to accept the DPD report and take the
next step in the designation process for the Cook County Hospital Administration Building to
request consent from the property owners.
Motioned by Tolliver, seconded by Dziekiewicz. Approved unanimously (6-0).
5. Citywide Adopt-a-Landmark Fund - Application
11127-11129 South Langley Avenue & 704-706 East 112th Street
PULLMAN DISTRICT

WARD 9

Kandalyn Hahn presented the report. Vote to approve the resolution approving a Citywide
Adopt-a-Landmark Fund grant to 11127-11129 South Langley Avenue & 704-706 East 112th
Street.
Motioned by Rossi, seconded by Houlihan. Approved unanimously (6-0).
6. Permit Review Committee Reports
Report on Projects Reviewed at the August 2, 2018, Permit Review Committee Meeting
Commissioner Houlihan presented the report from the Permit Review Committee meeting of
August 2, 2018 (see attached).
Report on Permit Decisions by the Commission Staff for the Month of August 2018
Lawrence Shure presented the staff report for the month of August 2018 (see attached).
7. Announcements
Chairman Leon announced:

 The meeting of the Program Committee for review of suggestions received from the public
for Chicago landmark designation and nominations to the National Register of Historic
Places will be held:
Date:
Time:
Location:

Wednesday, September 26, 2018
10:00 a.m.
City Hall, 121 North LaSalle Street, Room 201-A

Deadline for submissions is Tuesday, September 18, 2018. All suggestions must be
submitted on a form available on the Chicago Landmarks website or from the Historic
Preservation office.
The meeting is open to the public and all are welcome.
8. Adjournment
There being no further business, the meeting was adjourned at 2:05 p.m.
Motioned by Houlihan, seconded by Dziekiewicz. Approved unanimously (6-0).

David Reifman, Secretary

1. 3256 and 3258 S. Prairie
4th Ward
Calumet-Giles-Prairie District
Proposed construction of two new, raised, two-story, masonry, detached, singlefamily homes.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following condition:
1.

The project as proposed would require a zoning variation and/or
adjustment, and the Commission takes no position regarding any
requested variance/adjustment relative to the zoning code
requirements.

2. 2245 S. Michigan
3rd Ward
Motor Row District
Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation including new storefront on the front
façade and insertion of new windows and doors on the north façade.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. As proposed, the new elevator will not extend above the existing
front parapet;
2. As proposed, the storefront shall have a dark factory-applied finish
and all glass shall be clear vision glass; and,
3. All windows and storefront elevations, sections, and details shall be
submitted with the permit plans for staff review and approval.
