Regular Meeting, Open Session Minutes - Video
Illinois Gaming Board
Chicago, Illinois
September 21, 2017
The Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”) convened for a Regular Meeting - Video on September 20,
2017. The following Board members were present in the Illinois Gaming Board conference
room: Chairman Don Tracy, Members Steve Dolins, Thomas Dunn, Hector Alejandre and Dee
Robinson.
The following Staff members were also in attendance: Administrator Mark Ostrowski, General
Counsel Agostino Lorenzini, Deputy General Counsel James Pellum, Deputy AdministratorInvestigations Scott Deubel, Deputy General Counsel Paul Prezioso, Legal Counsel Sheera
Hobfoll, Deputy Administrator-Audit and Financial Analysis Jim Lopinski, Office Manager and
Acting Secretary to the Board Lucy Bautista, Senior Project Manager Mark Stevens, Deputy
Administrator-Enforcement Frank Spizzirri.
At 1:30 PM, the meeting was called to order. Member Robinson moved that the Board retire to
closed session pursuant to Section 2(c), paragraphs (1), (11), (14), (15) and (21) of the Open
Meetings Act and Section 6(d) of the Riverboat Gambling Act to discuss the items listed on the
closed session agenda relating to personnel, investigations concerning applicants and licensees,
litigation and hearing requests, staff recommendations and closed session minutes. Member
Alejandre seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by voice vote.
At 2:15 PM, Member Dunn moved to adjourn the closed session and return to open session the
next day, Thursday, September 21, 2017, after the 9am Riverboat meeting. Member Dolins
seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by voice vote.
On Thursday, September 21, 2017, the Board reconvened the Regular Meeting – Video of the
Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”) on the 5th floor of the Michael Bilandic Building in Chicago,
Illinois immediately following its Regular Meeting - Riverboat. Present were the following:
Chairman Don Tracy, Members Hector Alejandre, Thomas Dunn and Dee Robinson.

Failure to Cooperate with Board Agents
Member Robinson moved that the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint fining establishment
licensee NORTH MAIN BAR, INC. d/b/a NORTH MAIN BAR, $1,000 for the improper
conduct of its PSIC Jorge Romero, said action to take effect 21 days from the date of service of
the Complaint, unless an Answer is properly filed within that timeframe. Member Dunn
seconded the motion. The motion passed unanimously by roll call vote.
Member Robinson moved that the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint revoking the Terminal
Handler license of Martez Frank Burden for failing to cooperate with the Board's agents, said
actions to take effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaints, unless an Answer is
properly filed within that timeframe. Member Dunn seconded the motion. The motion passed
unanimously by roll call vote.
Failure to Properly Monitor VGTs
Member Alejandre moved that the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint revoking the
establishment license of Kartar Truck Stop, LLC d/b/a Woodhull Travel Plaza, for failing to
properly monitor its video gaming terminals, this action to take effect 21 days from the date of
service of the Complaint, unless a $5,000 fine is paid or an Answer is properly filed within that
timeframe. Member Robinson seconded the motion. The motion passed unanimously by roll call
vote.
