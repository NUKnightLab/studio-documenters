The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:
1. 20 S. Wells
42nd Ward
Quincy CTA Station – Proposed Landmark
Proposed accessibility upgrades to the Quincy Station, including construction of two
new elevators and associated walkways.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. As proposed, an ornamental ceiling treatment for the mezzanine spaces shall
be installed to match the historic character of the original pressed metal
ceiling as closely as possible. Historic Preservation staff shall review for
approval final products and installation details prior to order and installation;
2. The wood-grained porcelain floor tiles shall be installed with narrow joints
and grout to match the color of the tile; and,
3. Historic Preservation staff shall be notified to view and approve material
samples, colors and finishes prior to order and installation.
2. 317 W. Wisconsin
43rd Ward
Old Town Triangle District
Proposed removal and replacement of the existing L-shaped masonry front stair
structure and metal fence in the front yard.
Action:

Approved 2-0-1 (with Commissioner Dziekiewicz abstaining) with the
following conditions:

1. The existing non-historic L-shaped masonry stair may not be rebuilt as
proposed. The new stair should be straight-run, consistent with the historic
configuration, and as shown in drawings titled Alternate A or B on Sheet ASK
dated 11/4/15. The stair shall have closed risers with decorative newel posts
and pipe railings with vertical pickets to meet code requirements; and,
2. The new front fence, proposed to be at 7’-3”-tall, should be lowered in height
to match the adjacent metal fences.

 3. 800 N. Clark
2nd Ward
Bush Temple of Music
Continued Review: Proposed new rooftop deck and elevator and stair enclosures atop
the existing 6-story building as part of the rehabilitation of the property for residential
use.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The location, scale, size, and design of the proposed rooftop deck and elevator
and stair additions are approved as shown on plans dated October 16, 2015.
As proposed, the new brick shall match existing and a sample of proposed
brick shall be reviewed for approval by the Historic Preservation staff;
2. The glass for the rooftop deck railing shall be clear glass and the color of the
corrugated metal cladding shall be consistent with the color of the common
brick and a sample should be reviewed for approval by Historic Preservation
staff prior to order and installation;
3. As proposed, new architectural shingle roofing shall be installed on the
building. The color of the roofing shall be based on the color of original slate
roofing if any remains. Should no physical evidence of original slate roofing
be found, the roofing color shall be lighter tone than existing roofing and the
final selection shall be reviewed for approval by Historic Preservation staff
prior to order and installation;
4. The project requires an adjustment, and the Commission takes no position
regarding any requested adjustment relative to the zoning code requirements;
and,
5. The permit approval is conditioned on the applicant submitting to Historic
Preservation staff a proposal and analysis for adding decorative roofing
cresting, finials, masonry finials and pediments using substitute materials to
match the historic configuration as much as possible prior to roof work
commencing.

4. 4858 S. Kenwood (The Blossom House)
Kenwood District

4th Ward

Informational Only: Salvage and reinstallation of existing wood siding with
replacement as needed of deteriorated siding, sheathing and building paper.
