Ladies and Gentlemen:
Your Committee on Workforce, Job Development and Training Opportunities of the Board
of Commissioners of Cook County met pursuant to notice on Wednesday, December 1, 2010 at
the hour of 9:00 A.M. in the Board Room, Room 569, County Building, 118 North Clark Street,
Chicago, Illinois.
Your Committee has considered the following items and upon adoption of this report, the
recommendations are as follows:
309791

PRESIDENT'S OFFICE OF EMPLOYMENT TRAINING (P.O.E.T.) by Arthur L.
Turner, Director, transmitting a Communication, dated November 10, 2010:
requesting authorization to renew a grant in the amount of $13,197,785.00 from the
Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity (IDCEO), Springfield,
Illinois, for Workforce Investment Act (WIA) Title 1 services.

309792

PRESIDENT’S OFFICE OF EMPLOYMENT TRAINING (P.O.E.T.) by Arthur L.
Turner, Director, transmitting a Communication, dated November 10, 2010:
requesting authorization to accept additional funds in the amount of $1,541,343.00
from the Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity (IDCEO),
Springfield, Illinois, for the American Recovery Act of 2009, Workforce Investment
Act (WIA).

Commissioner Sims, seconded by Commissioner Suffredin, moved to defer
Communication Nos. 309791 and 309792 to the board meeting of December 14, 2010.
The motion carried.

Chairman Collins stated job training and development should be a top priority for Cook County.
The Workforce, Job Development and Training Opportunities Committee should be a committee
of the whole, and it should be a goal to establish a working subcommittee to work in
collaboration with Director Arthur Turner of P.O.E.T.
Commissioner Sims inquired about the four benchmarks the State of Illinois required from the
Cook County’s, President’s Office of Employment Training Department.
Arthur L. Turner, Director, President’s Office of Employment Training stated the benchmarks are
in place.
Commissioner Gorman asked in deferring the items would the Cook County Board jeopardize
the grant funding.
Mr. Turner stated no.
Commissioner Daley inquired as to whether a performance audit is prepared on the vendors.
Chairman Collins stated audits were performed for fiscal and program performance.
Chairman Collins asked the Secretary to the Board to call upon the registered public speakers,
in accordance with Cook County Code, Sec. 2-108(dd):

Commissioner Suffredin, seconded by Commissioner Gorman, moved to adjourn the
meeting. The motion carried and the meeting was adjourned.
