FABF Trustee Tom Cody’s Retirement
On behalf of the entire Board of Trustees, I would like to warmly congratulate Battalion Chief
Tom Cody on his retirement from the Chicago Fire Department and thank him for his service
and leadership during his tenure as a Trustee of the FABF.
Please find the resolution below, that was passed by the FABF Board of the Trustees at the
December 19, 2018 meeting, honoring Battalion Chief Tom Cody’s contributions to the Fund.

THE FIREMEN’S ANNUITY AND BENEFIT FUND
OF CHICAGO RESOLUTION HONORING THOMAS CODY
WHEREAS, with heavy hearts at having to say goodbye, tempered by gratitude for the
dedicated and generous public service of a valued colleague, the Firemen’s Annuity and
Benefit Fund of Chicago (“Fund”), its Board of Trustees (“Trustees”) and Staff wish to thank
Thomas Cody for his service as a Trustee of the Fund; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Cody entered the Chicago Fire Department in November, 1988, where
ultimately, he advanced through the ranks to his current rank of Battalion Chief; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Cody was elected as an Active Firefighter Trustee to the Firemen’s
Annuity and Benefit Fund of Chicago in December, 2017; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Cody, as a trustee of the Fund served faithfully and diligently in many
capacities, including serving on the Fund’s Legal Committee, the Fund’s Investment
Committee and the Fund’s Budget Committee, where he served as Chairman, where he
faithfully executed his duties as a trustee and a leader of the Fund; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Cody, throughout his tenure as a trustee of the Fund, dedicated his focus
and efforts to implementing Fund policies and procedures to enable the Board to effectively
and efficiently administer the Fund on behalf of Fund participants and beneficiaries; and

 WHEREAS, while we will miss our friend and colleague, we take comfort in knowing that
Thomas Cody and his family will enjoy happiness and success in his future endeavors; now
therefore,
BE IT RESOLVED, on this 19th day of December, 2018, the Trustees, Staff and all Active and
Annuitant members of the Firemen’s Annuity and Benefit Fund of Chicago wish to honor and
thank Thomas Cody for his unwavering dedication to the Fund and its participants. We will
miss his knowledge, his professionalism and his uncompromising dedication to the Fund and its
participants.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, in order that all may know of the esteem and honor in which
this Fund, its Trustees, Staff and participants hold Thomas Cody, that this Resolution be
entered on the permanent record of this Fund and that a copy of this Resolution be suitably
engraved and presented to Thomas Cody as a token of our respect and gratitude for his valued
service to the Fund, and its Trustees, Staff and Participants.

Fund Secretary,

Captain Anthony Martin
