INVESTMENTS REPORT
The Investments Report was taken out of order.
Market Tracker Report
Mr. Obed provided a summary market update.
June 30, 2019 Preliminary Performance Report
Mr. Obed reviewed the Fund’s June 30, 2019 Preliminary Performance Report and gave an overview of
some of the Fund’s investment strategies.
Private Equity Update
Mr. Wesner provided an overview of the private equity (PE) market. Mr. Wesner informed the Trustees
that the PE market continues to provide a relatively attractive investment opportunity for institutional
investors and that, in order to adhere to its pacing plan, LABF must continue to make new commitments
every 12 to 18 months.
James Wesner, Kweku Obed and Neil Capps left the meeting.
Introductions
President Roa introduced Reshma Soni, the Acting City Comptroller for the City of Chicago, to the Board.
Mr. Walsh introduced the Trustees, Staff and Consultants to Ms. Soni.
Election of Board Vice-President
This item was taken out of order. Mr. Walsh and Mr. Burns discussed an issue regarding the Illinois
Pension Code’s requirements for filling vacant appointed positions on the Board. The Trustees agreed the
issue should be reviewed by Corporation Counsel for the City of Chicago. The Board also decided to defer
the election of a Board Vice-President until a future meeting.

ADMINISTRATIVE HEARING - TREADWELL
Pursuant to the March 25, 2019 Order issued by the Circuit Court of Cook County, an administrative hearing
was held to determine how to distribute the employee contributions made to the Laborers’ and Retirement
Board Employees’ Annuity and Benefit Fund of Chicago (“LABF”) by Alphonso Treadwell Sr.
(“Alphonso”), a deceased member of the LABF.
Victor Roa, Board President, presided over the hearing. Joseph Burns and Taylor Muzzy, Fund Counsel,
represented the LABF. Alphonso’s ex-spouse, Yvonne Treadwell was present and was represented by Neil
Kauffman. Four of Alphonso and Yvonne’s five children were present: Tameka Esco, represented by
Ralanda Webb, Alphonso Treadwell Jr., Dedra Treadwell, and Lakisha Esco. The hearing was transcribed
by a court reporter.
At the conclusion of the hearing, the trustees decided to enter executive session pursuant to 5 ILCS
120/2(c)(4) and (11).

ADMINISTRATIVE REPORT
Tier 3 Normal Cost Calculation
Mr. Walsh reported that AON determined that the Tier 3 normal cost as a percentage of pay is 17.34% for
2019, based on the December 31, 2018 valuation data. Therefore, the contribution rate for Tier 3 employees
will remain 11½% for the year 2020. Trustee Hamburger requested a copy of the results be sent to the City
of Chicago.
Pre-Approval of Reciprocal Conference
Mr. Walsh reported that the 2019 Reciprocal Conference will be held in Champaign, Illinois on August 12
and 13, 2019 and informed that Board that up to five staff members might attend the conference.
Legislative Matters
Mr. Walsh reported that Senate Bill 1264 and House Bill 2460 were sent to the Governor on June 28, 2019
and June 27, 2019, respectively, and have yet to be signed by the Governor.
Miscellaneous
This item was not discussed.
Public Act 100-0023 Employer Contribution Mechanics
Mr. Walsh briefed the Board regarding a meeting he had with representatives from the City of Chicago
regarding contributions associated with those taking a leave of absence from the City of Chicago to work
for a local labor organization. Further discussion was deferred to a future Board meeting.
LEGAL REPORT
Underwood Litigation
Mr. Walsh updated the Board regarding the actions taken at the September 15, 2019 hearing in the Circuit Court
of Cook County regarding the Underwood litigation. Mr. Walsh also updated the Board on the mechanics of
the court order issued related to the notices that are to be sent to retiree members of LABF who may be eligible
for the retiree healthcare subsidy.
