The following project was reviewed by the PRC:
1. 913-925 N. Hoyne
2nd Ward
Ukrainian Village District Extension
Proposed residential conversion of a former 3-story masonry church building and
former 2 ½ story masonry school building into 20 residential condominium units with
parking.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

925 N. Hoyne (former church)
1. The permit plans shall include detailed specifications for the retention and
repair of the historic stained glass panels and sashes on the front façade and
the corner towers, which includes the large central pointed window, the
stained glass transoms above the entrance doors, and fourteen pointed arch
windows on the corner towers;
2. The applicant shall confirm that the proposed skylights are necessary to meet
light and ventilation requirements (provide calculations) and the permit plans
shall include the product specifications of the skylights, which shall be lowprofile with a finish to match the color of the roofing shingles;
3. Wall partitions on the first and second floors which terminate on a vertical
window mullion on the north façade shall be pulled back from the mullion and
redesigned so as to terminate against the wall;
4. The new drywall enclosure behind the large stained glass window on the west
(front) façade shall be revised to be set back at least 3’ from the glass, access
for maintenance shall be provided, and the space shall be heated/conditioned;

 5. Enlarged existing and proposed window details shall be included in the permit
plans to show that the replacement window profiles and configurations will
match the historic profiles and configurations. Any new muntins shall have
interior and exterior profiles with spacer bars to match the historic muntin
profiles;
6. New metal railings safety railings at the main entrance shall have a simple
design and be painted black;
7. All exterior wall samples, including any replacement masonry units and
mortar, shall be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff prior to
order and installation; and,
8. A report by a licensed structural engineer addressing the proposed structural
modifications, relative to how the building should be braced and protected
during construction, shall be submitted for Historic Preservation staff review
and approval, as part of the permit plans. The recommended measures,
sequencing, and protections shall be incorporated in the structural and
architectural drawings.
913 N. Hoyne (former school)
9. The proposed rooftop addition shall be clad with a dark, non-reflective
material, such as cement fiber clapboard or metal panels;
10. The aluminum replacement windows for the front façade shall be modified to
have a sash to glazing minimum depth of 3/8” typical of wood windows. All
typical proposed aluminum replacement window details shall be submitted
with the permit plans and shall show historic brick molds to be replicated inkind as proposed;
11. On the second floor the wall partition terminating at the vertical mullion
dividing the two central double-hung windows shall be no wider than the
mullion or be relocated to terminate against a solid wall;
12. Proposed parking for two cars in the side yard shall be screened from the
street with a solid fence or hedge; and,
13. All exterior wall samples, including any replacement masonry units and
mortar, shall be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation staff prior to
order and installation.
2. 310 N. Peoria
27th Ward
Proposed Fulton-Randolph Historic Market District
Proposed alteration of an existing 1-story side addition, construction of a new
elevator with rooftop override, new storefronts on the east elevation, and a new
canopy on the front façade.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. A report by a licensed structural engineer addressing the proposed canopy and
foundation repairs, relative to how the historic canopy should be braced and
protected during construction, shall be submitted for Historic Preservation
staff review and approval, as part of the permit plans. The recommended

 measures, sequencing, and protections shall be incorporated in the structural
and architectural drawings;
2. Enlarged dimensioned details and sections of the proposed storefront and
loading dock shall be provided with the permit plans. All storefront elements
shall have a dark, factory-applied finish;
3. Enlarged dimensioned details of the steel cladding at the foundation wall shall
be submitted with the permit plans; and,
4. Should this proposal require a zoning variation and/or adjustment, and the
Commission takes no position regarding any requested variance/adjustment
relative to the zoning code requirements.
3. 2345 N. Kedzie
32nd Ward
Logan Square Boulevards District
Proposed new masonry openings, metal awning, raised deck and a free standing
pergola in the north side yard of a 2-story masonry building.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The new wood deck, stairs, railings and pergola shall be painted; and,
2. Dimensioned existing and proposed replacement window and front door
details shall be submitted with the permit plans to show that the replacement
window and door profiles will match the historic profiles. The historic wood
brick molds shall be retained in place or replaced to match if necessary.
4. 930 E. 42nd Pl.
Oakland District
Proposed interior and exterior alterations to 3-story row house including
reconstruction of a decorative stone pediment at the front facade.
Action:

4th Ward

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The limestone shall match the color, texture and appearance of the historic
stone; and,
2. Window details (with dimensions) shall be included in the permit plans.
5. 2117-19 W. LeMoyne
2nd Ward
Wicker Park District
Proposed new 2½-story residential building on proposed subdivided lot, a new rear
addition for the existing greystone, demolition of 1-story portion of the rear buildings
and conversion of remaining rear buildings into garages.

The motion to approve the project per the staff recommendations did not
pass (The vote was 1-2-1 with Commisioner Wong voted for,
