 PRESENTATION OF PETITIONS, ORDINANCES AND RESOLUTIONS
A.

A resolution by all Commissioners in memory of former Wayne County Commission Chair
Jarrette Simmons, who passed away in December 2018. (2019-66-007)

Pursuant to the Wayne County Commission Rules of Procedure, a Resolution of
Memoriam was read into the record in memory for former Wayne County Commissioner
Jarrette Simmons. The resolution is listed below:
JARRETTE SIMMONS
WHEREAS, it is with great respect that we, the members of the Wayne County Commission,
solemnly pause and reflect on the life of JARRETTE SIMMONS, a truly celebrated public servant and
distinguished Wayne County Board of Commission Chair, who made her transition December 2, 2018, at
age 90;
WHEREAS, JARRETTE SIMMONS made history in 1978 as the first African American woman to
chair the Wayne County Commission, and one of the first African American women to serve in such a
position nationwide; and
WHEREAS, JARRETTE SIMMONS was born February 1, 1928, in Cincinnati, Ohio, to the
loving union of Murphy and E. Louise Wright and graduated from the prestigious Walnut Hills High
School at age 16, with the dream of serving in government; and
WHEREAS, JARRETTE SIMMONS served as a keypunch operator and switchboard operator for
the Wayne County Road Commission and a campaign staff member for Detroit City Council member David
Eberhard prior to entering elective politics on her own. First elected as a precinct delegate,
JARRETTE SIMMONS was later elected to the Wayne County Commission and served in the days preceding
the current county charter; and
WHEREAS, married for many years to the love of her life, her late husband, Clarence,
JARRETTE SIMMONS leaves many loving memories for her three surviving children, 18 grandchildren and
13 great-grandchildren; therefore, be it
RESOLVED, that on this 7th day of February, that this resolution endure as a lasting
record of acknowledgement and remembrance as the Wayne County Commission proudly recognizes the
inspirational life lived so graciously by JARRETTE SIMMONS and that a suitable copy be presented to
her beloved family.

Members rose in support of the adoption of the resolution.
[Resolution No. 2019-058]
