The Chicago Housing Authority originally entered into an intergovernmental
agreement with the Chicago Police Department for above baseline services in
2000 after disbanding the CHA Police. The CHA entered into subsequent IGAs and
amendments, with the current IGA due to expire December 31, 2011. In the past and in
accordance with the prior IGA and amendments, CPD provided special policing programs for the
Henry Homer and the surrounding Westhaven Community, Ickes, Cabrini-Green Homes, and
1230 Burling.

The IGA for 2012 provides for the specialized policing programs at Altgeld/Murray, Henry
Homer/Westhaven, Lake Park Place, Oakwood Shores, Jackson Square, Parkside/Seward Park
and other CHA communities as assigned. These specialized services include:

0 Dedicated watches for Homer/Westhaven Community, Altgeld/Murray, Lake Park Place
Oakwood Shores, Jackson Square, Parkside/Seward Park and other CHA locations as
needed.

0 Increased police services for the above mentioned developments, including increased foot
patrols

0 Customized policing strategy collaborated by private property managers,
and CPD.

0 Attendance at community security meetings and have a high visibility in the

community.

Monitoring and prevention of gang activities.

Monitoring of drug and other illegal activities and take appropriate action as necessary.

Attendance at all court hearings for all arrests made by the police officers.

Responding to acts of domestic violence.

Responding to unauthorized or unlawful activities or entries into residences.

In addition to these services the CPD will continue to provide statistics, and relevant data
including crime reports.
Based on the foregoing, it is in the best interest of the CHA to enter into a new IGA with CPD

for the period one (1) year from January 1, 2012 to December 31, 2012 for an amount not-to-
exceed $6,000,000.00 to ensure that various CHA sites have supplemental police services.
