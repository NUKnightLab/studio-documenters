ADMINISTRATIVE MATTERS

Chairman Gierut asked Executive Director Mlynski to present the Local Panel administrative actions report for the period
of time from March 1 through March 31, 2014. She presented the report and summarized its highlights. The Board
examined the report and discussed it. Fiscal, legislative, and mediation reports were also presented to the Board by the
Executive Director.
V.

BOARD ACTIONS
1.
Case No. L-RC-13-011
American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees, Council 31/ City of Chicago (Office of
Inspector General)

Chairman Gierut asked General Counsel Post to call the case. General Counsel Post called the case and asked Counsel
Philip Kazanjian to present and discuss the issues of the case. Counsel Kazanjian stated that Administrative Law Judge
(ALJ) Anna Hamburg-Gal issued a Recommended Decision and Order (RDO) on a petition filed by the Petitioner seeking
to include in an existing bargaining unit individuals in the titles of Investigator I, Investigator II, and Investigator III
employed by the Employer and that the ALJ found that the petitioned-for employees were confidential employees within
the meaning of Section 3(c) of the Act and that the petition should be dismissed. After his presentation, Counsel
Kazanjian recommended that the Board accept the RDO. After discussion, Member Anderson moved to accept the RDO.
Member Lewis seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
2.
Case No. L-CA-10-061
Service Employees International Union, Local 73/ City of Chicago
Chairman Gierut asked General Counsel Post to call the case. General Counsel Post stated that ALJ Elaine Tarver issued a
RDO finding that the Respondent violated Sections 10(a)(4) and (1) of the Act, by failing to bargain with the Charging
Party before installing and using hidden surveillance cameras to aid in disciplining bargaining unit employees. After his
presentation General Counsel Post recommend that the Board accept the RDO. After discussion, Member Lewis moved to
accept the RDO. Member Anderson seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
VI.

EXECUTIVE SESSION

unnecessary.
