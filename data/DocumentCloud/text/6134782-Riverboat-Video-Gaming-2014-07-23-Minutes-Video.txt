MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
July 24, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, July 24, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”)
was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building, Chicago, Illinois.
The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 11:00 A.M. Present were
Members Maribeth Vander Weele, Lee Gould and Michael Holewinski.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES – Open Session
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of June 26, 2014. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
Administrator Ostrowski stated that there are 4,369 live locations and 17,719 VGT’s up and
running.
ITEM FOR INITIAL CONSIDERATION –
Approval of a New Video Game for Play on a VGT -- Waiver of the Two Meeting Rule
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board waive the Two Meeting Rule related to the request
for the approval of Keno, a new video game for play on a VGT. Member Gould seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ITEM FOR FINAL CONSIDERATION –
Approval of a New Video Game for Play on a VGT -Member Gould moved that the Board approve Keno as a new video game for play on a VGT
pursuant to Section 5 of the Video Gaming Act. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
SUPPLIER, DISTRIBUTOR AND MANUFACTURER LICENSEE ITEMS –
M3 Technology Solutions, LLC – Supplier – License Renewal
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board renew the Supplier License of M3 Technology
Solutions, LLC effective July 25, 2014, for a term of one year, expiring in July 2015. Member
Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

 TERMINAL OPERATOR LICENSEE ITEMS –
G.F.M. Gaming, LLC – Terminal Operator License Renewal
Member Gould moved to renew the Terminal Operator License of G.F.M. Gaming, LLC,
effective July 25, 2014 for a period of one year, expiring in July 2015. Member Vander Weele
seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
Schlotte Gaming LLC – Terminal Operator License Renewal
Member Vander Weele moved to renew the Terminal Operator License of Schlotte Gaming
LLC, effective July 25, 2014 for a period of one year, expiring in July 2015. Member Gould
seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
Midwest Electronics Gaming, LLC – Terminal Operator License Renewal
Member Gould moved to renew the Terminal Operator License of Midwest Electronics Gaming,
LLC, retroactive to April 2014 for a period of one year, expiring in April 2015. Member Vander
Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
TERMINAL HANDLER & TECHNICIAN LICENSEE ITEMS –
Approvals & Denials
Member Vander Weele moved to approve 2 applications for a Technician Licenses, 28
applications for Terminal Handler Licenses and issue a Notice of Denial to Craig Jenkins.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call
vote.
VIDEO GAMING LOCATION LICENSEE ITEMS
Approvals & Denials
Member Gould moved to approve 96 applications for Video Gaming Location Licenses, and that
the Administrator issue a Notice of Denial to the following applicants:
o Chateau Restaurant, Inc.
o Las Palmas Nightclub, Incorporated
o J Ruby’s, LLC
o SFQ Properties Inc.
o Angel’s Tap Incorporated
o TPC Longshots Inc.
o INTIMO, LLC
Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.
Moatsy Bowl, Inc.
Member Vander Weele moved to deny the renewal of the Video Gaming Location License of
Moatsy Bowl, Inc. for its failure to maintain its State of Illinois Liquor License. Member Gould
seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

2

 ADMINISTRATIVE REQUESTS FOR HEARING/REQUESTS TO REAPPLY –
Requests for Hearing
Member Gould moved to deny the requests for hearing from the following individuals and
entities, as they have failed to establish a prima facie case for hearing: Michael Tulo, Larry S’s
of Harvey, Inc., Sangamo Surf Club, A1 Vending Inc., and Brass Ring Gaming, Inc.; and grant
the request for hearing from Universal Gaming Group, LLC as it has presented a prima facie
case for hearing. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
Requests to Reapply
Member Vander Weele moved to grant the requests to reapply from P.T.’s Mescher &
Associates, LLC, JSJ Enterprises, Inc., Morris Moose Lodge 967, and J’s Petti-coat Junction,
Inc. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll
call vote.
DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS –
NRT Technology Corp. & M3 Technology Solutions, LLC
Member Gould moved that the Board issue Disciplinary Complaints to fine NRT Technology
Corp. and M3 Technology Solutions, LLC each $5,000.00 for their failure to timely submit their
annual renewal application materials. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
Doit’s Village Inn, Inc.
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint to fine location
licensee Doit’s Village Inn, Inc. $5,000.00 for its failure to inform the Board of a change in
ownership structure. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
Long Shot to Win, Inc.
Member Gould moved that the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint to fine location licensee
Long Shot to Win, Inc. $5,000.00 for its failure to disclose a PSIC to the Board. Member
Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call
vote.
Offie’s Tap, Inc.
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board issue a Disciplinary Complaint to fine location
licensee Offie’s Tap, Inc. $2,781.18, for its conduct in operating video gaming for 23 days
without a valid State of Illinois liquor license. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

3

 At 11:15 A.M. Member Gould motioned to adjourn and Member Vander Weele seconded the
motion. All Members voted in favor of adjournment.
