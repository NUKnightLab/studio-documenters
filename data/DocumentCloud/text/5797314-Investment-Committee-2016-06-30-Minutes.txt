UHY CONTRACT FOR PROCESS OPTIMIZATION AND SOFTWARE SELECTION SERVICES
By Trustee Diaz – Supported By Trustee Gnatek
WHEREAS, at its regular meeting on February 11, 2016, the Board of Trustees of the Police and Fire
Retirement System of the City of Detroit (“PFRS”) authorized execution of an engagement agreement
with UHY Advisors of MI, Inc (“UHY”) to provide professional services for Process Optimization and
Software Selection (the “Contract”); and
WHEREAS, the Contract and the services received will benefit the joint administration of the PFRS and
the General Retirement System of the City of Detroit (“GRS”), and the costs for which services from
UHY will be equally shared between PFRS and GRS; and
WHEREAS, the Board is in receipt of a presentation document presented to the Board on June 16, 2016, by
UHY Principal Cindy Hannafey entitled “Process Optimization and Software Selection Phase I & II
Report Out,” dated June 16-17, 2016 (the “Phase I & II Report”), which includes discussion of Phase III
Implementation Strategy and anticipated project budget (“Phase III Implementation”); and
WHEREAS, the Board continues to view investment in improvements to optimization of administrative
processes and software as a use of funds consistent with the obligation to discharge fiduciary duties to
PFRS pursuant to the Public Employee Retirement System Investment Act, Act 314 of 1965; and
WHEREAS, the Board has been advised by the Executive Director and General Counsel in this matter; and
WHEREAS, the Board has considered this matter in open session; therefore be it
RESOLVED, that the Board authorizes UHY to proceed with Phase III Implementation consistent with the
Phase I & II Report; and be it further
RESOLVED, that the Board authorizes a budget for Phase III Implementation of between $3.2 million and
$4.2 million, to be equally supported and shared between PFRS and GRS; and be it further
RESOLVED, that UHY provide Phase III Implementation updates with presentations before the Board not
less frequently than quarterly; and be it further
RESOLVED, that the Executive Director is authorized to execute an engagement agreement with UHY
consistent with the foregoing terms subject to the Executive Director’s approval of an agreed-upon
Statement of Work with UHY for Phase III Implementation, and further subject to review and approval of
the form of engagement agreement for Phase III Implementation by General Counsel; and be it further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this resolution will be forwarded to UHY; and Clark Hill:
Yeas – Trustees Adams, Diaz, Gnatek, James, Jones, Neary, Roberson, Scales, Serda, Szymanski, Trozak,
Tucker and Chairperson Pegg – 13
Nays – None
