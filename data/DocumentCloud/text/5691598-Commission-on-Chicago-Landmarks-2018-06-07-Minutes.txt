The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:

1. 1246 N. Astor
43rd Ward
Astor Street District
Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation including a new, one-story rooftop
addition with deck.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. Samples of the proposed cladding for the rooftop enclosure shall be
submitted for Historic Preservation staff review and approval prior to
order and installation; and,
2. The project as proposed has outstanding zoning issues that will need
to be addressed before the pending permit application can be
approved. Should the project, for which the Permit Review
Committee issued a preliminary approval, be substantially altered,
the project will need to be reviewed anew by the Permit Review
Committee. The Commission takes no position regarding any
requested zoning variances/adjustments or other requests relative to
the zoning code requirements.

2. 1958 W. North
2nd Ward
Milwaukee Avenue District
Proposed interior and exterior renovation including partial window replacement, new
storefronts, and partial construction of a new flat roof corner turret and pressed metal
cornices based on historic documentation.

 Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. Partial reconstruction of the missing corner turret is approved as
proposed;
2. Samples of any replacement stone, patching, and any new masonry
and mortar shall match the historic in size, color, profile, texture, and
type and shall be reviewed and approved by Historic Preservation
staff prior to order and installation;
3. The new windows shall match the existing window in size, glass
size, operation, muntin arrangement, and muntin size. The windows
can be simulated divide lite windows as proposed. Existing and
proposed details of exterior profiles shall be submitted with the
permit application;
4. The storefronts shall have a dark factory-applied finish and all glass
will be clear vision glass;
5. No exterior lighting is approved with this submittal. The design,
materials, locations, and installation details shall be reviewed by
Historic Preservation staff when available;
6. No signage is approved with this submittal. Any proposed signage
shall be permitted separately and reviewed and approved by Historic
Preservation staff prior to order and installation; and,
7. A report by a licensed structural engineer addressing the proposed
interior structural removal and new interior wall and floor openings,
relative to how the building should be braced and protected during
such removal and reconstruction, shall be submitted for Historic
Preservation staff review and approval, as part of the permit plans.
The recommended measures, sequencing, and protections shall be
incorporated in the structural and architectural drawings
.
3. 1425 W. Fullerton
2nd Ward
Fullerton State Bank Building
Proposed rehabilitation and conversion of existing 3-story bank building into an 8unit residential use including façade restoration and new 4-story side addition and a
1-story rooftop addition.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. Masonry repair scope of work shall be indicated in the permit
application plans. Samples of the exterior wall materials for the
additions including the new brick, stone base, and metal
color/finishes shall be reviewed and approved by Historic
preservation staff prior to order and installation;
2. The proposed 1-story rooftop addition, is approved as proposed.
Any rooftop elements such as landscaping in built-in planters,
trellises, or code-required railings shall be set back and low enough
so as not to be visible from the public rights-of-way;
3. A report by a licensed structural engineer addressing how the
historic facades to remain should be supported, brace, and protected,
in-situ, during the construction of the new third floor, side addition,

 and rooftop addition shall be included on the permit plans. The
recommended measures, sequencing, and protections shall be
incorporated in the structural and architectural drawings;
4. The proposed new vinyl windows are not a compatible match to the
dimensions and profiles of the existing steel windows and are not
approved. Replacement windows shall be aluminum or steel and
shall more closely match the historic windows in size, glass size,
operation, mullion and muntin arrangement, and size. The windows
may be simulated divided lite windows. Existing and proposed
details of exterior profiles shall be submitted with the permit
application;
5. The aluminum panels proposed to clad the rooftop addition shall be
dark gray so that it better recedes visually. Staff shall review and
approve all material samples prior to order and installation; and,
6. The Commission takes no position regarding any variations and/or
adjustments relative to the zoning code requirements.
