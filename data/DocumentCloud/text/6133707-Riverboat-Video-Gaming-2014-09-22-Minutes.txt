MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - RIVERBOAT
OPEN SESSION
September 23, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Tuesday, September 23, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Riverboat of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois at 10:19 A.M. Present were the following: Chairman Aaron Jaffe, Members,
Michael Holewinski and Lee Gould.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES – Open Session
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of August 21, 2014. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
OWNER LICENSEE ITEMS:
• Elgin Riverboat Resorts d/b/a Grand Victoria Casino – Ownership Transfer
Member Holewinski moved to waive Rule 105(e) regarding consideration of
requests at two separate meetings, and pursuant to Rule 235, move to approve the
ownership transfer of Hoinfad Trust #3. Member Holewinski seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
•

Southern Illinois Riverboat Casino Cruises, Inc. d/b/a Harrah’s Metropolis Casino –
Bradley Marx Hirsch – Level One
Member Gould moved to approve Bradley Marx Hirsch as a Level One
Occupational Licensee at Southern Illinois Riverboat Casino Cruises, Inc. d/b/a
Harrah’s Metropolis Casino in his position as Senior Vice President and General
Manager. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
•

Hollywood Casino Aurora, Inc. d/b/a Hollywood Casino Aurora – Scott E. Rhoad –
Level One
Member Holewinski moved to approve Scott E. Rhoad as a Level One
Occupational Licensee at Hollywood Casino Aurora, Inc. d/b/a Hollywood
Casino Aurora in his position as IT Manager. Member Holewinski seconded the
motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

•

Penn National Gaming, Inc. – Ameet L. Patel – Key Person
Member Gould moved to approve Ameet L. Patel as a Key Person of The Alton
Gaming Company d/b/a Argosy Casino Alton; Empress Casino Joliet
Corporation d/b/a Hollywood Casino Joliet; and Hollywood Casino – Aurora,
Inc. d/b/a Hollywood Casino Aurora in his position as Senior Vice President of

 Regional Operations for Penn National Gaming, Inc. Member Holewinski
seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
SUPPLIER LICENSEE ITEMS:
• GLP Capital, L.P. – License Renewal
Member Holewinski moved to renew the Supplier License of GLP Capital, L.P.,
effective September 23, 2014 for a period of four years, expiring in September 2018,
and designate the following as Key Persons: Gaming and Leisure Properties, Inc.,
GLP Capital Partners, LLC and Peter Carlino. Member Gould seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
• Micro Gaming Technologies, Inc. – Scott Allen Winzeler – Key Person
Member Gould moved to approve Scott Allen Winzeler as a Key Person of Micro
Gaming Technologies, Inc. in his position as Chief Operating Officer. Member
Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll
call vote.
OCCUPATIONAL LICENSEE ITEMS:
• Member Holewinski moved to find suitable and approve 47 level 2 occupational
licenses, and 108 level 3 occupational licenses, and issue notices of denial to
Oscar Mendez and Michael Aslinger. Member Gould seconded the motion. The
Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS:
• Member Gould moved to issue Disciplinary Complaints to revoke the
occupational licenses of Nikia Peoples, Sean S. Lewis, Brett Porter and Alicia
Ferreira, said actions to take effect 21 days from the dates of service of the
Complaints unless the licensees file Answers within that time period. Member
Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATIVE REQUESTS FOR HEARING:
• In Re: the Disciplinary Action of Ernest Lee Marbley: DC-14-11
Member Holewinski moved to dismiss the Disciplinary Action DC-14-11 filed
against him in April 2014. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
At 10:31 A.M. Member Gould motioned to adjourn and Member Holewinski seconded the
motion. All Members voted in favor of adjournment.
Respectfully submitted,
Mary C. Boruta
Secretary to the Administrator
