PUBLIC TESTIMONY

Authorization as a public speaker shall only be granted to those individuals who have submitted in writing,
their name, address, subject matter, and organization (if any) to the Secretary 24 hours in advance of the
meeting. Duly authorized public speakers shall be called upon to deliver testimony at a time specified in
the meeting agenda. Authorized public speakers who are not present during the specified time for public
testimony will forfeit their allotted time to speak at the meeting. Public testimony must be germane to a
specific item(s) on the meeting agenda, and the testimony must not exceed three minutes; the Secretary
will keep track of the time and advise when the time for public testimony has expired. Persons authorized
to provide public testimony shall not use vulgar, abusive, or otherwise inappropriate language when
addressing the Board; failure to act appropriately; failure to speak to an item that is germane to the
meeting, or failure to adhere to the time requirements may result in expulsion from the meeting and/or
disqualify the person from providing future testimony.

FINANCING AUTHORITY
WHEREAS, the Federal Tax Reform Act of 1986, as amended, imposes a limit on the aggregate amount
of tax exempt private activity financing authority, also known as “volume cap”, that can be authorized by
a State; and
WHEREAS, the State of Illinois has adopted procedures for the allocation of volume cap pursuant to the
Illinois Private Activity Bond Allocation Act, 30ILCS 345, also known as the “Illinois Allocation Act”; and
WHEREAS, the Governor’s Office is the entity charged with authority to allocate volume cap among the
political subdivisions within the State of Illinois; and
WHEREAS, the current limit on the aggregate amount of volume cap that a State can issue, adjusted for
inflation for calendar year 2018, is $105.00 multiplied by the State’s population; and
WHEREAS, the current limit on the aggregate amount of volume cap that the State of Illinois can issue,

PROPOSED RESOLUTION
ESTABLISHING A FOUNDATION FOR THE BENEFIT OF THE SHERIFF’S OFFICE OF
COOK COUNTY
WHEREAS, pursuant to 55 ILCS 5/3-6021 the Sheriff shall be conservator of the peace in his or her
county, and shall prevent crime and maintain the safety and order of the citizens of that county; and
WHEREAS, the Sheriff manages and operates the Cook County Department of Corrections, located at
2700 S. California, Chicago, Illinois, 60608 (“CCDOC”); and
WHEREAS, the CCDOC is the largest single-site and consolidated detention facility in the country,
housing and providing essential needs and services to approximately 10,000 inmates per day, with upwards
of 100,000 inmates processed through CCDOC annually; and
WHEREAS, the Sheriff has emphasized the importance of mental health and substance abuse treatment
for detainees, in addition to educational and job skills programming in CCDOC and reentry planning to
benefit detainees post-release; and
WHEREAS, the Sheriff has expanded the Office’s advocacy role in many ways to positively impact
Cook County, including overhauling Cook County’s approach to foreclosures and evictions, and working
with criminal justice stakeholders to address issues impacting the Cook County Jail population; and
WHEREAS, the Sheriff is also responsible for all security and maintenance at the 14 court facilities
located throughout Cook County, staffed by the CCSO Court Services Department; and
WHEREAS, the Sheriff’s Police Department provides police services in unincorporated areas of Cook
County and several suburban municipalities, and also assists the Chicago Police Department and many
other municipal police departments; and
WHEREAS, in order to assist the Sheriff’s Office in furtherance of its expansive missions, supportive
members of the community incorporated a non-profit charitable Foundation in the State of Illinois for the
benefit of the Sheriff’s Office; and
WHEREAS, the Thomas J. Dart Foundation for the Advancement of Justice will achieve not-for-profit
status for the purpose of accepting charitable contributions in the form of financial, property, or other
donations appropriate to and in support of the mission of the Sheriff’s Office; and
WHEREAS, the purpose of the Foundation is to support the Sheriff’s Office of Cook County, Illinois in
its mission, including but without limitation to providing services and programming to individuals in the
custody of the CCDOC and alternative sentencing programs, to providing services to the community by
and through the Cook County Sheriff’s Police and/or Court Services Department, and to support

WHEREAS, the Foundation will serve to maintain and strengthen the Sheriff’s Office’s role improving

PROPOSED ORDINANCE
THE CONSENSUS REVENUE FORECASTING COMMISSION FOR COOK COUNTY
BE IT ORDAINED, by the Cook County Board of Commissioners that Chapter 2 Administration,
Article III, County Board, Division sections 2-84 through 2-86 of the Cook County Code, is hereby
enacted as Follows:
Sec. 2-84. Short Title

This Division shall be known and may be cited as the “Cook County Forecasting Commission”
Sec. 2-85 Policy and Purpose.

(a) There is hereby created a Commission to develop a consensus revenue forecast on an annual

(b) The Commission will also be responsible for evaluating and forecasting any new revenue sources

Sec. 2-86 Cook County Forecasting Commission.

(a) The Commission will have seven (7) members appointed by the County Board President and
confirmed by the County Board, but also by virtue of their respective positions including: Chairman of the
Budget and Economic Development, Chairman of the Pension Committee, Chairman of the Finance
Committee, the Chief Financial Officer of Cook County, an economist working for the Federal Reserve
Bank of Chicago, an academic economist with expertise in local economic conditions housed at a
University located in Cook County, one member of the public with expertise in public finance.
(b) Board Members will serve for three (3) years and can be reappointed for consecutive terms.
(c) The Commission has the authority to evaluate county revenue sources, purchase subscriptions to
economic and financial data, engage outside forecasting resources and hire staff if needed.
(d) The forecast will be completed no later than September 15th of each fiscal year, and will be
updated quarterly thereafter, to enable coordination with the County Budget and to allow for mid-year
adjustments.
(e) All meetings of the Commission will be open to the public.
(f) The forecast and assumptions will be made publicly available on the County’s website.
