Chairman Gallagher called for approval of the minutes of the July 9, 2007 meeting. Member Piper moved to

IV.

ADMINISTRATIVE MATTERS

Chairman Gallagher asked Executive Director Brosnan to present the State Panel administrative actions for
the period of time from July 1 through July 31, 2007. He distributed the report to the Board and summarized
its highlights. The Board examined the report and discussed it.
V.

BOARD ACTIONS
Case No. S-RC-06-175
1.
International Union of Operating Engineers, Local 150/Village of Hazel Crest

Chairman Gallagher called the case and reviewed its background. Acting General Counsel Brosnan
reviewed the issues presented by the exceptions and recommended that the Board accept Administrative Law
Judge (ALJ) Clifford's recommendation. After discussion, Member Hade moved to accept the ALJ's
recommendation. Member Coli seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
2.
Case No. S-RC-07-036
Illinois Nurses Association/State of Illinois, Departments of Central Management Services and
Healthcare and Family Services
Chairman Gallagher called the case, noting that ALJ Morris issued a Recommended Decision and Order
(RDO) dismissing the petition filed by the Illinois Nurses Association (INA). Acting General Counsel
Brosnan reviewed the issues presented by the exceptions and recommended that the Board accept the ALJ's
recommendation. The Chairman and Board Members then engaged in a discussion of the ALJ's
determinations and the issues raised by the petition and the exceptions. At the conclusion thereof, Member
Coli moved to decline to accept the ALJ's recommendation and instead, direct an election in the petitionedfor unit. Member Piper seconded the motion, and the Board passed it unanimously, by a voice vote.
3.

Board take the case up on its own motion, without reaching the merits, for the sole purpose of effecting a

VI.

OTHER BOARD MATTERS

Chairman Gallagher noted that there was little to report regarding either fiscal or legislative matters, but
pointed out that thus far, our budget was in good shape and that the veto session would be during the first
two weeks of October. Subsequently, the Board discussed whether there was a need for executive session,
concluding it was not necessary at this time.
