 Chicago Landmarks are those buildings, sites, objects, or districts that have been officially
designated by the City Council. They are recommended for landmark designation by the
Commission on Chicago Landmarks, a nine-member board appointed by the Mayor and City
Council. The Commission is also responsible for reviewing any proposed alteration,
demolition, or new construction affecting individual landmarks or landmark districts.
The date the landmark was designated is shown in parentheses.
