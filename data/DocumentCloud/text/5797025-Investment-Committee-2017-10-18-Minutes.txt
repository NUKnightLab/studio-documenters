GENERAL COUNSEL’S REPORT (Cont’d)
RE: Rose McCracken v. Thomas McCracken, Jr.
Wayne County Circuit Court Case No. 17-100794-DM
Motion By: Trustee Sheehan

Supported By: Trustee Anthony

WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees is in receipt of an Amended Domestic Relations
Order for the General Retirement System of the City of Detroit Defined Benefit Plan in
Pay Status (the “Order”), dated July 25, 2017, wherein Rose McCracken, the Alternate
Payee, is awarded certain rights to the benefits of Thomas McCracken, Jr., the
Participant, and
WHEREAS, Mr. McCracken retired on May 25, 2015, elected the Plan’s Option B, 25%
Joint and Survivor with Pop-up form of benefit, and
WHEREAS, pursuant to the terms of the Order, the Alternate Payee is entitled to a
portion of the Participant’s retirement allowance payable from the Retirement System,
and
WHEREAS, said matter had been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that the
applicable terms of said court order are consistent with the provisions of the Retirement
System and applicable law, therefore be it
RESOLVED, that the Board acknowledges receipt of said court order and the Board will
pay pension benefits consistent with the order as soon as administratively feasible, and
further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this resolution be immediately attached as the top sheet of
the pension file and other appropriate records be kept for the Retirement System
relative to this matter, and further
RESOLVED, that copies of this resolution be sent to Thomas McCracken, Jr.,, the
Participant; Mary Anne Noonan, Esq., attorney for the Participant; Rose McCracken, the
Alternate Payee; and Jeremy M. Burks, Esq., attorney for the Alternate Payee.
Yeas: Anthony, Cetlinski, Cowan, Oxendine and Sheehan – 5
Nays: None

GENERAL COUNSEL’S REPORT (Cont’d)
Re: Leslie Anne Selis v. Bryan Scott Vanden Toorn
Wayne County Circuit Court Case No. 00-020673-DM
Motion By: Trustee Sheehan

Supported By: Trustee Anthony

WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees is in receipt of an Eligible Domestic Relations Order
(“EDRO”), dated May 19, 2017, wherein Leslie Anne Selis (f/k/a Leslie Anne Vanden Toorn), the
Alternate Payee, is awarded certain rights to the retirement allowance of Bryan Scott Vanden
Toorn, the Participant, and
WHEREAS, the Retirement System consists of both a defined benefit plan and a defined
contribution (a/k/a the Annuity Savings Fund) plan, and
WHEREAS, pursuant to the EDRO, the Alternate Payee is entitled to a portion of both the
Participant’s defined benefit plan benefits and defined contribution plan benefits payable from
the Retirement System, and
WHEREAS, the payments from the plan to the Alternate Payee shall begin when the Participant
commences benefits from the Retirement System or upon the Alternate Payee’s election, but no
time before the Participant’s earliest retirement eligibility, and
WHEREAS, the Board’s policy is to require that the cost for the actuary’s calculations are to be
borne by the parties to the domestic relations proceedings and the parties’ EDRO provides that
the parties are to share these additional actuarial costs equally, and
WHEREAS, said matter had been discussed with legal counsel who has opined that the
applicable terms of said court order are consistent with the provisions of the Retirement System
and applicable law including Public Act 46 of 1991 (MCLA 38.1701) as amended, therefore be it
RESOLVED, that the Board acknowledges receipt of said court order, will pay pension benefits
consistent with said order upon application by either the Participant or the Alternate Payee, and
further
RESOLVED, that a copy of this resolution be immediately attached as the top sheet of the
pension file and other appropriate records be kept for the Retirement System relative to this
matter, and further
RESOLVED, that copies of this resolution be sent to Bryan Scott Vanden Toorn, the Participant;
Christine K. L. Walsh, Esq., attorney for the Participant; Leslie Anne Selis, the Alternate Payee;
and the Board’s Actuary.
Yeas: Anthony, Cetlinski, Cowan, Oxendine and Sheehan – 5
Nays: None
