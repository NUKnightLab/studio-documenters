WHEREAS, approximately 1.5 million women and 834,700 men nationally are raped and/or physically

partner or boyfriend or girlfriend; and
WHEREAS, preventing domestic violence requires the support and contributions of many partners:
federal agencies, local health departments and private industry; and
WHEREAS, partners work together by collecting data about domestic violence, learning about risk
factors, creating strategies for prevention and ensuring appropriate implementation of best practices; and
WHEREAS, being aware of the policies, laws and assistance available to stand against domestic violence
is an essential factor for prevention; and
WHEREAS, not all domestic violence is physical; many victims suffer emotional harm resulting in low
self esteem and depression, and places them at higher risk for suicide; additionally, domestic violence
affects individuals and families across the life span; and
WHEREAS, we all have a role in preventing domestic violence by being aware of risk factors for
perpetration that create an unsafe environment for our community; and
WHEREAS, Domestic Violence Awareness Month is a nationwide effort held each October to inform
and encourage Americans to take an active stand against intimate partner violence; and
WHEREAS, victims of domestic violence and their families are encouraged to seek assistance through
the Illinois Domestic Violence Helpline, 1-877-TO-END-DV, to receive multilingual, confidential
services for all Illinois residents; and
WHEREAS, the Cook County Department of Public Health, Office of Violence Prevention Coordination
addresses the inpact of violence on the community through primary prevention efforts, awareness and
education; and
WHEREAS, the month of October has been designated Domestic Violence Awareness Month.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that I, Todd H. Stroger, President of the Cook County
Board of Commissioners, in recognition of Domestic Violence Awareness Month, strongly encourage

CC ITEM #2
Submitting a Resolution sponsored by
TODD H. STROGER, President, Cook County Board of Commissioners
RESOLUTION
WHEREAS, October 2008 marks the 24th year of the National Breast Cancer Awareness Month
campaign to educate women about breast cancer, especially concerning early detection through
mammography; and
WHEREAS, in 2008 approximately 3500 of the 180,000 women in the United States diagnosed with
breast cancer will be Cook County, Illinois residents; and
WHEREAS, 40% of all Illinois women newly diagnosed with breast cancer are Cook County residents;
and
WHEREAS, mammography screenings are a women’s best chance for detecting breast cancer early and,
when coupled with new treatment options, can significantly improve a woman’s chances of survival; and
WHEREAS, breast cancer is the most common cancer in women and is second only to lung cancer as the
leading cause of cancer death.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that I, Todd H. Stroger, President of the Cook County
Board of Commissioners, do hereby proclaim October 2008 as Breast Cancer Awareness Month in Cook
County and encourage all Cook County residents to reduce their risk of breast cancer by eating a healthy
diet including fresh fruits and vegetables, exercising regularly, controlling body weight and limiting
alcohol consumption.
