PROPOSED SUBSTITUTE ORDINANCE
DATABASE ORDINANCE
WHEREAS, the Cook County Assessor's Office maintains a database (the
"Database") of certain property identification numbers, address information,
property characteristics and images for all parcels in Cook County for the
purposes of real estate taxation; and
WHEREAS, the Database is currently available to all taxpayers on the Cook
County Assessor's website at no charge; and
WHEREAS, the Database is currently available on a PIN by PIN basis only;
and
WHEREAS, certain organizations desire the ability to access and compile the
Data in a bulk form ; and
WHEREAS, the Cook County Board of Commissioners desires to establish fair
and reasonable public access to the Database while maintaining website
access at no charge; and
WHEREAS, the County asserts copyright protection for the Database.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED BY THE
COMMISSIONERS OF THE COUNTY OF COOK, as follows:

BOARD

OF

 FINANCE AGENDA
APRIL 3, 2007
PAGE 29

Section 1. Definitions: The following term used in this ordinance shall have
the meaning set forth below:
"CCAO Database": means an electronic database maintained by
the Cook County Assessor's Office containing property identification numbers,
address information, property characteristics, and geocode information for all
parcels in Cook County for the purposes of real estate taxation.
Section 2. The CCAO Database shall be made available on an annual
subscription basis for a fee payable in cash, money order, or certified or
cashier's check in one installment. The fee for the subscription service shall be
made available to subscribers for a fee. This fee will start at $30,000 and will
increase with the number of users as reflected in Chapter 32-1, the Fee
Section of the Cook County Ordinance. Those fees shall be adjusted annually
by the Consumer Price Index as of January 1 of each year following the
effective date of this Ordinance.
Section 3. To further carryout the intent of the section above, the Cook
County Assessor's Office may enter into agreements substantially similar to the
form of agreement submitted herewith as Exhibit A.
Section 4. It shall be the duty of the County Assessor to collect and receive
for the County's general fund the subscription fees imposed by this ordinance.
The County Assessor shall adopt, promulgate and enforce rules and
regulations not inconsistent with this ordinance relating to the administration
and enforcement of the provisions of this ordinance.
Such rules and
regulations shall be consistent with the provisions of this ordinance designed to
promote fairness. The Cook County Auditor is hereby authorized to examine
the books, papers and records of any such subscription agreement during
regular business hours.
Section 5. The Assessor may implement reasonable security precautions to
prevent users of the website from downloading significant quantities of data
from the publicly available website. However, those precautions shall not
prevent the regular use of the website by the public or individuals not engaged
in automated or high volume repetitive search practices which can slow the
performance of the site.
Section 6. This ordinance shall be effective upon its passage by the Cook
County Board of Commissioners.

Submitting a Proposed Resolution sponsored by Peter N. Silvestri, County

PROPOSED RESOLUTION
RESOLUTION REQUIRING NOTIFICATION TO THE
BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS OF APPOINTMENTS TO ANY
EXECUTIVE OR ADMINISTRATIVE LEVEL POSITIONS
GRADE 17 THROUGH 24
WHEREAS, Cook County government recently approved a 2007 Cook County
budget with hundreds of employees in Grades 17 through 24 positions; and
WHEREAS, these positions are classified as either executive, administrative or
professional in nature, or of direct assistance to one of these classifications;
and
WHEREAS, in times of difficult financial constraints, it is imperative to
scrutinize any appointment to executive or administrative positions; and
WHEREAS, the President and Board of Commissioners recently approved a
desk audit of all employees of County government, and is considering a
management audit of employees in these same Grades 17 through 24; and
WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners recognizes the statutory authority of
the President in appointing various employees exempt under the federal court's
Shakman decree on hiring.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that within ten days of the
appointment of any executive or administrative employee, in Grade 17 through
24 positions, that written notice be submitted to the members of the Board of
Commissioners stating the position title, department, justification for the need
to fill that position, and the name of the appointed individual.
That said requirement apply to executive and administrative positions in all
County offices, including those under the jurisdiction and authority of the
separately elected County officials, and including special administrative
designations in the offices of the State's Attorney, Public Defender and the
hospitals and clinics operated by the Bureau of Health Services; and
That this requirement shall not apply to positions requiring a professional
license for such position, such as those required of lawyers, doctors, architects,
engineers and any other profession requiring a license to practice or perform
that specific profession, except for those being appointed to supervisory
positions for such professions, or those appointments requiring consent by the
Board of Commissioners.
