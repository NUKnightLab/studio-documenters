INTRODUCTION

Appointed by the Mayor, the Chief Public Health Officer is responsible for planning, directing,
and coordinating operations of the public health care programs, ensuring that essential local
public health needs are met. This position is also responsible for providing leadership to attain
the goals and objectives of the department, protecting and promoting the health, well?being, and
safety of all Detroiters.

3. Manage and oversee administrative policies and procedures consistent with local and state
laws, rules, and regulations for operation of the Health Department.

promoting the health, well?being, safety, and resilience of Detroit residents.

weaknesses.

10. Determine effectiveness of programs and services offered by the Health Department.

11. Advise and consult with a variety of medical, health, community, governmental, and
regulatory agencies in establishing departmental policies and procedures.

12. Direct the identification, evaluation, and acquisition of resources for health programs and
services including grants and funding.

13. Coordinate the periodic review of local, state, and federal legislation and ordinances to assure
current departmental policies.
