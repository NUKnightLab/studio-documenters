PROPOSED ORDINANCE AMENDMENT
Submitting a Proposed Ordinance Amendment sponsored by
FORREST CLAYPOOL, MIKE QUJGLEY and LARRY SUFFREDIN, County Commissioners
PROPOSED ORDfNANCE AMENDMENT

PROPOSED AMENDMENT TO THE COOK COUNTY ETHICS ORDINANCE
Section 2.14 of the Cook County Ethics Ordinance (04-0-18) shall be amended by the addition of a new
subsection (f) set forth below:

2.14

Limitations of Contributions to Candidates and Elected Officials
(a)

No person who has done business with the County within the preceding four years or is
seeking to do business with the county shall make contributions in an aggregate amount
exceeding $1,500 (i) to any candidate for County office or elected County official during
a single candidacy; or (ii) to an elected official of the government of the County during
any non-election year of his or her tenn. The combined effect of these provisions is
intended to permit total contribution up to but not exceeding $3,000 in a year in which a
candidacy occurs. A year for purposes of this Section is from January I st to December
31st of each year.

(b)

For purposes of subsection (a) above, an entity and its subsidiaries, parent company or
other.vise affiliated companies, and any of their employees, officers, directors and
partners who make a political contribution for which they are reimbursed by the entity or
its affiliates shall be considered a single person. However, nothing in this provision shall
be construed to prohibit such an employee, officer, director or partner from making a
political contribution for which he is not reimbursed by a person with whom he or she is
affiliated, even if that person has made the maximum contribution allowed under
subsection (a).

(c)

Any contributions made under this Section shall be reported as required by the Election
Code, 10 ILCS 5/ 1-1 et. seq.

(d)

For purposes of subsection (a) above, " done business" or "doing business" means any
one or any combination of sales, purchases, leases or contracts to, from or with the
County or any County agency in excess of $ 10,000 in any twelve consecutive months.

(e)

For purposes of subsection (a) above, "seeking to do business" means taking action
within the past six months to obtain a contract or business with the County when, if such
action were successful, it would result in the person doing business with the County as
defined in subsection (d) above.

Commissioner Claypool , seconded by Commissioner Quigley, moved that the Proposed Ordinance

At the request of Commissioner Hansen Deputy State's Attorney Patrick T. Driscoll, Jr. addressed the
Proposed Ordinance Amendment. DSA Driscoll stated that 50 ILCS 135/ 1-15 - the Local Governmental
Employees Political Rights Act "governs the political rights of local government employees". Section
135/5 defines political rights to include without limitation . .. "to make campaign contributions". Section
135/ 10 states that "(a) No unit of local government may make or enforce any rule or ordinance that in any
way inhibits or prohibits any of its employees from exercising the employee's political rights". Section
135/ 15 states that " A home rule unit may not regulate employee political rights in a manner inconsistent
with this Act. This Section is a limitation under subsection (i) of Section 6 of Article VII of the Illinois
Constitution on the concurrent exercise by home rule units of powers and functions exercised by the
State."
President Stroger stated that pursuant to 50 ILCS 135/1 -15 the Proposed Ordinance Amendment is ruled
out of order.
Commissioner Claypool, seconded by Commissioner Peraica, moved to appeal the ruling of the Chair.

I am submitting this Resolution as a New Item at the March 1, 2006 Board meeting to be referred to the
Committee on Health and Hospitals.
The Resolution requires the Bureau of Health Services to prepare a report assessing the potential fiscal
and health impact of absorbing many former patients from Bethany Hospital into our public health system
as well as the fiscal impact of operating the Bethany clinic on a rental basis. Furthermore, it requires the
Committee on Health and Hospitals to a hold public hearing to detennine the overall health and
community impact of converting Bethany Hospital to a long-term care facility.
Submitting a Proposed Resolution sponsored by
ROBERTO MALDONADO, County Commissioner
PROPOSED RESOLUTION

WHEREAS, Bethany Hospital has been serving Chicago's West Side for over 85 years as a full service
community hospital; and
WHEREAS, the West Side has Chicago' s largest proportion of medically needy residents as well as the
highest concentration of 'Â·working poor," people earning between 100% and 200% of the federal poverty
level, who are least likely to have either public or private health insurance; and
WHEREAS, according to the Sinai Urban Health Institute, neighborhoods in the Bethany service area
face a host of serious health challenges, including rates of asthma, diabetes, depression, and obesity
dramatically higher than the national and Chicago averages; and
WHEREAS, despite the growing need for general acute care services in Chicago's West Side, Advocate
Health Care Network, Chicago' s largest private nonprofit hospital system and Bethany's corporate owner,
recently announced plans to convert Bethany Hospital from a general acute care faci lity to a long-tenn
care faci lity; and
WHEREAS, Advocate's conversion plan would eliminate a wide range of health care services including
emergency, obstetrics/gynecology, intensive care, mental health, and other crucia l services for the poor
and underserved West Side residents; and
WHEREAS, since the majority of Bethany 's 67,000 patients lack private health insurance, these patients
will likely seek care at Cook County ' s Stroger Hospital, and other area hospitals already struggling to
deal with the area' s large uninsured and Medicaid populations; and
WHEREAS, prior to the conversion plan, Advocate allowed the Cook County Bureau of Health Services
to use space in Bethany Hospital free of charge to operate a walk-in clinic that served 15,000 patients
each year, and it now intends to charge Cook County between $200,000 and $300,000 annually to use the
same space; and

Section 3: Protection for building service employees.
(a) No less than B25 calendar days before terminating any building service contract,
any covered employer shall request the terminated contractor to provide the successor
employer and any collective bargaining representative of any of the affected employees
a full and accurate list containing the name, address, date of hire, and employment

PREAMBLES
WHEREAS, Section 6(a) of Article VII ofthe 1970 Constitution of the State of Illinois provides that "a
County which has a Chief Executive Officer elected by the electors of the County... (is) a Home Rule
Unit" and The County of Cook, Illinois (the "County"), has a Chief Executive Officer elected by the
electors of the County and is therefore a Home Rule Unit and may, under the powers granted by said
Section 6(a) of Article VII of said Constitution of 1970, exercise any power and perfonn any function
pertaining to its government and affairs; and
WHEREAS, the County maintains the Corporate, Public Safety, Health Facilities and Election Funds as
independent, self-balancing funds for accounting purposes; and
WHEREAS, the County has previously established separate Working Cash Funds for Corporate, Public
Safety, Health Facilities and Election Fund purposes; and
WHEREAS, all taxes and other receipts in support of Corporate, Public Safety, Health Facilities and
Election Fund purposes are paid into a single account, the County's " A" Account; and
WHEREAS, the County has therefore detennined that it is possible to consolidate its Working Cash
Funds while maintaining the accounting integrity of the Corporate, Public Safety, Health Facilities and
Election Funds; and
WHEREAS, the County has detennined it to be more efficient and to require less borrowing to manage
its cash on the basis of a consolidated Working Cash Fund; and
WHEREAS, it becomes necessary from time to time to borrow from a Working Cash Fund to meet
ordinary and contingent expenses of the Corporate, Public Safety, Health Facilities and Election Funds of
the County for cash flow needs in anticipation of settlement of various accounts receivable, including
property taxes; and
WHEREAS, it shall be the policy of Cook County to repay all loans to the Corporate, Public Safety,
Health Facilities and Election Funds from any Working Cash Fund from any and all available funds prior
to the end of the fiscal year during which the loan or loans were made; and
WHEREAS, the Annual Appropriation Bill for Fiscal Year 2006 for Cook County, Section 18,
establishes a limit of $264.9 million for the Working Cash Funds.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT AND IT IS HEREBY RESOLVED, by the Board of Commissioners of
the County of Cook, Illinois, as follows:
Section 1. Incorporation of Preambles. The Board hereby finds that all of the recitals contained in the
preambles to this Resolution are full, true and correct and does hereby incorporate them into this
Resolution by this reference.

 JOURNAL OF PROCEEDINGS FOR MARCH I, 2006

amount of $264.9 million to be used for the purposes described above.

consolidate all balances in the County's various Working Cash Funds, and to make available all remaining
sums, not to exceed $264.9 million, for 2006 cash flow borrowing needs to be transferred from time to
time.

Treasurer from any and all avai lable funds, the full aggrega te amount borrowed there from prior to

WHEREAS, Preserve America is a White House initiative developed in cooperation with the Advisory
Council on Historic Preservation, the U.S. Department of the Interior, and the U.S. Department of
Commerce; and
WHEREAS, the goals of this initiative include a greater shared knowledge about our Nation' s past,
strengthened regional identities and local pride, increased local participation in preserving the country' s
irreplaceable cultural and natural heritage assets, and support for the economic vitality of communities;
and
WHEREAS, in the years between the first and second world wars, the great migration brought to the area
south of the Chicago Loop tens of thousands of African-Americans seeking freedom and opportunity in
the great urban landscape of Chicago; and
WHEREAS, this initiative is compatible with our community's interests and goals related to the historic
preservation of Bronzeville, the birthplace of African-American culture, commerce and community in
America's urban heartland; and
WHEREAS, a Preserve America designation would be a step in institutionalizing this important segment
of America's history; and
WHEREAS, designation as a Preserve America Community will improve our community' s ability to
protect and promote its historical resources and use historic assets for economic development and
community revitalization.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Board of Commissioners of Cook County does
hereby support the application for designation of Bronzeville, a historic Chicago community, as a
Preserve America Community; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Board of Commissioners of Cook County will support and
celebrate its citizens rich heritage, and encourage people to experience and appreciate local historic
resources through education and heritage tourism.
