Regular Meeting, Open Session Minutes – Riverboat and Video Gaming
Illinois Gaming Board
Chicago, Illinois
March 1, 2019
Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”) convened for a Regular Meeting – Riverboat and Video Gaming – on
Friday March 1, 2019 in the Conference Room on the 5th Floor of the Michael Bilandic Building Center,
in Chicago, Illinois pursuant to the Illinois Open Meetings Act, 5 ILCS 120/1 et seq.
A roll call was taken. The following Board members were physically present: Chairman Don Tracy,
Member Hector Alejandre and Member Dee Robinson. Due to a work conflict, Member Steve Dolins
participated telephonically. Three members of the Board being physically present, a quorum was
satisfied.
At approximately 9:10 A.M., Member Alejandre moved that the Board go into closed session pursuant to
Section 2(c), paragraphs (1), (11), (14), and (21) of the Open Meetings Act and Section 6(d) of the
Riverboat Gambling Act to discuss items on the closed session agenda relating to specific personnel, the
closed session minutes, investigations, and pending litigation. Member Robinson seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by voice vote. Thereafter, the Board moved from the
Conference Room on the 5th Floor of the Michael Bilandic Building to the Conference Room on the 3rd
Floor of the building for a closed session meeting; the minutes of which are separately recorded.
At approximately 10:00 A.M., the Board returned to the Conference Room on the 5th Floor of the
Michael Bilandic Building Center, in Chicago, Illinois to reconvene its Regular Meeting – Riverboat and
Video Gaming – of Friday March 1, 2019 pursuant to the Illinois Open Meetings Act, 5 ILCS 120/1 et
seq.

Midwest Gaming & Entertainment, LLC d/b/a Rivers Casino-Des Plaines – Request for
Final Consideration of Churchill Downs Incorporated Suitability for Transfer

Prior to any final decision related to the transfer of ownership, the Board engaged in a lengthy discussion
with representatives of the proposed ownership group, existing owners group and their legal
representative regarding the impact that the proposed transfer will have on the minority-owned interests
in the owners license. Amongst others, the Board heard testimony from William Carstanjen, Chief
Executive Officer for Churchill Downs Incorporated and Neil Bluhm, Key Person for Midwest Gaming
& Entertainment, LLC. Following the discussion and Board deliberation on the matter, Chairman Tracy
moved that the Board approve a transfer of ownership, pursuant to an Acquisition Agreement
dated October 31, 2018, in which Churchill Downs Incorporated, through its subsidiary HCRH,
LLC, will purchase the ownership stake held by affiliates and co-investors of Clairvest Group Inc., in
Midwest Gaming Holdings, LLC, parent company of Midwest Gaming & Entertainment, LLC d/b/a
Rivers Casino, and will also purchase the ownership interests elected to be sold by High Plaines
Gaming, LLC and Casino Investors, LLC, provided that Churchill Downs Incorporated makes a
good faith effort to sell up to 10% of the equity value of Midwest Gaming Holdings, LLC to
2

 statutory investors within 90 days of the closing of this transfer u p o n the same price and terms
as the Casino Investors, Inc. received in this transfer. This transfer approval is subject to the final
executed documents being provided to the Illinois Gaming Board and subject to revocation if the final
terms and conditions are not materially consistent with those originally disclosed to the Board. The
transaction to close at a date mutually agreed to by the buyer and seller and approved by the
Administrator. Member Robinson seconded the motion. The motion passed unanimously via voice
vote.
