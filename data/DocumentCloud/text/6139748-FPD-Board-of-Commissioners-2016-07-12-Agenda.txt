PROPOSED ORDINANCE AMENDMENT
PUBLIC UTILITIES
BE IT ORDAINED, by the Forest Preserve District of Cook County Board of Commissioners, that Title
5, Public Utilities, Chapter 2, Franchises, Sections 5-2A-5 and 5-2B-5, Tree Mitigation of the Forest
Preserve District of Cook County Code is hereby amended as Follows:
***
Sec. 5-2A-5. Tree Mitigation.
All plans submitted will include a listing of all trees over four (4) inches in diameter breast height
(DBH) that will be removed from FPD property as a result of construction of the proposed facilities.
This list will include the DBH, common name, and species name for each tree to be removed. Plans
will also indicate the location of the trees to be removed, identity of each individual tree and indicate
that it is to be removed by showing an "X" through the tree symbol on the plans. The FPD will use
this information to determine the tree mitigation fee using the FPD's tree mitigation plan as adopted
by the Board of Forest Preserve District Commissioners on March 21, 2007, and as further amended
by the Forest Preserve District Board of Commissioners.

Construction Engineering ($530,000.00) costs and estimated Construction costs ($6,710,000.00)
associated with the Project; and
WHEREAS, due to the amount of available funding, the Project was divided into two stages: Stage 1
from Devon and Caldwell Avenue to Forest Glen Avenue and, Stage 2 from Forest Glen to Foster &
Kostner Avenues; and
WHEREAS, federal funds available for Stage 2 of the Project total $2,000,000.00 ($1,600,000.00 in

Request: requesting authorization for the Forest Preserve District of Cook County (the “Preserves”) to
enter into a revenue generating Partnership Agreement with Recreational Equipment Inc. (“REI”),
Chicago, Illinois.
Reason: The Preserves seek to partner with REI for the purposes of providing outdoor recreation and
outdoor activities as well as educating the public with nature based activities which includes but is not
limited to hiking, biking, camping, archery and volunteer stewardship. For all courses conducted on the
Preserves’ property, REI will compensate the Preserves with 20% of all registration fees collected from
their participants. Participants of REI’s courses will also receive extensive environmental education
supplemented by nature-based recreational activities which promote forest preserve awareness, and
teaches participates how healthy forest preserves contribute to their community and quality of life.
These courses promote the use of the Preserves and REI has committed to partnering with the Preserves’
volunteers to provide them with recognition gifts to reward these individuals that have dedicated an
enormous amount of volunteer service hours to protect and restore the Preserves. In addition, REI will
cross promote each other’s events and activities that occur on the Preserves property.
