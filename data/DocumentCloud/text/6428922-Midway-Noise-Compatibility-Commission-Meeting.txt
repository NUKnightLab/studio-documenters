REPORT OF THE CHICAGO DEPARTMENT OF AVIATION (“CDA”)
A. Noise Monitors
The new permanent noise monitor installed at Site 314 at 39 th and Albany is
completely done.
Mr. Frame stated that the portable noise monitor scheduled to be installed for
three months in the southeast area of Midway is forthcoming. Work is being
done to determine the best installation location.
B. 2nd Quarter 2016 Airport Noise Management System (ANMS) Report
Mr. Frame asked Mr. Jackson of Landrum & Brown to present the 2nd Quarter
2016 Airport Noise Management System Report.
Mr. Jackson provided a brief overview of the information contained in the report
and proceeded with the details.
Runway Use Report
In the 2nd quarter the most used departure runway heading to the northeast was
04L/04R with 39% of all departures. The most used arrival runway was also
04L/04R coming from the northeast with 52% of all arrivals.
April – The most used departure runway was 04L/04R going northeast with 50%
of all departures and 22L/22R with 25%, followed by 31C with 24% and last 13C
with 1%.
The most used arrival runway coming in from the southwest was 04L/04R with
60%, then 22R/22L with 23%, followed by 31C with 17% and last 13C with 0%.
May – The most used departure runway was 04L/04R with 37% followed by
22R/22L with 33%, next 31C with 26% and last 13C with 4%.
The most used arrival runway was 04L/04R with 48% of all arrivals, followed by
22R/22L with 32%, then 31C with 17% and 13C with 3%.

June – The most used departure runway was 31C with 34%, followed by

The most used arrival runway was 04L/04R with 38%, followed by 31C with 32%,

Airline Fleet Mix Report
There was an average of 712 operations per day with 95 of those occurring at
night. Southwest Airlines had the highest operations with an average of 479
operations per day with 76 of those occurring at night. The B737, used almost
exclusively by Southwest, was the most used aircraft with an average of 479
operations per day. General Aviation followed with 179 operations per day, 10 of
which were helicopters.
Chairman Baliga requested to have a quarter by quarter printout of the average
day and night operations for the past 3 years at the October meeting.
Permanent Noise Monitor Locations
The report indicates the addresses of the 13 permanent noise monitor locations
near the airport as reflected in relation to the Midway 2018 Noise Contour. The
report shows the results for April 2016 through June 2016 with quarterly
breakdowns and a 12 month rolling average. The 2nd quarter 2016 results are
compared to those of the 2nd quarter 2015.
Noise Hotline Report
The total number of complaints for the 2nd quarter was 35,640 of which 4,935
were at night. The highest number of complaints came from Burbank with 34,038
and Chicago with 1,353, the majority of which came from two individuals who
contributed 33,480 and 1,171 respectively.

Mr. Frame reminded the Commission members that a committee should be

B. Planning for the Celebration of the Commission’s 20th Anniversary

Mr. Frame reminded the Commission members that an event is going to be
planned to thank all of them for their last twenty years of community service. The
members decided that they would plan to have an invite only luncheon event
from 12:00pm – 2:00pm on Thursday August 18th at the Midway Hotel Complex
at the Marriott. Invitations for the event will be sent out approximately 2 weeks in
advance of the event.
Mr. Frame presented 3 graphics for a proposed new logo for the Commission to
celebrate 20 years of service. It was decided that a hybrid of two of the proposed
designs would be created and unveiled at the 20th Anniversary Celebration.
NEW BUSNESS
A. Intergovernmental Agreements
Mr. Frame called for a motion to reinstate each of the last 3 intergovernmental
agreements from the Town of Cicero, the Village of Lyons and Cook County. A
motion to reinstate Cicero was made by Trustee Norma Pinion and seconded by
Trustee Mitch Milenkovic. Following a motion to reinstate the Village of Lyons
was made by Christopher Koczwara and seconded by Mayor Harry Klein. Third a
motion was made to reinstate Cook County by Jerry Ponio and seconded by
Joseph Loduca and Nance Dulaj. All motions passed unanimously.
Mr. Frame proposed to initiate a Midway Phase 16 of the Residential Sound
Insulation Program to accommodate over 1,000 homes that have not yet
participated in the program. He recommended a residential subcommittee
meeting before the next MNCC meeting to discuss the new eligibility criteria of
the Part 150 program. Mr. Frame will send out a letter to see who is interested in
joining the residential subcommittee.
B. MNCC Executive Committee Meeting Minutes
Chairman Baliga brought up that for the next MNCC meeting there will be a
condensed version of the minutes of an Executive Committee meeting that was
held Tuesday July 26, 2016 in which flight paths and how things have changed
under new procedures were discussed.
MEETING ADJOURNMENT
Chairman Baliga asked if there were any further questions or new business requiring
further discussion.
With no further questions or other new business, a motion to adjourn was made by Ms.
Dulaj and seconded by Mr. Frame. The motion passed.
Chairman Baliga adjourned the meeting and informed those present that the next
meeting of the Midway Noise Compatibility Commission will be held on Thursday,
October 27, 2016 at 6:30 p.m. at The Mayfield, Chicago.
