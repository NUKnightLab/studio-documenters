 PERMIT REVIEW COMMITTEE
Summary of projects and staff recommendations, September 6, 2018
1. 3256 and 3258 S. Prairie
3rd Ward
Calumet-Giles-Prairie District
Proposed construction of two new, raised, two-story, masonry, detached, single-family
homes.
Applicant:

Tom Boney, TRB Property Management, developer
Christopher G. Boehm/360 Design Studio, architect

Staff Recommendation: Staff recommends that the Committee find that the project, with the
following condition, meets the criteria set forth in the
Commission’s Rules and Regulations and the Commission’s
Guidelines for Alterations to Historic Buildings and New
Construction and therefore, the project will not have an adverse
effect on the significant historical and architectural features of the
landmark district and approve the project with the following
condition:
1. The project as proposed would require a zoning variation
and/or adjustment, and the Commission takes no position
regarding any requested variance/adjustment relative to the
zoning code requirements.
2. 2245 S. Michigan
3rd Ward
Motor Row District
Proposed exterior and interior rehabilitation including new storefronts on the front façade and
insertion of new windows and doors on the north façade.
Applicant:

Windy City RE, owner
Suzi Reinhold, architect

Staff Recommendation: Staff recommends that the Committee find that the project, with
the following conditions, meets the criteria set forth in the
Commission’s Rules and Regulations, the Commission’s
Guidelines for Alterations to Historic Buildings and New
Construction, and Standards 9 and 10 of the U.S. Secretary of the
Interior’s Standards for Rehabilitation of Historic Buildings and
therefore, the project will not have an adverse effect on the
significant historical and architectural features of the proposed
landmark property and approve the project with the following
conditions:
1. As proposed, the new elevator will not extend above the
existing front parapet;
2. As proposed, the storefront shall have a dark factory-applied
finish and all glass shall be clear vision glass; and,
3. All windows and storefront elevations, sections and details
shall be submitted with the permit plans for staff review and
approval.
