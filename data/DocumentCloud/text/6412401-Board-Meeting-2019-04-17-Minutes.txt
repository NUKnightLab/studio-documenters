I.

Approval of Minutes
a. A motion was made by Commissioner O’Connell to approve the release of the
minutes of the March 21, 2019 Board Meeting. The motion was seconded by
Commissioner Pulido Sanchez. The motion was unanimously approved.

II.

Director’s Report
a. Acting Director Enyia informed the Commission the ILCC website will be
undergoing updates and a brief interruption in accessibility will occur on April
23rd.
b. Acting Director Enyia informed the Commission a Transition Meeting is
forthcoming, May 2019 or June 2019.
c. Acting Director Enyia explained HB3625 is still pending.

 d. Illinois Retail Merchants Association (IRMA) presented to the Commission.
e. Acting Director Enyia provided memorandums on One Time Transfers, Alcohol
Infused Products & Special Use Returns.
f. Acting Director Enyia notified the Commission the LC-13 program will be
phased out.

III.

Enforcement Division
a. The ILCC completed 110 SAM compliance checks, 12 failed. Seventeen
compliance checks were repeat violators, 3 violated again. This gives a repeat
violator compliance rate of 82%.
b. The ILCC cited 186 establishments for operating without a valid State of Illinois
Liquor License.
c. As of April 5, 2019, the FY19 LC-13 program has 61 active contracts, using 269
inspectors. These contracts will cover 5,825 licenses.
d. For the period 7/1/18 – 2/28/19 the LC-13 program inspectors have performed
3,449 inspections resulting in 799 licenses with violations.

IV.

Licensing Division
a. The percentage of online renewals is 83%, a 7.7% increase from March 2018.
b. Mrs. Marijan also provided a report of all new licenses issued for March 2019.
c. In March 2019 the ILCC issued 238 new licenses across all categories.

V.

Industry Education
a. The total number of BASSET applicants trained in March 2019 was 10,258.
b. The total number of BASSET applicants in the look up is 273,157.

VI.

Legal Division
a. A motion was made by Commissioner O’Connell to approve the Administrative
Law Judge default recommendations. The motion was seconded by Commissioner
Pulido Sanchez. The motion was unanimously approved.

 b. Kinzie Liquors. v. Village of Bradley 18 SA 08 appeal case was motioned for
dismissal with prejudice. A motion was made by Commissioner O’Connell to
dismiss the appeal case. The motion was seconded by Commissioner Gibbons.
The motion was unanimously approved.
c. Deputy Paziotopoulos notified the Commission of the continuance of Rafael
Nava, Bukana Sports Bar Corporation v. Round Lake 19 CA 05.

VII.

VIII.

Closing Remarks and Adjournment to Hearing
a. Commissioner O’Connell made a motion to adjourn the meeting. Motion was
