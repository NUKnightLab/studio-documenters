MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
May 23, 2013
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, May 23, 2013 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”)
was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building, Chicago, Illinois.
The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 10:00 A.M. Present were
Members Michael Holewinski, Lee Gould, Maribeth Vander Weele and Chairman Aaron Jaffe.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES –
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of April 18, 2013. Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
Administrator Ostrowski commented on the Spring Legislative Session referencing the
possibility of gaming expansion. He gave an update on video gaming stating that 1,620 locations
are in operation and 6,673 VGTs are up and running at the present time.
MANUFACTURER, DISTRIBUTORS AND SUPPLIERS LICENSEE ITEMS –

• Spielo International Canada ULC – Manufacturer Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board
renew the Manufacturer License of Spielo International Canada ULC,
retroactive to December 20, 2012, for a term of one year, expiring in
December 2013. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
TERMINAL OPERATOR LICENSEE ITEMS –

• LZ Entertainment, LLC
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board approve
LZ Entertainment, LLC for a Terminal Operator License effective May
23, 2013 for a period of one year, expiring in May 2014. Member Vander
Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.

 • Terminal Operator License Renewals
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board renew the
Terminal Operator Licenses of the following entities, effective May 23, 2013 for
a period of one year, expiring in May 2014: G3 Gaming, LLC and Tap Room
Gaming, LLC. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
•

Hyperactive Gaming, LLC
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board renew
the Terminal Operator License of Hyperactive Gaming, LLC, effective May 23,
2013 for a period of one year, expiring in May 2014. Member Holewinski
seconded the motion. Member Gould abstained. Chairman Jaffe was in favor of the
motion. The motion passed 3-0-1.

•

J&J Ventures Gaming, LLC
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board renew the
Terminal Operator License of J&J Ventures Gaming, LLC, retroactive to
January 24, 2013 for a period of one year, expiring in January 2014. Member
Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.

TECHNICIAN & TERMINAL HANDLER LICENSEE ITEMS –
•

Approvals & Denials
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board approve 9
applications for Technician Licenses and 80 applications for Terminal Handler
Licenses and that the Board deny the license applications of Robert Gene Kelly,
Jr. and George Waylon Reynolds. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.

ADMINISTRATIVE REQUESTS FOR HEARING –
 Requests for Hearing –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board deny the
requests for hearing from Platinum Gaming Illinois, LLC; TJM Vending, Inc.; KRon Ltd.; and MCRC, Inc. as none has offered facts to establish a prima facie case;

3

 and deny the request for hearing from 219 Sports Bar, LLC as that request is now
moot based on the Board’s previous motion. Member Gould seconded the motion.
The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
• Requests for Hearing –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board deny the following
requests for hearing, as none has offered facts to establish a prima facie case:
Bloomington Columbian Home Association; Venice Social Club; and Quail
Restoration Association of Illinois. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. Member
Vander Weele opposed the motion. Chairman Jaffe was in favor of the motion. The
motion passed 3 to 1.

• Golden Route Operations LLC –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board rescind
its March 20, 2013 denial of Golden Route Operations LLC, and deny its
request for hearing as the request is now moot. Member Vander Weele
seconded the motion. Member Gould abstained. Chairman Jaffe was in favor of
the motion. The motion passed 3-0-1.
• Metro Gaming, LLC –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board grant the
request for hearing from Metro Gaming, LLC as it has offered facts to establish a
prima facie case. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
• Requests to Reapply –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board deny the request
to reapply within one year from the date of denial from The Spirit House, Inc. d/b/a
Spirit House I and II, and grant the requests to reapply from Boxcar Liquors, Inc.
and The Precinct. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the
motion unanimously by roll call vote.
At 11:19 A.M. Member Holewinski motioned to adjourn and Member Vander Weele seconded
the motion. All Members voted in favor of adjournment.
