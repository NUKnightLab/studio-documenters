293373

AN ORDINANCE FOR THE RECORDER OF DEEDS TO MODIFY POSTAGE
RATE TO REFLECT INCREASES BY THE U.S. POSTAL SERVICE; WHEN A
DOCUMENT IS RETURNED TO THE CUSTOMER AND RENT MAIL TYPE
BOXES FOR $20.00 PER MONTH TO CUSTOMERS FOR DELIVERY OF
DOCUMENTS FROM RECORDER OF DEEDS OFFICE (PROPOSED
ORDINANCE). Submitting a Proposed Ordinance sponsored by Robert B. Steele,
County Commissioner.
PROPOSED ORDINANCE
PURSUANT TO THE HOME RULE AUTHORITY OF THE COUNTY OF COOK
AS VESTED IN IT BY THE ILLINOIS CONSTITUTION is hereby enacted the
following Ordinance:
When a document is returned to the customer by the U.S. Postal Service, the
Recorder of Deeds is hereby authorized to charge, in addition to the recording
fees, postage and handling fee of $2.25. The Recorder of Deeds is authorized to
modify the postage rate to reflect increases by the U.S. Postal Service.
The Recorder of Deeds is further authorized to rent mail type boxes to customers
for delivery of documents from the Recorder of Deeds Office. The Rental for each
box shall be $20.00 per month.
This Ordinance shall be in full force and effect 30 days after passage by the Board
of County Commissioners.
* Referred to the Finance Committee on 04/09/08.

PROPOSED SUBSTITUTE ORDINANCE FOR COMMUNICATION NO. 293373
Submitting a Proposed Substitute Ordinance sponsored by
ROBERT B. STEELE, County Commissioner
PROPOSED SUBSTITUTE ORDINANCE
RECORDER POSTAL FEE ORDINANCE AMENDMENT
WHEREAS, Cook County is a “home rule” unit of local government pursuant to
Article VII, section 6(a) of the 1970 Illinois Constitution, and as such, may exercise
any power and perform any function pertaining to its government and affairs; and
WHEREAS, when a customer of the Recorder of Deeds requests that a
transaction be returned to the customer by the U.S. Postal Service, the Recorder
of Deeds is authorized, by Sec. 2-217 of the County Code, to charge, in addition to
the recording fees, a service charge for each document that shall be mailed to the
customer; and
WHEREAS, the Recorder of Deeds is further authorized, by Sec. 2-217, to rent
mail type boxes to customers for delivery of its documents from the Recorder of
Deeds office; and
WHEREAS, these two fees need to be increased to cover the Recorder’s costs for
these services.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED, by the Cook County Board of
Commissioners that Chapter 32 Fees, Sec. 32-1 of the Cook County Code is

PROPOSED ORDINANCE
WHEREAS, pursuant to Counties Code, Fees 55 ILCS 5/3-5018 and Public
records; Internet Access 55 ILCS 5/5 – 1106.1, the Recorder has been expressly
authorized to charge a fee for obtaining documents on the Internet for the purpose
of offsetting the expense of providing such access; and
WHEREAS, Cook County is a “home rule” unit of local government, pursuant to
Article VII, section 6(a) of the 1970 Illinois Constitution, and as such, may exercise
any power and perform any function pertaining to its government and affairs; and

WHEREAS, the Counties Code, Fees of Recorder in Third Class Counties, 55
ILCS 5/4- 12002, as amended by P.A. 93-671 effective June 1, 2004 authorizes
the Recorder to charge a fee for providing non-certified copies of documents in an
amount of up to one half the fee charged for providing certified copies; and
WHEREAS, on September 21, 2004, the Recorder Internet Document Fee
Ordinance was passed, providing a $.50 fee for an Internet copy; and
WHEREAS, the Industrial Engineers surveyed the recording process and
performed a cost analysis.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED, for electronic copies of recorded
documents obtained from the Recorder’s Internet Website, the Recorder is hereby
authorized to charge $1.50 per document.

PROPOSED SUBSTITUTE ORDINANCE
RECORDER INTERNET DOCUMENT FEE ORDINANCE AMENDMENT
WHEREAS, pursuant to Illinois Statute 55 ILCS 5/3-5018 and 55 ILCS 5/5-1106.1,
the Recorder is authorized to charge a fee for electronic copies of recorded
documents obtained from the Recorder's Internet website; and
WHEREAS, in 2004, the Cook County Board of Commissioners adopted the
Recorder Internet Document Copy Fee Ordinance, Sec. 2-216 of the County
Code, and set the fee at $.50 for an Internet copy; and
WHEREAS, the Industrial Engineers have since surveyed the recording process
and performed a cost analysis and determined that $.50 does not cover the
Recorder’s costs.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED, by the Cook County Board of
Commissioners that Chapter 32 Fees, Sec. 32-1 of the Cook County Code is
hereby amended as follows:
