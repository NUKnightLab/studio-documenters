 PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE TO THE FLAG OF THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
Chair Bell advised all to stand to recite the Pledge of Allegiance.

INVOCATION
Chair Bell called upon Commissioner Webb for the introduction of Darcel Brown,
Legislative Research Assistant, who gave the following invocation:
Lord, thank you for the former and newly elected Wayne County Commissioners. As
they make decisions on behalf of the people of Wayne County, give them the necessary
wisdom and perseverance to overcome any obstacle. Provide them with insight and strength
as they work diligently to ensure the success of this region. As they serve the residents of this
County, continue to protect and bless them and their families. Lord, we say a special prayer for
Commissioner Palamara and his family. Comfort them during this time of loss. Let the good
memories sustain them during the days to come. In Jesus’ name we pray. AMEN.”

REPORT FROM THE CHAIRMAN
“At this time I would like to actually acknowledge this as a bittersweet day. It is bitter for
us at Wayne County because we are losing two great public servants, but I suppose it is sweet
for them because they are going into retirement. I would like to call Judge Kelley and Kevin
Kelley to come stand with me please. As you know, Judge Kelley and Kevin Kelley have been
dedicated public servants to this county for several years. Kevin, of course, as we all know is
a former commissioner and currently, as of recently, the Director of our Seniors and Veterans,
and then just Seniors Department. Judge Kelley has had a dedicated service on the bench and
today, I would like to honor this dynamic duo with a resolution signed by all of us
acknowledging their service to us, the service to Wayne County and all that they have done to
12

 make all of the residents here in Wayne County a better place. We thank you and we are very,
very sad to see you both go, I think you are too young to retire, but you know what’s best! ”
At this point Chair Bell read the resolution and Judge Kelley and Kevin Kelley provided
remarks, as well as the Commissioners.

PRESENTATION OF PETITIONS, ORDINANCES AND RESOLUTIONS
A.

A resolution by Commissioner Ware proclaiming January 2019 as Human Trafficking
Awareness Month, which urges residents of Wayne County to speak out against sex and
labor exploitation, encourages community leaders to hold offenders accountable, shows
support to survivors of these horrific crimes and creates appropriate programs and
policies at home, school and work aimed at ending and preventing all forms of human
trafficking. (2019-66-001)

Vice-Chair Pro Tempore Ware, supported by Commissioner Killeen, moved
approval of foregoing item VII. A, and adoption of the resolution.
