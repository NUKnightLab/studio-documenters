I.

Approval of Minutes
a. A motion was made by Commissioner Gibbons to approve the release of the
minutes of the April 17, 2019 Board Meeting. The motion was seconded by
Commissioner O’Connell. The motion was unanimously approved.

II.

Director’s Report
a. Director Enyia informed the Commission the ILCC Chicago and Springfield
locations have been decided and the cost factor will be minimal.
b. Director Enyia informed the Commission the IT, CFO and Industry Education
Manager positions plan to be hired before July 1, 2019.
c. Director Enyia explained several pending Liquor related legislation.

d. Director Enyia announced Industry comments on pending rule making items.

 e. Director Enyia notified the Commission the One Time Transfer document is
being formatted and will be sent when completed.

III.

Enforcement Division
a. The ILCC completed 160 SAM compliance checks, 27 failed. Twenty-seven
compliance checks were repeat violators, 1 violated again. This gives a repeat
violator compliance rate of 96%.
b. The ILCC cited 129 establishments for operating without a valid State of Illinois
Liquor License.
c. The FY19 LC-13 program has 61 active contracts, using 269 inspectors. These
contracts will cover 5,825 licenses.
d. For the period 7/1/18 – 4/30/19 the LC-13 program inspectors have performed
4,560 inspections resulting in 1,477 licenses with violations.

IV.

Licensing Division
a. The percentage of online renewals is 83%, a 10.6% increase from April 2018.
b. Mrs. Marijan also provided a report of all new licenses issued for April 2019.
c. In April 2019 the ILCC issued 267 new licenses across all categories.

V.

Industry Education
a. The total number of BASSET applicants trained in April 2019 was 9,738.
b. The total number of BASSET applicants in the look up is 275,426.

VI.

Legal Division
a. A motion was made by Commissioner O’Connell to approve the Administrative
Law Judge default recommendations. The motion was seconded by Commissioner
Pulido Sanchez. The motion was unanimously approved.

b. Deputy Paziotopoulos notified the Commission of the continuance of Rafael
Nava, Bukana Sports Bar Corporation v. Round Lake 19 CA 05.
c. A motion was made by Commissioner Pulido Sanchez to approve a $3500.00 fine
and 1-day suspension to Moonlight Foods for a 3rd Sale to Minor in 4 years. The

 motion was seconded by Commissioner O’Connell. The motion was unanimously
approved.
