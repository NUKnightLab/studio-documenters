Pursuant to Forest Preserve District of Cook County Code Section 1-4-30 Public Testimony, public testimony will

14-0156
Presented by: ARNOLD L. RANDALL, General Superintendent
PROPOSED INTERGOVERNMENTAL AGREEMENT
Department: Planning and Development
Other Part(ies): Cook County Sheriff’s Office
Request: authorization to enter into and execute
Goods or Services: Cook County Sheriff’s Office plans to pilot a new program to train inmates and post release
participants from the Vocational Rehabilitation Impact Center (a/k/a “Boot Camp”) and participants in the
Sheriff’s Work Alternative Program (“SWAP”) to provide deconstruction and building material salvage services
for removal of cabins at Forest Preserve campgrounds (Dan Beard, and Sullivan) and at a residential property (7
Hickory Lane in Westchester).
Agreement Number(s): N/A
Agreement Period: 3/11/2014 - 3/10/2015, with option to extend for and additional one (1) year period.
Fiscal Impact: $84,839.00
Accounts: Building, 51-6710 & 52-6710 Real Estate Land Acquisition, 06-6801
Summary: The Forest Preserve District of Cook County (the “Preserves”) is in need of demolition/deconstruction
services to clear old campground cabins for redevelopment, as well as remove a residential building for grant
compliance purposes. Under the terms of the IGA, the Sheriff’s Office is responsible for inmate deconstruction
training, transportation, supervision, equipment, reconstruction and demolition of buildings specified by the
Preserves, packaging and removal of reusable building materials according to the Preserves’ specifications, and
hauling of remaining construction debris. The Preserves would commit capital demolition funds to compensate the
Sheriff’s office for deconstruction training, job site supervision, waste hauling and other costs for each site up to an
agreed cap.
