CHICAGO DEPARTMENT OF AVIATION (“CDA”) REPORT
A. Presentation: 3rd Quarter 2013 Airport Noise Management System
(“ANMS”) Report
Mr. Frame asked Mr. Jackson of Landrum & Brown to present the 3rd Quarter
2013 Airport Noise Management System Report.
Runway Use Report
In the 3rd quarter the most used departure runway was 31C with 43% and the
most used arrival runway was 04L/04R with 41%. The most used departures left
from the west and arrivals came in from the west.
July – 70% of all departures were headed west. The most used departure
runway was 31C with 40%, followed by 31% from 22R/22L, 28% from 04L/04R
and 1% on 13C.
60% of arrivals came in from the east. 40% arrived on 04L/04R, followed by 31%
on 31C, 28% on 22L/22R and 1% on 13C.
August – 75% of traffic departed to the west. 55% of departures were from 31C,
followed by 22% from 04L/04R, 21% from 22R/22L and 2% from 13C.
42% of arrivals were on 31C, coming from the southeast, 37% on 04L/04R, 19%
on 22R/22L and 2% on 13C.
September – 60% of traffic departed west. 34% of departures were from both
31C and 04L/04R, followed by 26% from 22R/22L and 6% from 13C.
47% of arrivals were on 04L/04R, followed by 25% on 31C, 22% on 22R/22L and
an increase to 6% on 13C.
Chairman Baliga asked if wind, temperature and atmospheric conditions are
documented every month. Mr. Jackson replied that the National Weather
Service records that information and is a factor when establishing a new contour.
Airline Fleet Mix Report
Southwest Airlines had the highest operations with an average of 450 per day.
The total operations for all airlines for the quarter were an average of 710 per
day, 64 of which were at night. This total now includes Public Charters and Sun
Country which were not previously included. The total for this quarter is higher
than totals in the last couple of years but less than the 900 average in 2004.

The B737, used almost exclusively by Southwest, was the most used aircraft with
an average of 463 operations per day, 42 of which occurred at night. Mr. Baliga
noted that General Aviation was not included in the quarter totals from last year.
He also asked what comprises General Aviation. Mr. Jackson responded that
General Aviation includes helicopters along with everything other than scheduled
airlines. He also stated that because there had been questions regarding
General Operations, starting last quarter those totals have now been included.
Permanent Noise Monitor Locations
The report has been updated to reflect the new 2018 noise contour, the monitor
locations remain the same. The report indicates the addresses of all noise
monitor locations near the airport. The report shows noise monitor results for
October 2012 through September 2013 with quarterly breakdowns along with a
twelve month average.
Chairman Baliga inquired whether GPS procedures were being factored in at this
point. Mr. Jackson noted that some procedures have been published. They will
be checked as the aircraft fleet begins using them more frequently.
Noise Hotline Report
There were 1,118 total complaints for the 3rd quarter, with 395 being nighttime.
The complaints came from 95 different complainants. 1076 were from Chicago
with 815 of those coming from a single address. Complaints can be registered
either by phone or online.

NEW BUSINESS
A. Approval of Resolution 2013-B
Resolution 2013-B identifies the next group of homes included in the sound
insulation. Ms. Conwell presented the Resolution.
Chairman Baliga asked for clarification on how block rounding is determined. Mr.
Frame responded that the legal block based on addresses is what is used. If a
qualified dwelling is located on the even side of the street, all those homes on
that side of the block are included as are those on the odd side of that block.
Chairman Baliga asked if there were no further questions that a motion to adopt
Resolution 2013-B be made. A motion was made by Ms. Conwell and seconded
by Mr. Lihosit. The Resolution was adopted.
