MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
March 27, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, March 27, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois. The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 10:22 A.M.
Present were Members Lee Gould, Maribeth Vander Weele and Michael Holewinski. Chairman
Aaron Jaffe participated via telephone.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES –
Member Vander Weele moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its
Regular Meeting of February 26, 2014. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
The Administrator stated that over 4,258 locations have been licensed, with 4,398 “live
locations”. There are over 15,995 machines in these locations. Mark reminded the terminal
operators about illegal gambling pertaining to March Madness. Discipline and revocations will
be considered in the future for illegal gambling.
BOARD POLICY ITEM –
•

Proposed Amendments to Video Gaming Rules: Sections 230, 240 and 270
Member Holewinski moved to amend sections 230, 240 and 270 of the Board’s
rules on video gaming regarding Duties of Licensed Manufacturers, Distributors
and Video Gaming Locations by authorizing staff to submit a proposal for First
Notice Filing with the Secretary of State upon final review and approval by the
Administrator.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by
roll call vote.
SUPPLIER, DISTRIBUTOR AND MANUFACTURER LICENSEE ITEMS –
•

Chicago Gaming Company: Distributor and manufacturer Licensee Renewal
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved to renew the Distributor and
Manufacturer Licenses of Chicago Gaming Company, effective March 27, 2014,
for a term of one year, expiring in March 2015.
Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.

Windy City Gaming LLC – Terminal Operator License Renewal

DISCIPLINARY COMPLAINTS –


Hayden Kolodey – Terminal Handler
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved to issue a Disciplinary
Complaint to revoke the Terminal Handler license held by Hayden Kolodey due
to his admission of theft while employed by a Licensed Terminal Operator, said
action to take effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaint unless he
files an Answer within that time period.
Member Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
