ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD

NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Tuesday, May 18, 2010 a Regular Meeting of the Illinois Gaming Board (“Board”) was held
in the conference room of Jumer’s Casino Rock Island in Rock Island, Illinois.
On Tuesday, May 18, 2010 at approximately 10:40 A.M. the following Board Members were
present: Chairman Jaffe, Members Charles Gardner, Eugene Winkler, Joseph Moore and James
Sullivan. At 11:05 a.m. on Tuesday, May 18, 2010, Chairman Jaffe called the meeting to order.
Pursuant to Section 2(c), paragraphs (1), (11), (14) and (21) of the Open Meetings Act and
Section 6(d) of the Riverboat Gambling Act, Member Gardner moved that the Board retire to
Closed Session to discuss the items listed under Closed Session on today’s agenda and
relating to the following subject matters:

Investigations concerning applicants and licensees; and

Member Sullivan seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by voice
vote. The Board recessed at 11:35 A.M and reconvene into Closed Session at 12:05 P.M. At
3:30 P.M. Member Winkler moved to go into Open Session in order to tour the gaming operation
of Jumer’s Casino.
On Wednesday, May 19, 2010 the Closed Session reconvened at 9:13 a.m. Present were the
following Board Members: Chairman Jaffe, Members Charles Gardner, Eugene Winkler, James
Sullivan and Joseph Moore.
The Board remained in Closed Session until approximately 9:27 a.m. The Board recessed and
then went into open session at 10:05 a.m. Chairman Jaffe, Members Charles Gardner, Eugene
Winkler, James Sullivan and Joseph Moore were present.

PUBLIC COMMENTARY –
Dennis Pauley, Mayor of Rock Island –
Mayor Pauley addressed the Board staff and commended the facility and staff of Jumer’s Casino
Rock Island. The Mayor stated that Jumer’s Casino has done an abundance for the community

2

 of Rock Island. Gaming revenue has become a pivotal part in the capital improvement of Rock
Island.
Ray Hamilton, President of Vietnam Veterans of America Chapter 299 –
Mr. Hamilton addressed the Board and staff. A special project is underway in which the Chapter
is involved and is recognizing John Baker a veteran from the City of Rock Island. A bridge in
the City of Rock Island is named after John Baker. Jumer’s Casino has arranged for a formal
tribute to John Baker and the past and present veterans of Rock Island. A monument was erected
and Jumer’s sponsored the project. For the cost of the monument, Jumer’s donated $10,000 for
the project and donated a piece of property on the front lawn of the casino for the monument to
rest.
