Transmitting a Communication, dated April 28, 2005 from
ROBERT MALDONADO, County Commissioner
In January of 2005, I directed my staff to prepare an ordinance entitled the "Cook County Motor Vehicle
Weight Tax" in order to help bridge an unprecedented budget gap. The purpose of the tax was twofold:
to raise funds while discouraging consumers in Cook County from purchasing larger motor vehicles. The
legislation also aimed to protect public health and safety as well as our public investment in the Cook
County highway system. However, keeping in the spirit of budget negotiations, I shelved the proposal
until such time the Board might be receptive to its adoption. In the wake of Comm issioner Quigley's
announcement that he favors such a tax I am now introducing this item.

ROBERT MALDONADO, County Commissioner
PROPOSED ORDINANCE

COOK COUNTY MOTOR VEHICLE WEIGHT TAX
WHEREAS, according to the World Health Organization, larger passenger motor vehicles, particularly
Sport Utility Vehicles, known as SUVs, pose a serious health and environmental impact that will become
the world's third most common cause of death and disability by the year 2020; and
WHEREAS, despite dire warnings, SUV sales have exploded on the U.S. market, representing 25% of
new passenger vehicles sold in the U.S., and their sales continue to soar; and
WHEREAS, in addition to the obvious health and environmental concerns, the Board of Commissioners
further finds that larger passenger motor vehicles, because of their size, contribute to greater wear upon
the highway system within Cook County than smaller passenger motor vehicles, resulting in increased
street maintenance and repair costs; and
WHEREAS, the County Highway Department spends approximately $17 million annually to provide an
adequate level of preventive and corrective maintenance of the highway facilities; and
WHEREAS, in the same way the Cook County cigarette tax discourages consumers from smoking,
consumers in Cook County should be discouraged from purchasing SUVs for the protection of the public
health and safety and for the protection of the public investment in the Cook County highway system; and
WHEREAS, the County of Cook is a home rule unit of local government pursuant to Article VII, Section
6(a) of the 1970 Illinois Constitution; and
WHEREAS, Illinois law (55 ILCS ยง5/5-1009) permits home rule units to impose " taxes not based on the
selling or purchase price or gross receipts from the use, sale or purchase of tangible personal property";
and
WHEREAS, the Cook County Board of Commissioners has determined that a County tax not based upon
the selling or purchase price or gross receipts from the use, sale or purchase thereof should be imposed
upon the larger passenger vehicles within Cook County.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED AS FOLLOWS:
SECTION 1.

TITLE.

This Ordinance shall be known and may be cited as the "Cook County Motor Vehicle Weight Tax". The
tax herein imposed is in addition to all other taxes imposed by the County of Cook, the State of Illinois or
any municipal corporation or political subdi vision thereof.

1416

 JOURNAL OF PROCEEDINGS FOR MAY 3, 2005

SECTION 2.

DEFINITIONS.

For the purposes of this Ordinance, unless the context otherwise requires, the following words or terms
shall have the meanings respectively applied to them:
A. "Commercial motor vehicle" means any vehicle operated for the transportation of
persons or property in the furtherance of a business.
B. " Department" means the Department of Revenue in the Bureau of Finance of Cook
County.
C. " Motor vehicle" means any vehicle that is self-propelled, otherwise than by the
muscular power of humans or animals, and that is designed for the transportation of
persons, except such vehicles as run on rails or tracks.
D. " Purchased at retail" means obtained for use or consumption in exchange for a
valuable consideration, whether in the form of money, credits, barter or any other nature,
and not for resale.
E. " Purchaser" means any person who purchased at retail.
F. " Retailer" means a person who sells or offers for sale, for use or consumption and not
for resale.

SECTION 3.

TAX IMPOSED; EXEMPTIONS.

Commencing on the effective date of this Ordinance, a tax, in addition to any and all other taxes, in the
amount of $50 is imposed upon every motor vehicle with a model year of 1999 and prior, and with a curb
weight of at least 4,500 pounds, and a tax, in addition to any and all other taxes, in the amount of $100 is
imposed upon every motor vehicle with a model year of 2000 and following, and with a curb weight of at
least 4,500 pounds, as determined by the vehicle' s manufacturer, which is purchased at retail from a
retailer and which is titled or registered at a location wi thin the corporate limits of Cook County with an
agency of the state government. The liability for payment of the tax shall be borne by the purchaser.
Commercial vehicles, vehicles owned and operated upon the public streets of Cook County by the United
States government or any agency thereof, or by the State of Illinois or any department thereof, or by any
political subdivision, public or municipal corporation of the State of Jllinois or any department or other
agency of such corporation, or by the American Red Cross, all vehicles owned and operated by churches
in connection with the authorized activities of such institutions and all vehicles registered under Section
3-6 16 of the Illinois Vehicle Code shall be exempt from the tax.

SECTION 4.

COLLECTION OFT AX BY RETAILER.

Every retailer located within Cook County shall have the duty to collect and account for said tax from
each purchaser at the time that the consideration for such purchase is paid.

SECTION 5.

COLLECTION OF TAX BY DEPARTMENT.

The Department shall be responsible for collecting the tax from purchasers who purchase their motor
vehicles from retailers located outside of Cook County.

141 7

 JOURNAL OF PROCEEDINGS FOR MAY 3, 2005

SECTION 6.

BOOKS AND RECORDS.

Every retailer within Cook County, shall jointly and severally have the duty to maintain complete and
accurate books, records and accounts showing the gross receipts for sales of motor vehicles with a curb
weight of at least 4,500 pounds and the taxes collected each day, which shall be made available to the
Department for examination and for audit by the Department upon reasonable notice and during normal
business hours.

SECTION 7.

REGISTRATION OF OWNERS AND OPERATORS.

Every retailer within the County of Cook shall register with the Department no later than twenty (20) days
after the adoption of this Ordinance or the commencement of business.
Retailers within the County of Cook shall file a sworn tax return on a monthly basis with the Department
showing gross sales of motor vehicles with a curb weight of at least 4,500 pounds during the preceding
monthly period, upon forms prescribed by the Department. Each tax return of sales of motor vehicles
with a curb weight of at least 4,500 pounds shall be accompanied by a remittance to the County of Cook
of all taxes imposed by this Ordinance which is due and owing for the period of time to which the tax
return applies.
The remittance and tax return shall be filed with the Department on the last day of the month following
the month which the tax return and remittance covers. A tax return properly addressed to the Department
which is postmarked on or before the last day of the month shall be considered timely filed. The
remittance shall be made payable to the Cook County Department of Revenue. The tax return shall also
be accompanied with a copy of the return filed with the Illinois Department of Revenue for sales within
Cook County covering the same reporting period.

SECTION 8.

RULE MAKING.

(a) The Department may prescribe reasonable rules, definitions, and regulations not inconsistent with this
Ordinance necessary to carry out the duties imposed upon it by this Ordinance. Such rules, definitions,
and regulations shall include, but not be limited to, reasonable procedures consistent with existing
practices of retailers for collection and remittance of the tax herein levied upon the purchaser of a motor
vehicle with a curb weight of at least 4,500 pounds.
(b) The Department shall have the authority to appoint retailers or any other person within or without the
County of Cook as agents for the collection and remittance of the tax herein levied. The Department is
hereby authorized to grant a commission not exceeding one percent (!%) of the tax due under this
Ordinance to such agent for services rendered in connection with the tax herein levied, provided said tax
is remitted, in full, by the due date.

SECTION 9.

PENALTIES.

Any violation of this Ordinance shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $100.00 and not more than
$1,000.00 or imprisonment for a period not to exceed six (6) months, or by both such fine and
imprisonment. It shall be deemed a violation of this Ordinance for any person knowingly to furnish false
or inaccurate infonnation or fail to file the tax return and remittance as required herein. Criminal
prosecutions pursuant to this Ordinance shall in no way bar the right of Cook County to institute civil
proceedings to recover delinquent taxes, interest and penalties due and owing, as well as costs incurred
for such proceeding. Civil penalties and interest assessed pursuant to this Ordinance shall be computed at
the rate provided by the Cook County Uniform Penalty, Interest and Procedures Ordinance.

SECTION 10. TRANSMITTAL OF EXCESS TAX COLLECTIONS.
If any person collects an amount in excess of the tax imposed by this Ordinance, but which amount is
purported to be a collection thereof, and does not return the same to the purchaser who paid the tax, the
person who collected the tax shall account for and pay over those excess amounts to Cook County along
with the tax properly collected.
SECTION 11. SEVERABILITY.

If any one or more of the provisions of this Ordinance is declared unconstitutional or the application
thereof is held invalid, the validity of the remainder of this Ordinance and the application of such
provisions to other persons and circumstances shall not be affected.
SECTION 12. CONFIDENTIALITY.
All infonnation received by the Department from returns filed pursuant to this Ordinance or from any
investigations conducted pursuant to this Ordinance shall be confidential, except for use for official
County purposes or if required to be disclosed by law.
SECTION 13. EFFECTIVE DATE.
This Ordinance shall take full force and effect on June 1, 2005.

vacant properties to determine comparables and the parcel includes a large church; the second license
appraiser used comparables of developed properties within the area.
Commissioner Maldonado asked whether the parcel is improved or unimproved.
Superintendent Kos replied that the parcel is improved.
Commissioner Goonan reiterated that the two different appraisers are large in variation. The County had
to distinguish between developed property appraisals versus undeveloped property appraisals. That
would explain the large discrepancy between the eight dollars per square footage and the one dollar and
fifty cents per square footage.

Superintendent Kos replied that whenever there are two appraisals, the Highway Department accepts the
highest appraisal. However, if there is a large difference as in this appraisal, the Highway Department
utilizes a review appraiser who is not associated with the two certified appraisers who conduct the review
for the Highway Department. Superintendent Kos further stated that the Highway Department does not
have a certified appraiser on staff.
Chainnan Hansen asked for the names of the three appraisers.
Superintendent Kos replied that the three appraisers are Ronald S. Lipman, Jennifer L. Dounias and the
Review Appraiser, Charles A. Southcomb.

Superintendent Kos replied that the roadway has one lane in each direction. The proposed scope is to
remove and replace the roadway with three lanes, one lane in each direction and a center turning lane that
will widen the roadway. Additionally, the Highway Department is installing curbs and gutters. The
parcel of land in Palos Hills has dips, valleys, ravines and thirty-three feet of right-of-way by township
dedication from one hundred years ago. The Highway Department is acquiring twenty feet of land in this
particular area.

and the parcel is slightly less than three acres. The Highway Depa1tment is purchasing .15 of an acre
which is approximately one sixth of an acre. Along the frontage are trees, scrubs, fancy gate works,
bricks, and light fixtures to highlight and illuminate the driveways. The cost of the property includes
damages that will be incun-ed for removal of trees, scrubs, fancy gate works, bricks and driveways in a
residential area.

270718

OFFICE OF CAPITAL PLANNING AND POLICY, by Michael E. LaMont, Director,
transmitting a Communication, dated March 30, 2005:
Transmitted herewith for your approval is a request for authorization to enter into a
professional services contract with DuSable, Inc. for design and construction administration
services for the Oak Forest Hospital of Cook County Water Main Survey and Valve
Replacement Project at the Oak Forest Hospital of Cook County Campus. It is respectfully
requested that this Honorable Body approve this request.
Reason:

This project provides for an operational analysis of the domestic water
distribution system that leads from the Halsted Pumping Station to and through
the hospital campus. Many of the existing valves, vaults, and water mains that
are part of this system were installed in the l 940 's, and are inoperable or in need
of repairs. The scope of this project will be to repair and/or replace equipment as
needed, to better service the needs of this campus and comply with current codes.

Estimated Fiscal Impact: $547,091.00. Bond Issue (33000 Account).
Sufficient funds have been appropriated to cover this request.

*Referred to the Construction Committee on 04/19/05.
Commissioner Hansen asked whether the eleven miles of water main are included in the $3.3 million
project cost.
Michael E. LaMont, Director, Office of Capital Planning and Policy, replied that the consultant will
identify main-line piping to be replaced or repaired for the system. Mr. LaMont further stated that the
consultant was present to answer any questions for the Commissioners.
R. Murty Yedavalli, P.E., F-ASCE President, DuSable, Inc., replied that the contract includes eleven
miles of tracing and locating of the piping. Further, Mr. Y edavalli stated that he has experienced
difficulties in locating seven miles of pipe because of the I-57 construction project. Additionally, Mr.
Yedavalli stated that he does not expect any of the main-line piping to have problems. In conclusion, Mr.
Yedavalli stated that entire piping within the campus will be replaced.
Commissioner Hansen asked whether the committee was seeking approval of the $3 .3 million
construction cost or the $547,091.00 desig n fee.
Mr. LaMont replied that the $547,09 1.00 is the desig n fee and the $3.3 million is the estimated cost of
construction calculated by the Office of Capital Planning and Policy based on previous studies that were
done for the replacement of valve vaults and the main-line piping.

Commissioner Hansen asked for a report once the investigation is completed.

Commissioners.

contingency fees, percentage-wise, are so varied, and she also inquired as to the discrepancies within the
contracts.

at the discretion of President Stroger. President Stroger selects finns that are qualified to provide these

Your Committee has considered the following item and upon adoption of this report, the
recommendations are as follows:
Illinois State Representative Julie Hamos, Chainnan of the Special Transportation Committee on
Regional Transportation, Illinois House of Representatives, testified that transportation is both a regional
and state-wide issue. She further stated that the Committee on Regional Transportation received
assistance from the University of Illinoi s at Chicago's Urban Transportation Center in analyzing data
from the current funding fonnula a summary of which is submitted with th is report.
Representative Harnos noted that the 1983 funding formula has no relationship to transit perfonnance.
This funding formula was required to address a crisis that occurred in this region. In 1983 the legislature
designed a solution as to how to divide a large sum of money for transit. A portion of the sales tax would
fund the government subsidy that is associated with each trip. The legislature decided that Cook County
would have a one percent sales tax that would be allocated to the RT A for distribution among three transit
agencies. In the collar counties one-fourth-of-one percent sales tax would be allocated to the RTA for
Metra and Pace. The CTA receives all sales taxes from Chicago. Cook County suburbs divide the sum
among the CTA, METRA and PACE. The suburban Cook County dollars generated are allocated as
follows: Metra receives fifty-five percent, CTA receives thirty percent, and Pace receives fifteen percent.
Fifty-four percent of the total of all sales tax revenues come from Suburban Cook County.
There are a few imbalances concerning where passengers are, which sub-regions are being served and
where the money is being allocated.
Suburban Cook County subsidizes the services for both Chicago and the collar counties. There is $100
million of Suburban Cook sales taxes that go to collar county services.
The goal is to understand why the CTA believes it is not receiving adequate dollars out of the existing
funding formula. The funding formula, developed in 1983, does not support the full range of CTA
services. The CTA suggested that the funding formula should be connected more to transit service. The
current sales tax base for regional mass transit is expended. Without additional revenues, no one can
move forward.
When the Federal Reauthorization Bill is decided, the RTA will know which expansion projects are being
funded. Also to be determined is how many operating dollars will be needed to subsidize the expansions,
and when and how this subsidy will occur. The legislature will have to devise a new funding fonnu la.
The Committee on Regional Transportation plans to travel and create a consensus in the region and the
Cook County Board should be a part of the process.
One of the short tenn goals is to provide more state and federal dollars in para-transit services for the
disabled. This is a very important public policy goal. The State should more fully support this endeavor.
Some of the transit service, for example, transportation to doctors' offices, should be claimed under the
Medicaid program so that the federal government can supply fifty-percent of the transportation cost. The
restructuring of the para-transit system will relieve the burden placed on CTA and PACE budgets. If
para-transit were transferred to a state responsibility, the short term crisis at the CTA would be solved.
Commissioner Murphy inquired whether Representative Harnos has observed the need for public
transportation in the south end of Cook County.
Representative Harnos stated that Metra has an ambitious plan for new stations in the south suburbs. In
Will County three new stations will be built for the south-east extension; two new stations will be built
for the south-west extension.

WHEREAS, the Cook County Board of Commissioners is responsible for the establishment of an annual
operating budget for Cook County Government; and
WHEREAS, overtime expenses are budgeted in the County' s annual appropriation bill; and
WHEREAS, ove1time expenditures increase as a result of many factors including but not limited to
staffing shortages, emergencies and increased workload; and
WHEREAS, while expenditures have increased in recent years, the administration has worked diligently
in decreasing the total hours of overtime assigned and granted; and
WHEREAS, the various County agencies, departments and bureaus are responsible for managing their
overtime needs and expenses; and
WHEREAS, each employee has a direct supervisor responsible for authorizing overtime expenditures;
and
WHEREAS, each department has financial staff overseeing expenditures; and

1530

 JOURNAL OF PROCEEDfNGS FOR MAY 3, 2005

WHEREAS, there are legitimate reasons for overtime expenditures, especially in the areas of public
health and public safety; and
WHEREAS, by ensuring that employees are not working excessive overtime, the potential for risk to
employees, patients, detainees and others is diminished; and
WHEREAS, in many instances, overtime rules are governed by collective bargaining agreements; and
WHEREAS, many of these agreements stipulate that no employee shall be entitled to overtime before
having worked a full week; and
WHEREAS, the Cook County Board of Commissioners seeks to further safeguard our employees,
patients, detainees and others and to control overtime expenditures as much as possible.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Cook County Board of Commissioners hereby
establishes the following policy to guide overtime expenditures and to protect the employees, patients,
detainees and citizens of Cook County:
That all supervisors are required to provide a written report bi-monthly to their department head
relative to staffing needs. This report shall detail the number of vacancies and their effect on
operations as well as the expected overtime needs for the period of the report; and
That all department heads, bureau chiefs and elected officials immediately assign a member(s) of
their financial team to oversee overtime expenses and that this individual(s), along with an
employee's immediate supervisor, shall provide written authorization prior to an employee being
assigned or granted overtime; and
That this written authorization shall be forwarded to the departmen t head, bureau chief and/or
elected official on a bi-monthly basis; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that no Cook County employee shall be allowed to work more than 20
hours in overtime in any seven day period with the exception of emergency situations where the bureau
chief or elected official will be required to provide written authorization; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that in any one fiscal year no Cook County employee shall be allowed
to work more than 624 hours (30% of 2080 full-time hours) in overtime with the exception of emergency
situations where the bureau chief or elected official will be required to provide written authorization; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that these supervisors and managers are expected to adhere to this
policy and will be held accountable for unauthorized overtime expenditures within their respective
departments; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the County ' s labor team is directed to address any matters
pertaining to overtime during the negotiating process and make recommendations that ensure fairness to
both the County' s employees and taxpayers.
This policy shall go into effect immediately upon passage.
