Chicago Low-lncome Housing Trust Fund Update
I t the Chicago Low-Income Housing Trust Fund hosted more than 300
to mark 25 yearc of success in creating affordable housing opportunities for Chicago's
low-income residents. The occasion brought together non-profit organizations, public offìcials,
landlords, the research and foundation communities, as well as private developers in a warm
tribute to the past, present and future of the Trust Fund. The celebration recognized the
Trust Fund's partners in this work who have contributed to past successes and remain
vitally imponant to the future of the City and the well-being of its lower-income residents.

On September

leaders

Honored at the event \Mere Jackie Taylor Holsten and
Peter Holsten of the Holsten Real Estate Development
Corporation, who received the Douglas C. Dobmeyer
Partnership Award for their work in creating affordable
housing opportunities, including their current project-the preservation of the Lawson House YMCA on
the Near North Side.

The contributions of Thomas J. McNulry and the attorneys at Neal Gerber and Eisenberg
LLP were also recognized. Mr. McNulty has provided 25 yexs of volunteer service to the
Ciry and the Trust Fund as its president, and the firm provides pro bono legal counsel to the
Trust Fund.
The Chicago Low-Income Housing Trust Fund works to bridge the gap between the cost of
market-rate rental housing and the rate a low-income household is able to pay. Its Rental
Subsidy Program (RSP) is the largest municipally funded rental assistance program for lowincome households in the nation. Currently there are nearly 2,800 households benefiting
from the RSP living in more than 600 properties across the city.

Large Lot Program Expands to East Garfield Park
Building on the successful launch of the Large Lot Pilot Program in Englewood,'Washington Park and'!Øoodlawn this past March, DPD expanded the program to the East Garfield
Park community startingJuly 1, enabling homeowners, block clubs and non-profit groups
to purchase City-owned land for $l per parcel.

To qualify for the program,

applicants must: own property on the same block as the
parcel to be acquired; be current on property taxes; end have no financial obligations to the
City. Lots proposed for purchase from the city must be vacant and zoned residential (R).
Properties are sold "as is" via quit claim deed. Eligible applicants may purchase up ro rwo
parcels under the program

More than 400 property owners submitted applications in the first round of the program. In
August the Chicago Plan Commission authorized the sale of 322 parcels, clearing the way for
City Council approval later this year.

In July over 400 East Garfield Park lots were made available in the second round of the
program. In response, 319 purchase applications were submitted and are now being reviewed by DPD staff.
The Large Lot Program is the first initiative launched by the City under its new Five-Year
Housing Plan for 2014-2018.
