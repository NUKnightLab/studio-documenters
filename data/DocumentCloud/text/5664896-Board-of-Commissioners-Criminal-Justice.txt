PUBLIC TESTIMONY

Authorization as a public speaker shall only be granted to those individuals who have submitted in writing,
their name, address, subject matter, and organization (if any) to the Secretary 24 hours in advance of the
meeting. Duly authorized public speakers shall be called upon to deliver testimony at a time specified in
the meeting agenda. Authorized public speakers who are not present during the specified time for public
testimony will forfeit their allotted time to speak at the meeting. Public testimony must be germane to a
specific item(s) on the meeting agenda, and the testimony must not exceed three minutes; the Secretary
will keep track of the time and advise when the time for public testimony has expired. Persons authorized
to provide public testimony shall not use vulgar, abusive, or otherwise inappropriate language when
addressing the Board; failure to act appropriately; failure to speak to an item that is germane to the
meeting, or failure to adhere to the time requirements may result in expulsion from the meeting and/or
disqualify the person from providing future testimony.

PROPOSED RESOLUTION
CREATING A FUNERAL AND CEMETERY VIOLENCE TASKFORCE
WHEREAS, violence at funerals across Cook County have reached an unacceptable level, and

WHEREAS, celebrating the life of a loved one is an important way to say goodbye for the families, and
WHEREAS, all families whose loved ones are killed should be able to mourn their loved ones in peace,
and
WHEREAS, many Cook County residents bury their loved ones in cemeteries in unincorporated areas of
the County, and
WHEREAS, in early 2018, shootings and violence at funerals and cemeteries spiked, and
WHEREAS, from 2016 to February 2018, more than 1,500 people have been killed in Chicago, so this
issue affects many Cook County residents, and
WHEREAS, the County should direct any and all available resources to fight the scourge of retaliatory
shootings at funerals, funeral processions, and cemeteries, and
WHEREAS, Cook County shall create a Task Force to meet and discuss potential solutions to this
problem.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the President and the Cook County Board of
Commissioners, that there be established a Task Force to explore the issues and offer recommendations
surrounding retaliatory funeral shootings, and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, by the President and the Cook County Board of Commissioners that
the Task Force created herein shall consist of seven members, appointed in the following manner:

(1) A designee of the Cook County Sheriff shall chair the Task Force
(2) A designee of the Cook County Stateâ€™s Attorney
(3) A designee of the Superintendent of the Chicago Police Department
(4) A designee of the West Suburban Chiefs of Police
(5) A designee of the South Suburban Chiefs of Police
(6) A member of the Cook County Funeral Directors Association
(7) A cemetery owner
