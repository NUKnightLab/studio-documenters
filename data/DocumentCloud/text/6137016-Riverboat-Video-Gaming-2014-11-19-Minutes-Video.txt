MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - VIDEO
OPEN SESSION
November 20, 2014
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, November 20, 2014 a Regular Meeting - Video of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois. The Open Session on Video Gaming started at approximately 10:45 A.M.
Present were Chairman Aaron Jaffe, Lee Gould and Michael Holewinski.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES – Open Session
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of October 27 2014. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATOR’S REPORT –
Administrator Ostrowski stated that there are 18,813 machines running at 4,604 locations. The
equivalent to positions at 16 casinos.

• WTS Investments, LLC

period of one year, expiring in November 2015.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.

ADMINISTRATIVE REQUESTS FOR HEARING & REQUESTS TO REAPPLY:
• Requests for Hearing

2

 Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved to deny the requests for
hearing from VO&J Investments, LLC and Ndina, Ltd. as they have failed to
establish a prima facie case for hearing; and deny the request from Freedom Oil
Company as moot.
Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
•

Requests to Reapply
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved to deny the requests to
reapply from Chana Tap, Inc., Springfield Airport Authority and Kenneth E. King
and deny the request from Expresslane, Inc. as moot.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.

DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS:
• Pam Kaye, Inc. d/b/a Dunne Inn Home Again
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved to issue a Disciplinary
Complaint to revoke the Video Gaming License held by Pam Kaye, Inc. due to its
failure to secure video gaming terminals during construction of the location, said
action to take effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaint unless a $1,000
fine is paid or an Answer is properly filed within that time period.
Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion
unanimously by roll call vote.
•

Loren M. Robinson d/b/a Giovanni’s Caterers
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved to issue a Disciplinary
Complaint to revoke the Video Gaming License held by Loren M. Robinson d/b/a
Giovanni’s Caterers for its failure to comply with the Video Gaming Act, said action
to take effect 21 days from the date of service of the Complaint unless an Answer is
properly filed within that time period.
Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously
by roll call vote.

•

Beer Barrel Pub, Inc.
Based on staff’s further investigation, Member Gould moved to rescind the
Disciplinary Complaint DC-V-14-37 issued at the October 2014 meeting against Beer
Barrel Pub, Inc.

3

 Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll
call vote.
