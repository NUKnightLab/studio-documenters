MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING - RIVERBOAT
OPEN SESSION
October 24, 2013
ILLINOIS GAMING BOARD
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS
NOTE: ITEMS IN BOLDFACE PRINT REFLECT OFFICIAL BOARD ACTIONS
On Thursday, October 24, 2013 a Regular Meeting - Riverboat of the Illinois Gaming Board
(“Board”) was held on the 5th floor at 160 N. LaSalle at the Michael J. Bilandic Building,
Chicago, Illinois at 10:10 A.M. Present were the following: Members Maribeth Vander Weele,
Lee Gould, Michael Holewinski and Chairman Aaron Jaffe.
APPROVAL OF MINUTES
Member Gould moved that the Board approve the open session minutes of its Regular
Meeting of September 19, 2013. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTATOR’S REPORT –
Administrator Ostrowski commented on the Open Session Meeting of September 19, 2013
referencing 24 hour gaming. He stated that a number of groups spoke in opposing 24 hour
gaming a long with some who were in favor of 24 hour gaming. He announced that there will be
no casino in the State of Illinois that will operate on a 24 hour schedule. The Administrator also
mentioned the fact that there was a vendor diversity fair that was held in Metropolis Illinois
earlier in September and the opportunities for minority vendors were quite promising.
Administrator Ostrowski also indicated there would be no December 2013 Riverboat Board
meeting.
BOARD POLICY ITEM
Member Holewinski moved to authorize staff to amend Section 660 of the Board’s rules on
Riverboat Gambling regarding Minimum Standards for Electronic Gaming Devices by
submitting a proposal for First Notice Filing with the Secretary of State upon final review
and approval by the Administrator. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved
the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
OCCUPATIONAL LICENSE APPROVAL AND DENIALS –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Vander Weele moved that the Board find suitable and
approve 42 applications for Level 2 Occupational Licenses and 106 applications for Level 3
Occupational Licenses and that the Administrator issue a Notice of Denial to Ghirmai
Desu. Member Gould seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll
call vote.

 PROPOSED COMPLAINTS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS –
Tourrie Frazier:
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Gould moved that the Board issue a Disciplinary
Complaint and revoke the Occupational License of Tourrie Frazier, said action to take
effect twenty-one days from the date of service of the Complaint, unless the licensee files an
Answer within that time period. Member Holewinski seconded the motion. The Board
approved the motion unanimously by roll call vote.
ADMINISTRATIVE HEARINGS –
Based on staff’s investigation, Member Holewinski moved that the Board deny the request for
hearing from Justin McGown, as he has failed to establish a prima facie case. Member
Vander Weele seconded the motion. The Board approved the motion unanimously by roll call
vote.

At 10:20 A.M. Member Holewinski motioned to adjourn and Member Vander Weele seconded
the motion. All Members voted in favor of adjournment.
Respectfully submitted,
Mary C. Boruta
Secretary to the Administrator
