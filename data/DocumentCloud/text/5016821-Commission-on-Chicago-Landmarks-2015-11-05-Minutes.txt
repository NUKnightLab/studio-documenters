The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:
1. 111 E. Cermak Road
3rd Ward
Motor Row District
Proposed new construction on a vacant lot in the Motor Row District as part of a
larger hotel project.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. As proposed, no projecting canopies will be installed on the exterior of the
new structure along Michigan and operable door systems shall be incorporated
at the storefronts along Michigan and Cermak where possible;
2. Exterior architectural lighting is proposed for the Michigan Avenue and
Cermak Road elevations of the new building. The lighting fixtures above the
2nd floor level shall be located to minimize their visibility from the public
rights-of-way and details and fixture cut sheets shall be submitted with the
permit application. A lighting mockup may be required prior to final
approval;
3. Dimensioned details of the proposed windows and storefronts shall be
submitted with the permit plans. Storefront elements shall have a dark,
factory-applied finish;
4. The proposed spandrel glass on the Cermak and Michigan elevations of the 3story portion shall be modified to a more transparent material, such as
translucent glass or glass which incorporates a frit pattern to be submitted for
staff review and approval;
5. The applicant may propose a cladding treatment of the 3-story base more in
keeping with the design vocabulary of the high-rise tower subject to staff
review and approval;
6. Material samples are approved as submitted; and
7. All previously approved conditions related to the restoration and renovation of
the historic building at 2215 S. Michigan shall remain in effect.

 2. 800 N. Clark
2nd Ward
Bush Temple of Music
Proposed new rooftop deck and elevator and stair enclosures atop the existing 6-story
building as part of the rehabilitation of the property for residential use.
Action:

The Committee voted unanimously to defer the project to the next PRC
meeting to have the applicant address questions from Committee members
regarding the scope of work.

3. 1001 W. Fulton Market
27th Ward
Fulton-Randolph Market District
Proposed new 3-story masonry building with an attached 1-story rear garage and roof
deck with commercial space on the first floor and residential space above.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. A section drawing and product specifications of the proposed elevator shall be
submitted with the permit plans, indicating that, as proposed, the override will
not extend above the east parapet;
2. Proposed window and storefront details shall be submitted with the permit
application. All muntins are to have a substantial exterior profile, storefronts
are to have a dark, factory-applied finish and all glass is to be clear vision
glass; and,
3. The applicant shall provide face brick, mortar and limestone samples for
Historic Preservation staff review and approval prior to permit submittal. The
brick and stone colors, sizes and textures shall match typical colors and
textures found within the district.
4. 2119 W. LeMoyne
2nd Ward
Wicker Park District
Proposed new 1-story rear addition to a 3-story masonry single-family residence and
a new 1-story 3-car garage with covered patio.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:

1. The new 1-story rear addition and retention of the historic masonry wall at the
west property line are approved as proposed. The rear addition requires a
zoning variation and/or adjustment, and the Commission takes no position
regarding any requested variance/adjustment relative to the zoning code
requirements.
2. Given the new 3-car garage and covered patio are setback more than 57’ from
the front property line, and that there is no room for a new garage in the rear
yard facing the rear alley due to existing historic coach houses on this
property, the new garage, proposed to be clad in common brick, is approved
as proposed with drawings dated 9/1/15;
3. Cut sheets for the garage doors shall be included in the permit plans; and,
4. The new 5’-tall open metal fence and posts enclosing the front yard is
approved as proposed, and the proposed 6’-tall and 7’-tall wood fence along
the side alley shall be painted or stained (fence heights measured from
sidewalk grade).
