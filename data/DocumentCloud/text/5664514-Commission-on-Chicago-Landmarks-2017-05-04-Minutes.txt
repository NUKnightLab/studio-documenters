The following projects were reviewed by the PRC:
1. 17 E. Monroe
42nd Ward
Palmer House Hotel
Proposed replacement of an existing storefront on Monroe Street elevation with a
revolving door.
Action:

Approved unanimously with the following conditions:
1. As proposed, the new revolving door shall be as transparent as
possible and the metal cladding shall match adjacent storefront colors
and finishes. Details of the new door and storefront shall be submitted
with the permit application;
2. Historic Preservation staff shall review and approve any new flooring
samples to confirm that they match the existing; and,
3. The applicants shall install a minimal floor barrier or rope to prevent
visitors from mistaking the historic decorative C.D. Peacock doors for
an operable entrance. Drawings/details of the barrier shall be
submitted with this permit plans.

2. 911 W. Randolph
27th Ward
Futon-Randolph Market District

storefronts, new windows, and a new rooftop addition and deck.

new one-over-one, double-hung, clad-wood windows. The
