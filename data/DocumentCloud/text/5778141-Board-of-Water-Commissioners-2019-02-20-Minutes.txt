The Board of Water Commissioners for the City of Detroit, Water and Sewerage
Department authorizes the Director to enter into Contract No. 6001844, “Green
Stormwater Infrastructure Program Management” (CS-1884A) with OHM
Advisors (Consultant), at a cost not-to-exceed, $12,000,000.00 for a
duration of five (5) years, with the option of two (2) one-year extensions to
be mutually agreed upon by both parties, and also authorizes the Director to
take such other action as may be necessary to accomplish the intent of this vote.
Sponsors:

Mobley

A motion was made by Commissioner Lane Coleman, seconded by
Commissioner Linda Forte that this Contract be approved. The motion carried
by a unanimous vote.

PUBLIC COMMENTS (2 minutes per speaker)
a. Daryl Edwards came before the board regarding sewage in his basement.
b. Renee Palmer of 19172 Grand River came before the board seeking assistance on
how to obtain a final real estate closing meter read.
c. William Davis stated that the department should seek a more aggressive approach
to hire city employees as oppose to contracting work in the city.
d. Creighton Rippy of 20062 John R came before the board about a $5,000 monthly
increase on his water bill.
e. Darrell Harris of 1547 Clairmount came before the board about a manhole cave-in
that caused sewage backup at his home.
f. Russ Bellant came before the board with several matters: 1) to thank Theresa
Redden-Flennoy of Billing and Collections for outstanding customer service assistance;
2) lack of follow-up on drainage appeals; and 3) request speaker for the 11th Precinct
community meeting on March 18.
g. Michelle George of National Action Network (NAN) came before the board to ask
how many shutoffs are done monthly by DWSD.
h. Aaron McIver of 19929 Warrington asked to explain why his bill has quadrupled
within the last two years.
i. Lory Parks of the National Action Network came before the board with several
concerns: 1) what other organs were found in the water system; 2) need for an
affordable water plan; 3) what qualifications are required to be a water commissioner; 4)
what were the commissioners previous employment position; and do the
commissioners live in the city.
j. Catina Willis of 9130 Middlebelt came before the board seeking assistance on how to
obtain a final real estate closing meter read.
