PUBLIC HEARING and REGULAR MEETING COMMENTS (2 minutes per speaker)
Vice Chair Blackmon stated that staff was available to address the concerns of the
citizens and those who submitted comments in writing would be addressed in writing.
1. Ronnie Mixon of 10204 Traverse asked why the owner of a property is responsible
for the water bill when they are absent from the home and the property has been
invaded by squatters.
2. Richard Tyson, Bishop of Catheral of Hope Church, asked why the property is being
charged for water when the service was interrupted back in 2014.
3. Janet Ware of 531 S. Eastlawn St. asked how is the impervious rate calculated and
why is it being implemented.
4. Shirlene Burns of 16570 Washburn asked why are constituents unable to navigate
the DWSD website to retrieve information needed.
5. William Davis of 8550 Appoline St. asked what steps has the department taken to
employ more minority workers, and how will the department address the massive
number of leaks in the system.
6. Mary Ellen Howard of 1099 Van Dyke # 304 asked for adoption of a water
affordability plan for low income households.
7. Diane King of 17601 Kentucky asked why Detroiters are paying more for water than
the suburban communities.

massive toxic waste and opposed expansion of US Ecology
9. Pam Pfeiffer of the Peoples Water Board Coalition came to express her opposition
on the expansion of US Ecology.
10. Russ Bellant of 19619 Helen was concern about water running in a home for six
weeks. He stated that the Seven Mile/Van Dyke Association will hold a forum on June
28 at 7 p.m. about drainage fees, and asked for representative from the department to
participant in the discussion.
11. Tarnetta Carter 3494 Hurlbut St. asked why the department allows water to
continue running at abandoned homes.
12. Dorothy Wallingford 3172 E. Lafayette stated that her drainage charge concern
was addressed by the customer care division.
13. Debra A. Wooten of 1378 Village Dr had several concerns: how drainage fees are
calculated; any communication should be rolled out to constituents; and water bill
credits should be refunded.
